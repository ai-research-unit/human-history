# __1,700,000 BCE – 1,400,000 BCE__ — Emergence of Acheulean Technology on the Plateau
__Summary:__ A long-term trend documenting the gradual transition from Oldowan to Acheulean technology across the Southern African Plateau as Homo erectus populations expand and refine bifacial reduction techniques; the window spans approximately three hundred thousand years of incremental elaboration in which handaxes and cleavers progressively supplement and displace simpler core-and-flake assemblages; the binding constraint remains the imitative transmission of increasingly complex motor sequences, which sets the pace of technological diffusion; the system holds ample margin against environmental variability through dietary flexibility and spatial mobility; the direction of change is toward greater technological sophistication, population consolidation, and broader landscape occupation.

__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The interior plateau extends across present-day Gauteng, Free State, and Northern Cape at elevations of 1,000 to 1,500 meters; dolomitic karst in the northeast provides cave sites while the western interior offers open savanna with seasonal pans and river courses; movement is facilitated by gentle topography and abundant water sources during interglacials.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Early Pleistocene conditions oscillate between wetter interglacials supporting woodland expansion and drier glacial phases favoring open grassland; rainfall ranges from 400 to 800 mm annually depending on phase; secular trend is toward increasing amplitude of glacial-interglacial cycles, widening the environmental band populations must track.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Acute hazards remain minimal; the cratonic plateau is seismically stable and volcanically inactive; drought cycles occur on multi-decadal scales but populations buffer through mobility; fire is recurrent but non-catastrophic; margin against environmental shock is wide.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Lithic raw materials remain abundant: quartzite, dolomite, chert, and banded ironstone provide suitable blanks for Acheulean biface production; water availability fluctuates seasonally but springs and pans ensure perennial access at focal points; large mammal biomass supports scavenging and hunting; the resource stock is not approaching depletion.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris accumulates at production sites but the open landscape absorbs refuse without measurable impact; no sink saturation occurs; environmental degradation from hominin activity is negligible relative to landscape scale.<br><br>
