# __250,000 BCE – 200,000 BCE__ — Florisbad and Archaic Homo sapiens
__Summary:__ A static snapshot of the Southern African Plateau centered on the Florisbad spring site in the Free State, where the Florisbad cranium documents archaic Homo sapiens morphology in association with early Middle Stone Age artifacts around 259 ka; the window captures a population exhibiting transitional cranial features between earlier hominins and anatomically modern humans, sustained by spring-fed wetland resources in a savanna-grassland mosaic, with prepared-core lithic technology now standard and all knowledge transmission proceeding through direct observation and gestural demonstration without linguistic encoding.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Elevated interior plateau at 1200-1400 meters altitude; spring-fed pans and seasonal watercourses punctuate semi-arid grassland; the Florisbad spring creates a local wetland oasis drawing fauna and hominins, with movement otherwise constrained by water availability across the open highveld.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Interglacial conditions during MIS 7 yielding moderate rainfall and warm temperatures; seasonal precipitation concentrated in summer months; climate trending toward cooler and more variable conditions as the window progresses toward MIS 6 glacial onset.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Periodic drought as the primary hazard, with spring permanence providing critical buffering; lightning-ignited grassland fires frequent but manageable given open terrain; no seismic or volcanic exposure; absorptive margin adequate for typical seasonal variability.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Silcrete, quartzite, and hornfels available for lithic production within foraging range; spring sites concentrate game and plant resources; fuel wood from scattered trees and shrubs sufficient for fire maintenance; resource base adequate without evident depletion.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris and organic waste dispersed across open landscape with effectively unlimited absorptive capacity; spring-margin middens accumulating but far below any saturation threshold; negligible anthropogenic load on plateau-scale sinks.<br><br>

__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Archaic Homo sapiens reproductive physiology presumed comparable to later humans; pair-bonding patterns inferred from group sizes; no direct evidence for mating systems; biological fertility sufficient for population maintenance.<br><br>
