# __2,000,000 BCE – 1,700,000 BCE__ — The Sterkfontein Oldowan and Early Homo
__Summary:__ A static snapshot of early Homo presence in the Cradle of Humankind during the terminal Pliocene and earliest Pleistocene; Oldowan core-and-flake technology represents the dominant toolkit as Australopithecus-dominated assemblages give way to Homo habilis and early Homo erectus populations; the binding constraint is the lithic reduction skill transmissible only through direct observation and imitation, limiting the pace of technological elaboration; the system holds wide margin against environmental shocks given low population density and abundant resources; direction of change is toward increased Homo presence and gradual displacement of Australopithecus.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Cradle of Humankind occupies dolomitic karst terrain at approximately 1,400 meters elevation on the interior plateau, with limestone caves and sinkholes providing natural shelters and sediment traps; movement across the landscape is relatively unimpeded by the open woodland-savanna mosaic.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Seasonal subtropical climate with summer rainfall averaging 600-700 mm annually; interglacial conditions support mixed woodland and grassland; interannual variability is moderate, with periodic droughts within multi-decadal cycles; secular drift is toward cooler and more arid conditions as Pleistocene glacial cycles intensify.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Exposure to acute hazards is low; seismic and volcanic activity is negligible in this cratonic setting; drought is the primary risk but occurs at frequencies the small, mobile population can absorb through range shifting; fire is natural and recurrent but not catastrophic.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Dolomitic terrain provides abundant high-quality quartz, quartzite, and chert cobbles suitable for Oldowan knapping; perennial springs and seasonal streams supply water; large mammal biomass is high, providing scavenging and occasional hunting opportunities; the raw material and food stock remain effectively unlimited relative to extraction rates.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris, bone refuse, and organic waste are scattered across an open landscape with effectively unlimited absorptive capacity; no measurable accumulation or sink saturation occurs; degradation imprint on the environment is negligible.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Endemic parasitic and bacterial loads are present but not epidemic; predation by large cats, hyenas, and crocodiles imposes significant mortality, particularly on juveniles and isolated individuals; background death rate is high but within replacement capacity.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Nutritional status is adequate, supported by a broad diet of tubers, fruits, small game, and scavenged meat; early Homo shows increased stature and reduced gut size relative to Australopithecus, indicating dietary quality improvement; physiological vitality is stable.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Sexual dimorphism remains pronounced; mating patterns likely involve male competition and female choice within small multi-male groups; pair-bonding is weakly developed; reproductive physiology is stable with no evident selection pressure shifts within the window.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density is very low, perhaps a few individuals per hundred square kilometers; birth rates roughly match death rates, maintaining small, dispersed bands; age structure skews young due to high adult mortality; no significant migration pressure is evident.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ The dependency load from juveniles is substantial given prolonged hominin childhood; aged and infirm dependents are few due to high adult mortality; the productive cohort absorbs this load without evident strain given low overall population.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Subsistence relies on broad-spectrum foraging including plant foods, small vertebrates, insects, and scavenged large mammal carcasses; the population sits well below carrying capacity; household pooling is minimal, with food sharing likely limited to immediate kin and opportunistic contexts.<br><br>
__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Energy capture is exclusively through human muscle; no fire use is attested for this window; efficiency of energy conversion is low, constrained by caloric costs of foraging and the energetic demands of brain growth; per-capita work ceiling is modest but adequate for survival needs.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Division of labor is minimal, likely limited to age and sex differences in foraging tasks; no coercive labor organization exists; cooperation is voluntary and situational, driven by immediate mutual benefit rather than obligation.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Oldowan tool production creates small cutting and pounding implements from cobbles; no surplus accumulation beyond immediate use; production output matches daily consumption needs without stockpiling.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No storage technology exists; reserves are held in body fat and in knowledge of resource locations across the landscape; buffering against shortage relies entirely on mobility and dietary flexibility.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ No evidence of exchange networks beyond immediate group sharing; raw material transport is limited to a few kilometers from source; logistics are constrained by what individuals can carry during daily foraging rounds.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No medium of value storage or deferred exchange exists; reciprocity is immediate and situational; no credit or debt relationships are evident; obligations are transient and based on direct observation of past sharing behavior.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ No coordinating authority extracts or redistributes surplus; any redistribution occurs through voluntary sharing within the immediate group, likely favoring kin and reciprocating partners.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ No accumulated wealth or property concentration exists; positional advantage is limited to social rank within the group; no asset defense strategies are needed or evident.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Stone flakes and cores are discarded at knapping sites; bone and organic refuse scatter at processing locations; all waste is dispersed across the landscape with no routing decisions required; no recycling or sequestration occurs.<br><br>
