# __300,000 BCE – 250,000 BCE__ — Transition to Middle Stone Age Technologies
__Summary:__ A long-term trend across the Southern African Plateau as Acheulean industries give way to Middle Stone Age technologies over fifty millennia. The binding constraint is the cumulative transmission of prepared-core and point-making techniques through purely imitative channels, requiring unbroken chains of skilled demonstrators. Handaxe frequencies decline as Levallois reduction and hafted points become dominant. The system holds moderate margin against environmental oscillation, though demographic continuity is fragile. The principal direction is technological replacement, with cognitive and demographic shifts implied by the elaboration of technique.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Interior plateau remains the stable arena at 1,000–1,500 meters elevation; drainage networks and spring-fed pans structure movement; kopjes and river valleys provide localized shelter; spatial friction low across grassland and open woodland.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Oscillation between wetter interglacial and drier glacial phases; savanna-woodland mosaic shifts toward more open grassland during arid intervals; volatility increasing as glacial-interglacial cycling intensifies; system provisioned for moderate range.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Drought cycles the primary hazard with multi-year episodes possible; wildfire frequency tracks vegetation density; no volcanic or seismic exposure; margin adequate for routine drought but thinning under prolonged aridity.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Cryptocrystalline silicates, quartzite, and hornfels abundant for knapping; water availability variable with climate phase; woody fuel adequate in woodland patches; no resource depletion approaching.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Lithic waste and organic debris dispersed across open landscape; population densities remain low; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited; no saturation trend.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__

__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Extended interbirth intervals continue; cooperative breeding supports juvenile survival; pair-bonding reinforced by provisioning interdependence; selective pressure favoring technical skill may intensify.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density low but possibly rising slightly as hunting efficiency improves; replacement ratios marginally positive; age structure skewed toward reproductive adults; migration across the plateau ongoing.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Juvenile mortality remains high; aged dependency rare; injured individuals face rapid attrition; dependency load stable and within band absorptive capacity.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__

__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle dominant; fire use controlled and routine for cooking and possibly hafting adhesives; no change in conversion efficiency but application expanding.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Age and sex differentiation in tasks; labor mobilized through kinship and direct demonstration; coercion absent; cooperation achieved through reciprocity.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Production remains immediate; prepared-core reduction yields more usable edge per stone mass, improving efficiency; surplus accumulation still limited to portable toolkit.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No durable storage; body fat and cached tools provide minimal buffer; mobility the primary shock-absorber; buffering capacity unchanged.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Raw material transported tens of kilometers; no formalized exchange; logistics constrained to what groups carry; transport costs high relative to material value.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No durable value tokens; deferred reciprocity embedded in kin and band relations; credit remains implicit and interpersonal.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ Large-kill sharing constitutes redistribution; no extractive authority; allocation by kinship and proximity; trending stable.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Material differentiation negligible; tools held personally but not defended as exclusive property; no concentration trend.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Knapping debris and bone refuse dispersed at occupation sites; volume trivial; no routing problem; load negligible.<br><br>
