# __1,000,000 BCE – 700,000 BCE__ — Acheulean Stability Across the Interior Plateau
__Summary:__ The Acheulean technocomplex achieves full distribution across the Southern African interior plateau during this window, with Homo erectus populations occupying both open-air sites and caves from the Northern Cape to the Cradle of Humankind. The binding constraint is the low population density that limits both inter-group contact and cumulative innovation. Margin against climatic and resource shock is substantial given the mobility of small bands across a well-known landscape. The system exhibits marked technological conservatism and demographic stasis, with no directional change in any major variable.

__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The interior plateau extends at 1000-1500m elevation across the present-day Free State, Northern Cape, and Gauteng; rolling grassland and savanna-woodland offer low transit friction, with seasonal rivers, springs, and pans serving as focal points constraining movement to water-proximate corridors.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Glacial-interglacial cycling continues to modulate rainfall patterns across multi-millennial intervals; interglacials bring wetter conditions supporting denser woodland, while glacials expand open grassland; the plateau's elevation moderates temperature extremes relative to lower-lying regions.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Drought represents the primary environmental hazard, periodically restricting water availability and concentrating fauna at remaining sources; no significant seismic, volcanic, or flood exposure affects the plateau interior; margin against drought is held through mobility.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Quartzite, dolerite, and other knappable stone are widely available at outcrops and in river gravels; the plateau supports abundant grazing fauna and diverse plant resources; energy endowment is ample relative to minimal human demands.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Vast open landscape absorbs negligible human waste production without measurable impact; no sink approaches saturation; absorptive capacity is effectively unlimited for the population scale.<br><br>
