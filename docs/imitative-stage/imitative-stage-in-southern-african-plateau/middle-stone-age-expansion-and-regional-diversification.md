# __170,000 BCE – 150,000 BCE__ — Middle Stone Age Expansion and Regional Diversification

__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Interglacial conditions prevail with moderate rainfall supporting savanna-woodland mosaics; seasonal variability is moderate; climate remains broadly stable within the window, neither significantly wetting nor drying.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Drought and wildfire constitute the primary hazards; seismic and volcanic exposure is negligible; low population density and mobile settlement patterns leave ample margin against environmental shocks.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Fine-grained silcrete and quartzite are abundant across the plateau; springs and seasonal pans provide reliable water; game biomass is high; the resource base far exceeds extraction pressure.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris and organic discard remain trivial relative to landscape scale; absorptive capacity is effectively unlimited; no detectable environmental degradation accrues.<br><br>
