# __400,000 BCE – 300,000 BCE__ — The Fauresmith Transitional Industry
__Summary:__ A static snapshot of the Southern African Plateau during the Fauresmith industry, when prepared-core flaking techniques emerged within an ongoing Acheulean tradition. The binding constraint is the cognitive and motor-skill threshold for mastering proto-Levallois reduction without linguistic instruction; transmission depends entirely on prolonged observation and guided imitation. The system holds moderate margin against climatic oscillation given flexible savanna-woodland resource mosaics, though demographic density remains low. The principal direction is technological elaboration toward volumetric core control while handaxe production persists.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Elevated interior plateau at 1,000–1,500 meters; low relief with scattered kopjes and incised drainage; movement relatively unconstrained across open terrain; spring-fed pans serve as focal nodes.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Interglacial phase with moderate summer rainfall; savanna-woodland mosaic with grassy openings; interannual variability present but within tolerable range; slow oscillation toward drier glacial conditions beginning.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seasonal drought the primary acute stress; wildfire cycles shape vegetation structure; seismic and volcanic hazards negligible; system holds adequate margin against routine shocks.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Abundant surface cryptocrystalline silicates and quartzite cobbles; water availability tied to spring systems and seasonal pans; woody fuel widely distributed; no approaching depletion.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris and organic residue dispersed across open landscape; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited at prevailing population densities; no saturation.<br><br>

__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Orphaned juveniles absorbed by group; few individuals survive to aged dependency; chronic injury carries high mortality; dependency load remains within band absorptive capacity.<br><br>
