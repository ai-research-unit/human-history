# __1,200,000 BCE – 900,000 BCE__ — Initial Hominin Occupation of the Iberian Peninsula
__Summary:__ Earliest secure hominin presence on the Iberian Peninsula, attested at Sima del Elefante and the contested Orce basin sites; small, transient populations exploiting interglacial windows with Mode 1 core-and-flake technology; species attribution uncertain; marginal foothold at the western edge of the inhabited world with no evidence of sustained occupation.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Iberian Peninsula forms a distinct landmass separated from continental Europe by the Pyrenees barrier and from Africa by the narrow Strait of Gibraltar, then a fluctuating marine crossing; interior plateaus, coastal plains, and incised river valleys structure movement corridors.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Early Pleistocene climate oscillates on a 41-kiloyear cycle, with interglacial phases opening windows for northward dispersal and glacial phases depressing temperatures and contracting habitable range; Mediterranean and Atlantic coastal zones retain milder conditions than interior plateaus.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seismic activity along the Betic Cordillera and occasional volcanic events in the southeast; flooding along incised river systems; drought during glacial phases on interior plateaus; exposure high but population density negligible, limiting aggregate impact.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Lithic raw materials available along river terraces and eroding limestone outcrops; lacustrine and riverine environments support large herbivore populations; forested refugia during interglacials provide plant resources; resource distribution patchy but adequate for minimal population.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Negligible hominin population generates trivial organic and lithic waste; landscape absorptive capacity effectively unlimited against such loads.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Endemic parasites and zoonotic exposure from scavenging large carcasses; baseline mortality high from trauma, predation, and environmental exposure; no evidence of epidemic disease at such low densities.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Nutritional status dependent on opportunistic scavenging and plant collection; skeletal evidence too fragmentary for direct assessment; robust postcranial morphology inferred from contemporaneous African populations suggests adequate caloric intake during favorable periods.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Mating and pairing structures unattested; reproductive success constrained by high juvenile mortality and female nutritional stress during lactation; small group sizes imply limited mate choice.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density extremely low, likely transient pulses rather than continuous occupation; replacement barely sufficient to maintain presence during interglacial windows; regional population likely numbered in low hundreds at most.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ High juvenile and young-adult mortality limits accumulation of dependent aged; dependency load minimal but so is capacity to absorb any surplus burden.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Foraging economy based on plant collection, small-animal capture, and scavenging from large-herbivore carcasses; provisioning floor precarious, subject to seasonal and climatic fluctuation.<br><br>
__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle is the sole power source; no evidence of controlled fire at these sites; energy budget limited to direct metabolic output, constraining mobility radius and processing capacity.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Small-group foraging with minimal division of labor beyond age and sex; no coercion structures; cooperative acquisition and immediate sharing within the band.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ No surplus beyond immediate consumption; Mode 1 lithic production is expedient and site-specific, generating simple cores and flakes for immediate use.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No storage technology; reserves held only as body fat; buffering capacity negligible, forcing immediate response to resource fluctuation.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ No evidence of inter-group exchange; raw-material sourcing local to site catchments; logistics limited to daily foraging radius.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No durable value storage; reciprocity operates within the band on immediate timescales; no deferred exchange beyond direct sharing obligations.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ No coordinating authority extracts surplus; communal sharing of large kills or finds operates within the foraging band without hierarchical allocation.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ No accumulation of property; all members operate at equivalent material level; no defensive strategies for holdings.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Lithic debitage and bone refuse left at processing sites; organic waste dispersed by scavengers; no concentration sufficient to saturate local sinks.<br><br>
## __P04. Social Environment__
__Social Trust & Mutual Aid:__ Trust radius confined to the immediate band; mutual aid expressed through food sharing and cooperative vigilance; no evidence of wider networks.<br><br>
__Stratification, Status & Group Identity:__ No evidence of stratification; status distinctions, if any, based on age, sex, or demonstrated competence; group identity defined by co-residence and kinship.<br><br>
__Kinship, Family & Inheritance:__ Kinship structures unattested but likely bilateral or matrilineal based on primate analogues; no material inheritance; social bonds transmitted through proximity and imitation.<br><br>
__Civic Association & Self-Organization:__ The foraging band is the sole associational unit; no nested structures; coordination emerges from immediate face-to-face interaction.<br><br>
__Leisure, Play & Sociability:__ Non-work time absorbed by rest, grooming, and juvenile play; sociability expressed through physical proximity and shared activity; no recovered evidence of formal play structures.<br><br>
__Grievance Channels & Social Discharge:__ Intra-group conflict resolved through dominance displays, avoidance, or group fission; no formal mediation; unresolved grievance may drive dispersal.<br><br>
## __P05. Political Environment__
__Power Structure & Authority:__ No institutionalized authority; situational leadership based on competence and physical dominance; power diffuse and contested within small groups.<br><br>
__Succession & Power Transfer:__ No formal succession; leadership shifts with circumstances and physical condition; no evidence of hereditary or ritual transfer.<br><br>
__Legitimacy & Political Narrative:__ No attested legitimizing narrative; authority rests on demonstrated capacity and social acquiescence; compliance maintained through direct interaction.<br><br>
__Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity:__ No administrative apparatus; coordination limited to immediate vocal and gestural signaling within the band; no record-keeping or formal allocation.<br><br>
__Political Participation & Mobilization:__ All adult members participate in group decisions through presence and action; no collective mobilization beyond the band; dissent expressed by withdrawal or fission.<br><br>
__Geopolitical Position & External Relations:__ No inter-group polity; external relations limited to occasional contact with neighboring bands; no alliances, treaties, or territorial defense.<br><br>
__Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force:__ No organized coercion; violence personal and situational; lithic tools double as weapons; force capacity distributed among adult males.<br><br>
__Internal Order & Threat Environment:__ Internal order maintained by social pressure and physical dominance; external threat from large carnivores significant; banditry and predation by other hominins unattested but possible.<br><br>
## __P06. Informational Environment__
__Channels & Transmission Velocity:__ Transmission entirely through observation and imitation; vocal communication limited to non-linguistic calls and gestures; information travels only as fast as individuals move and only to those physically present.<br><br>
__Record, Storage & Persistence:__ Information stored solely in living memory; no external recording media; knowledge persists only through continuous demonstration and imitation across generations; loss of skilled individuals erases their repertoire.<br><br>
__Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms:__ No symbolic discourse to frame or censor; communicative norms governed by attention, proximity, and social hierarchy; no evidence of taboo or permitted speech.<br><br>
__Reception, Attention & Amplification:__ Attention governed by salience of demonstrated action; amplification limited to repeated demonstration; no mechanism for broadcast beyond the immediate audience.<br><br>
__Signal Integrity & Noise Load:__ Signal fidelity depends on clarity of demonstration and observer attentiveness; copying errors accumulate without verbal correction; no verification mechanism beyond repeated observation.<br><br>

__Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution:__ No formal law or codified taboo; behavioral regulation operates through immediate social consequence; no recoverable evolution of rule systems.<br><br>

__Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden:__ No recognized rights regime; protection derives from group membership and physical capacity; compliance burden limited to social conformity within the band.<br><br>
__Impunity & Unredressed Wrong:__ Wrongs unredressed where aggressor dominates or victim lacks allies; impunity coextensive with power; no accumulated grievance beyond individual memory.<br><br>
## __P08. Intellectual Environment__

__Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution:__ Knowledge stock limited to lithic production, foraging routes, and carcass processing; skill transmitted through observation and guided practice; distribution universal within the band for basic competencies.<br><br>
__Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay:__ Knowledge lost with death of skilled practitioners; no external storage to buffer attrition; technique stable but vulnerable to transmission breaks during population bottlenecks.<br><br>
