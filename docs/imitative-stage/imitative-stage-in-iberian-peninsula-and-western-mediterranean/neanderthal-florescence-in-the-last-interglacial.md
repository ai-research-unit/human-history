# __130,000 BCE – 80,000 BCE__ — Neanderthal Florescence in the Last Interglacial
__Summary:__ Peak Neanderthal population density across Iberia during Eemian interglacial warmth, with site proliferation from Gibraltar to Catalonia; Mousterian prepared-core technology standardized; dietary breadth expanding to include marine shellfish and small game; ochre procurement at coastal sites attests to non-utilitarian material use; population well within carrying capacity; system in demographic and technological equilibrium.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Iberian topography unchanged — Cantabrian coast, Ebro and Guadalquivir valleys, interior Meseta, Mediterranean littoral — offering diverse microhabitats and transit corridors between coastal and upland zones with moderate friction.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Eemian interglacial conditions bring temperatures 1–2 degrees above present, reduced seasonality, expanded forests, and elevated sea levels flooding coastal shelves; climate stable within window, trending toward cooler conditions only near its close.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Acute hazards minimal; seismic activity episodic along Betic-Rif zone; volcanic quiescence; no evidence of catastrophic events during window; ample margin against environmental shocks.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Diverse lithic raw materials — high-quality flint in Cantabria and Catalonia, quartzite on Meseta — abundant; marine and terrestrial fauna concentrated; standing stock far exceeds extraction rate.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris and organic discard scattered across open landscapes and cave mouths; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited relative to negligible anthropogenic load.<br><br>
