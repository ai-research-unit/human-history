# __350,000 BCE – 200,000 BCE__ — Emergence of Prepared-Core Technology and Fire Management
__Summary:__ Across one hundred fifty millennia, early Neanderthal populations in Iberia transition from Acheulean bifacial reduction to Levallois prepared-core methods while systematic fire use becomes a consistent feature of the behavioral repertoire; the binding constraint is the cognitive and transmission overhead of mastering predetermined flake production, which required sustained intergenerational teaching through demonstration alone; the trend reflects behavioral modernity precursors without symbolic mediation, and the system holds moderate margin through dietary flexibility and climatic refugia.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Iberian Peninsula retains its corridor function between Africa and continental Europe; interior plateaus (Meseta), coastal strips, and montane zones create habitat mosaics; karstic cave systems along the Mediterranean and Cantabrian coasts provide shelter; river valleys serve as primary transit routes, while mountain passes impose seasonal friction.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ The window spans multiple glacial-interglacial oscillations (MIS 10–7); interglacial phases bring temperate forest expansion while glacial maxima push steppe-tundra south; the peninsula's southern latitude moderates extremes relative to northern Europe; volatility is high on multi-millennial timescales but predictable within generational experience.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seismic activity is present but low-intensity; volcanic hazard is negligible; wildfire risk increases during dry interglacial summers; cave collapse and flooding pose localized hazards; overall exposure is moderate and manageable through site selection.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Flint, quartzite, and quartz sources are distributed unevenly; high-quality flint occurs in specific limestone formations requiring logistical planning; fauna include horse, deer, aurochs, and rhinoceros; plant foods are seasonally abundant in coastal and riverine zones; fuel wood is adequate in forested phases.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Landscape absorptive capacity remains effectively unlimited; organic waste decomposes rapidly; lithic debris accumulates at occupation sites but at volumes trivial relative to available dispersal space.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Pathogen exposure is low relative to later sedentary populations; mortality risk derives primarily from trauma, predation, and nutritional stress; baseline death rates are elevated by modern standards but stable within the window; no epidemic disease is attested.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Neanderthal skeletal robusticity indicates high caloric throughput and demanding physical activity; dental wear and isotopic signatures show heavy meat reliance; episodic nutritional stress is marked by enamel defects but does not indicate chronic shortage; physiological vitality appears adequate.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Sexual dimorphism remains pronounced; reproductive biology followed hominin baselines with extended juvenile dependency; pairing dynamics are unrecoverable but small group sizes imply limited mate choice and some degree of regional endogamy.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density is low across the peninsula; replacement rates hover near equilibrium with high infant and juvenile mortality offset by adequate fertility; age structure skews young; net demographic change across the window is near stasis with possible slight increase during favorable interglacials.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Dependency loads include juveniles and injured adults; the small group size limits capacity to support non-productive individuals but evidence of healed trauma indicates some tolerance; aged individuals are rare in the skeletal record, suggesting attrition before senescence.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Subsistence combines large-game hunting with opportunistic small-game capture and plant gathering; coastal populations exploit marine mollusks and occasional marine mammals; the provisioning floor rises during interglacials and contracts during glacial maxima; dietary breadth provides buffering.<br><br>
__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Fire becomes a consistent feature during this window, attested at sites like Bolomor Cave; controlled combustion extends edible food range, provides warmth, and enables occupation of cooler microhabitats; energy conversion efficiency increases modestly through cooking and heat management.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Labor division follows age and sex; hunting parties coordinate through learned routines transmitted by demonstration; no coercive extraction exists; cooperation is based on kinship reciprocity and immediate mutual benefit.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Production consists of lithic tool manufacture, fire maintenance, and carcass processing; Levallois reduction increases flake yield efficiency from prepared cores; surplus above immediate consumption remains negligible; tools are made as needed rather than stockpiled.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No food storage technology exists; body fat and immediate sharing constitute the sole buffer; fire provides some capacity to preserve meat through drying or smoking, though evidence is indirect; reserves remain minimal.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Raw material transport distances increase during the window, reaching up to one hundred kilometers for high-quality flint; this implies logistical planning and landscape knowledge but not inter-group exchange; trade in the formal sense is absent.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No durable value storage exists; reciprocal obligation within kin groups constitutes the only deferred exchange mechanism; credit and debt are managed through memory and social expectation.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ No extractive authority exists; redistribution occurs through immediate sharing at kill sites; portions are allocated by social convention rather than centralized decision.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Material wealth is minimal and portable; differential access to resources is unattested; asset defense is not a structural concern.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Knapping debris, ash, and butchery refuse accumulate at occupation sites; volumes are small and readily absorbed by the surrounding environment; no waste management is required.<br><br>

__Stratification, Status & Group Identity:__ Stratification is minimal; status likely tracks demonstrated skill, age, and physical capacity without material expression; group identity is defined by co-residence, kinship, and shared behavioral repertoire including the distinctive Levallois reduction sequence.<br><br>
__Kinship, Family & Inheritance:__ Kinship structures are unrecoverable; genetic evidence from later Neanderthal populations suggests patrilocality and female dispersal in some regions; inheritance of material goods is not relevant; inheritance of skill occurs through observation and practice.<br><br>
__Civic Association & Self-Organization:__ The band is the sole unit of association; self-organization occurs through daily interaction and shared activity; no intermediate institutions exist.<br><br>
__Leisure, Play & Sociability:__ Non-work time includes rest, grooming, and presence around hearths; fire extends usable evening hours and creates a focal point for sociability; juvenile play is expected but unattested materially.<br><br>
__Grievance Channels & Social Discharge:__ Grievances are managed through direct interaction, avoidance, or group fission; no formal channels exist; small group size limits accumulation of unresolved conflict.<br><br>

## __P08. Intellectual Environment__
__Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm:__ Systematic inquiry does not exist as a distinct activity; problem-solving occurs through trial, observation, and imitation; no theoretical abstraction is attested; the reigning paradigm is practical know-how embedded in motor routines.<br><br>
__Applied Knowledge & Technical Method:__ The window's defining intellectual achievement is the Levallois prepared-core method: a reduction sequence requiring mental representation of the intended flake before removal, executed through a standardized preparation routine; this represents a significant cognitive advance over earlier approaches and constitutes the discovery frontier of the period.<br><br>
__Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution:__ Education occurs through apprenticeship-style observation; the complexity of Levallois reduction implies extended learning periods; skill distribution tracks individual aptitude and access to competent practitioners; the knowledge stock expands with the addition of fire management techniques.<br><br>
__Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay:__ Earlier Acheulean techniques gradually recede as Levallois methods prove more efficient; knowledge decay occurs when skilled individuals die without transmission; the long persistence of Levallois indicates robust transmission fidelity despite the absence of linguistic instruction.<br><br>
