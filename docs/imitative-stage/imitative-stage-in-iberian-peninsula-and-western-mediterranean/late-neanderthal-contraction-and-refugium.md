# __70,000 BCE – 42,000 BCE__ — Late Neanderthal Contraction and Refugium
__Summary:__ Neanderthal populations progressively contract under cooling climate and demographic fragmentation, with Iberia — particularly Gibraltar and the southern coast — emerging as final refugium; genetic diversity declining through drift and inbreeding in isolated groups; Mousterian technology persists without innovation; population approaching extinction threshold by window's close; system under mounting stress with diminishing margin against shocks.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Iberian topography unchanged; southern coastal zones and Gibraltar provide sheltered microhabitats; Pyrenean barrier increasingly significant as northern populations contract; interior Meseta marginal during cold phases.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Early MIS 4 glaciation (70–60 ka) followed by MIS 3 interstadials with high-frequency oscillation; overall cooling trend with expanding ice sheets to north; volatility elevated; conditions trending toward Last Glacial Maximum.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Acute hazards unchanged; climate volatility represents chronic stress rather than discrete hazard; coastal populations buffered by maritime moderation; margin against cold spikes narrowing.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Lithic raw materials remain accessible; faunal communities shift with climate — red deer and ibex increase, larger fauna diminish; marine resources stable at coastal sites; endowment adequate but patchier.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Anthropogenic load declining with population contraction; absorptive capacity remains effectively unlimited; no degradation pressure.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Pathogen load low in isolated populations; mortality increasingly driven by resource stress and reproductive failure rather than disease; no epidemic signature.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Nutritional stress increasing as evidenced by dental enamel hypoplasia at some late sites; body robusticity maintained but population vitality declining; isotopic signatures show continued high-protein diet but narrowing prey base.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Small, isolated populations face mounting inbreeding depression; mate availability constrained by group fragmentation; reproductive success declining; ancient DNA evidence from later studies indicates reduced genetic diversity.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density declining across window; birth rates likely depressed by nutritional stress and inbreeding; death rates rising; age structure skewing toward adults as juvenile recruitment fails; replacement ratio falling below unity.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Dependency load increasing relative to productive population; care of incapacitated still evidenced but straining smaller groups; demographic overhang accumulating as system contracts.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Subsistence increasingly marginal; large-game hunting less reliable as fauna shifts; reliance on small game and marine resources at coastal sites; provisioning floor closer to carrying-capacity edge.<br><br>
__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Fire use continues as essential technology for warmth, cooking, and tool production; energy budget tightening as prey returns diminish; conversion efficiency unchanged but input declining.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Labor organization unchanged; smaller groups may require broader individual skill sets; no evidence of changed coercion dynamics.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Tool production continues at reduced scale; no surplus accumulation; production just meeting replacement needs; capacity buffer eroding.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No storage innovation; buffering capacity declining as population mobility constrained by fragmentation; body fat reserves more critical; system more vulnerable to failed hunts.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Raw-material transport distances possibly increasing as populations thin; no evidence of inter-group exchange intensification; logistics constrained by reduced group size.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No change from earlier pattern; immediate reciprocity within diminishing groups; no deferred exchange innovation.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ Sharing continues within bands but less to share; redistribution immediate and necessity-driven.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Portable toolkit remains sole wealth; no concentration; defense unnecessary.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Waste load declining with population; routing unchanged; no accumulation pressure.<br><br>
## __P04. Social Environment__
__Social Trust & Mutual Aid:__ Intra-band trust remains high; mutual aid critical for survival in stressed conditions; evidence of care for disabled individuals persists at late sites like El Sidron; radius of trust unchanged but fewer individuals to trust.<br><br>
__Stratification, Status & Group Identity:__ No stratification change; status systems unchanged; group identity increasingly localized as inter-band contact diminishes; isolation reinforcing particularism.<br><br>
__Kinship, Family & Inheritance:__ Kinship increasingly constrained by small population; consanguineous mating more common; inheritance limited to diminishing toolkit; family structure stressed by demographic contraction.<br><br>
__Civic Association & Self-Organization:__ Band remains sole unit; coordination unchanged in form but challenged by smaller group sizes; no supra-band organization emerging under stress.<br><br>
__Leisure, Play & Sociability:__ Leisure time possibly reduced by subsistence stress; sociability constrained by fewer individuals; hearth-centered interaction continues.<br><br>
__Grievance Channels & Social Discharge:__ Fission less viable as option given low population density; grievances must be absorbed within group; social friction potentially rising but sparsely attested.<br><br>
