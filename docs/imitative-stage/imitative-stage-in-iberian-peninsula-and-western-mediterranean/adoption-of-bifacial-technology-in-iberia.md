# __600,000 BCE – 400,000 BCE__ — Adoption of Bifacial Technology in Iberia
__Summary:__ Across two hundred millennia, Iberian hominin populations gradually replace Mode 1 core-and-flake industries with Mode 2 bifacial handaxes, completing a transition that occurred earlier in Africa and the Levant. The shift is asynchronous, appearing first in the northeast and spreading unevenly across the peninsula. The binding constraint is the slow diffusion of technique through imitative transmission across small, scattered populations. Margin is wide; the trend unfolds without crisis.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Iberian Peninsula presents a compartmentalised landscape of mountain chains, interior plateaux, and coastal corridors; the Pyrenees restrict northern access while river valleys channel movement; spatial friction slows the diffusion of populations and their techniques across the region.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Glacial-interglacial cyclicity on approximately 100,000-year periods alternates cold-arid and warm-humid phases; the overall trend across this window includes both glacial maxima and interglacial ameliorations; volatility is moderate but predictable at millennial scales.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seismic risk is low; drought and wildfire affect interior zones episodically; flood hazard is localised along river systems; exposure is manageable and populations retain ample margin against acute shocks.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Siliceous raw materials, quartzite, and flint occur unevenly; the northeast has better flint sources while the interior relies on quartzite; faunal biomass fluctuates with climate but remains adequate for foraging; endowment supports dispersed hominin bands without depletion.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Lithic debitage and organic refuse accumulate slowly at occupation sites; the landscape absorbs all outputs without saturation; sink capacity remains effectively unlimited throughout the window.<br><br>
