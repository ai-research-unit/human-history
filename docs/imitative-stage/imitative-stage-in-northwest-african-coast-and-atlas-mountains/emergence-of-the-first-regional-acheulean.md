# __1300000 BCE – 1000000 BCE__ — Emergence of the First Regional Acheulean
__Summary:__ Long-term trend documenting the transition from Oldowan to Acheulean technocomplex along the Casablanca littoral of Atlantic Morocco, centered on the Thomas Quarry I Unit L assemblage dated to ~1.3 Ma. The window tracks the appearance of bifacially worked picks, trihedrons, handaxes, and cleavers — diagnostic Acheulean forms requiring imposed symmetry and hierarchically structured reduction sequences absent in the preceding Oldowan. Over 3,800 artifacts in Unit L1 alone make this one of the largest early Acheulean assemblages in Africa. Binding constraint remains the imitative transmission ceiling: technique passes hand-to-hand without verbal instruction, capping the complexity and standardization achievable. Direction of change is toward greater formal elaboration, but progress is slow, measured in tens of thousands of years per incremental refinement.
