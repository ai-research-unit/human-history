# __500000 BCE – 300000 BCE__ — Humid-Arid Oscillation Regime
__Summary:__ A 200,000-year snapshot of the dynamic equilibrium governing Northwest African environments: precession-driven (~23,000-year) monsoon cycles produce recurring North African Humid Periods interspersed with arid phases; during humid windows the Sahara greens and hominin dispersal corridors open; during arid phases desert barriers reform and populations contract to refugia; the binding constraint is the insolation threshold that triggers rapid state-switches between green and desert conditions; the margin lies in the coastal and montane refugia that buffer populations through arid phases; direction of change is oscillatory rather than linear.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Sahara functions as a dynamic barrier — expanding during arid phases to block north-south transit, contracting during humid phases to expose lake basins, reactivated wadis, and grassland corridors; Atlas Mountains and Atlantic coast remain accessible throughout.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Precession (~23,000-year) drives primary oscillation; obliquity (~41,000-year) and eccentricity (~100,000-year) modulate amplitude; glacial maxima suppress humid periods; volatility is the regime itself — threshold behavior produces rapid transitions once insolation crosses critical values.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Aridification is the primary hazard; multi-millennial dry phases eliminate interior water sources and collapse grassland productivity; coastal and Atlas refugia provide buffer but interior exposure is extreme during desert maxima.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ During humid phases: extensive lakes (paleo-Chad, paleo-Saharan basins), permanent rivers, grassland game, fish, and plant resources; during arid phases: endowment contracts to perennial springs, coastal shellfish, and montane water sources.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Negligible anthropogenic load; dust flux increases dramatically during arid phases (recorded in ocean sediment cores) as Saharan deflation intensifies; sink capacity unlimited for minimal hominin discharge.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Humid phases may increase waterborne pathogen exposure near lakes and marshes; arid phases reduce disease but increase physiological stress from heat and water scarcity; net mortality pressure oscillates with climate.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Nutritional status improves during Green Sahara phases when game, fish, and plant foods proliferate; declines during arid phases as populations concentrate at refugia and resource competition intensifies.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ No direct evidence; reproductive success likely tracks resource availability, expanding during humid phases and contracting during arid stress; pair-bonding and cooperative breeding patterns inferred stable.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population expands and disperses during humid phases, occupying interior basins; contracts and concentrates during arid phases; regional population oscillates but lineages persist through refugia; metapopulation dynamics govern renewal.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Arid phases likely increase juvenile mortality and reduce support for dependents as resources contract; humid phases relax these pressures; dependency load tracks carrying capacity oscillation.<br><br>
