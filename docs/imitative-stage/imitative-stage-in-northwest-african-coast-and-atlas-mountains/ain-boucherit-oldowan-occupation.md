# __1900000 BCE – 1700000 BCE__ — Ain Boucherit Oldowan Occupation
__Summary:__ Static snapshot of the earliest attested hominin presence in North Africa, centered on the Ain Boucherit and Ain Hanech sites in northeastern Algeria. Oldowan lithic industry — choppers, polyhedra, subspheroids, sharp-edged flakes — persists in dynamic equilibrium with no transition toward bifacial forms. Cutmarked bones attest carcass processing; no hominin remains recovered. Binding constraint is the cognitive ceiling of pre-linguistic imitative transmission, which limits technique to what can be shown and copied hand-to-hand. Shock-buffer margin rests on group mobility across a semi-arid grassland-savanna mosaic during a precession-driven humid phase; direction of change is effectively null — the system reproduces itself without detectable drift across the window.
