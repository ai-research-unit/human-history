# __600000 BCE – 400000 BCE__ — Second Regional Acheulean Refinement
__Summary:__ A 200,000-year trend across the Atlantic Moroccan littoral during which Acheulean technology advances from irregular handaxes toward symmetrical, standardized forms; Homo cf. rhodesiensis populations occupy cave sites at Thomas Quarry I and Sidi Abderrahman; the binding constraint is cognitive and motor-skill transmission through imitation alone, gating the pace of refinement; climate oscillation supplies intermittent humid corridors but does not yet disrupt regional continuity; the margin lies in the predictability of coastal resources and the accumulated skill embedded in knapping tradition.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Atlantic Moroccan coast and Casablanca platform provide consolidated quartzite and flint outcrops; coastal caves and karstic cavities offer shelter; east-west transit blocked by Atlas foothills; north-south mobility constrained by desert expansion during arid phases.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Precession-driven humid-arid oscillation (~23,000-year period) governs rainfall; interglacial phases bring open woodland-savanna; glacial maxima suppress monsoon penetration; volatility high but averaged over millennia the coastal belt remains habitable.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Low seismic and volcanic exposure; primary hazard is multi-millennial aridification pulses reducing interior water sources; coastal margin buffers these shocks through persistent groundwater and littoral resources.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ High-quality quartzite and flint for tool manufacture available in shore platforms and quarry exposures; perennial springs; coastal shellfish beds and savanna megafauna during humid phases.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Negligible; small hominin populations produce minimal waste; organic refuse recycled by scavengers; lithic debitage accumulates but imposes no environmental load.<br><br>
