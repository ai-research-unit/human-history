# __130,000 BCE – 90,000 BCE__ — The Skhul-Qafzeh Population and Early Modern Human Burials
__Summary:__ Anatomically modern humans occupy the Levantine coast and interior during the Last Interglacial, leaving multiple intentional burials at Skhul and Qafzeh caves with associated Mousterian tools and occasional grave offerings. The population exploits warm, wet conditions favoring Mediterranean woodland, but this expansion proves transient, with later withdrawal suggesting climatic or competitive limits. The binding constraint is environmental carrying capacity tied to interglacial moisture; margin against climatic reversal is narrow. Direction of change: stable occupation during the warm phase, followed by demographic contraction.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Mount Carmel ridges and Nazareth highlands provide limestone caves suitable for habitation; coastal plains and interior valleys offer foraging corridors with moderate transit friction across varied terrain.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Last Interglacial warmth brings elevated rainfall and expanded Mediterranean woodland; seasonal contrast remains moderate, and interannual variability is low relative to glacial phases.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seismic activity along the Jordan Rift poses intermittent risk; no acute hazard dominates the record, and cave sites buffer against storm and flood exposure.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Flint nodules abundant in Eocene chalk formations; fallow deer, aurochs, and gazelle provide protein; edible plants and freshwater springs support foraging bands without evident depletion.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Minimal organic and lithic waste disperses readily in woodland and cave sediments; absorptive capacity vastly exceeds load, with negligible landscape degradation.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Endemic parasite and infection burdens inferred from skeletal pathology are moderate; mortality baseline is typical for Middle Paleolithic foragers without epidemic peaks in the burial sample.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Robust skeletal builds and adequate stature at Skhul and Qafzeh indicate sufficient caloric intake; dental wear suggests mixed diet without severe nutritional stress.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Mating patterns unrecoverable; age and sex distribution in burials implies normal reproductive cohort presence within small, mobile bands.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density remains low; burial accumulation over millennia suggests small, dispersed groups with slow demographic turnover and replacement near equilibrium.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Juvenile and adult burials indicate dependent young are present; no evidence of overwhelming dependency load, though burial of children implies some loss before maturity.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Hunting of medium-bodied ungulates and gathering of plant foods provide subsistence baseline; interglacial productivity keeps bands above starvation margin with seasonal mobility.<br><br>
__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle and controlled fire convert raw biomass into usable energy; fire documented at both sites for warmth, cooking, and possibly tool-working, maintaining per-capita work capacity.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Cooperative foraging without recoverable division beyond age and sex; coercion absent in egalitarian band structure inferred from burial uniformity.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ No surplus accumulation beyond immediate consumption; production ceiling is set by daily foraging yield, with no storage or delayed-return systems attested.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ Perishable foods not stored; lithic caches possibly present but minimal; buffering relies on mobility and environmental abundance rather than accumulated reserves.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Local raw materials dominate assemblages; distant shell ornaments at Qafzeh imply some exchange or long-distance movement, but no regularized trade network.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No durable value medium; reciprocity obligations within and between bands constitute all deferred exchange, unrecoverable beyond inference from burial goods.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ No extractive authority; communal sharing of kills and gathered foods is the sole redistributive mechanism, load negligible.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Material differentiation minimal; grave goods (ochre, shell, boar mandible) suggest personal adornment rather than accumulated wealth; asset defense irrelevant at this scale.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Knapping debris and faunal refuse accumulate in occupation zones; disposal is local and unmanaged, routing waste into cave sediments without strain on sink capacity.<br><br>
