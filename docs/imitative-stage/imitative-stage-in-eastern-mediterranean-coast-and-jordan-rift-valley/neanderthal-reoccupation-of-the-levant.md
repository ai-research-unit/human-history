# __90,000 BCE – 55,000 BCE__ — Neanderthal Reoccupation of the Levant
__Summary:__ Neanderthal populations reoccupy the Levantine corridor as climatic cooling during MIS 4 and early MIS 3 shifts environmental conditions, with key sites at Amud, Kebara, and upper Tabun preserving skeletal remains and continuous Mousterian industry. The earlier anatomically modern human presence has withdrawn, leaving the region to Neanderthals who maintain technological continuity with preceding Mousterian traditions. The binding constraint is climatic regime favoring cold-adapted hominins over warm-adapted moderns; margin against warming reversal is moderate. Direction of change: stable Neanderthal presence through the window, with demographic persistence until later competitive or climatic pressures.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Mount Carmel and Galilee highlands provide cave shelters at Amud, Kebara, and Tabun; the Jordan Rift corridor permits north-south movement, while coastal plains and interior valleys offer foraging range with moderate friction.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ MIS 4 cooling brings drier, more continental conditions with expanded steppe vegetation; seasonal temperature swings increase, and interannual variability rises compared to the preceding interglacial.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seismic risk persists along rift faults; cold-season exposure is buffered by cave habitation; drought hazard increases with aridification but remains within adaptive range.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Flint remains abundant in local chalk formations; ungulate fauna shifts toward cold-tolerant species but gazelle, fallow deer, and aurochs persist; freshwater availability contracts in drier phases.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Organic refuse and lithic debris accumulate in cave deposits with ample absorptive capacity; no evidence of landscape degradation or sink saturation at low population density.<br><br>
