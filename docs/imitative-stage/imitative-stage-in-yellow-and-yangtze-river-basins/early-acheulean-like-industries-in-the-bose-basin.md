# __900,000 BCE – 600,000 BCE__ — Early Acheulean-like Industries in the Bose Basin
__Summary:__ A snapshot of a distinctive large cutting tool tradition in the Bose Basin of southern China, associated with Australasian tektites dated to approximately 0.8 Ma. The binding constraint is access to suitable raw material exposed by tektite-associated landscape disturbance. Populations hold adequate margin against subtropical hazards. The technological tradition is stable within the window, showing no directional change.
