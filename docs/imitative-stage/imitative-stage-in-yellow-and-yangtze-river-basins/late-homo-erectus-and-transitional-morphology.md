# __250,000 BCE – 180,000 BCE__ — Late Homo erectus and Transitional Morphology
__Summary:__ Late Homo erectus populations persist in the Yellow and Yangtze basins while morphological drift toward archaic sapiens features emerges; the Hexian cranium exemplifies this transitional state with progressive cranial features; glacial-interglacial oscillations shape population connectivity; the binding constraint remains lithic technology tied to immediate raw-material availability; margin against environmental variability is moderate; change trends toward encephalization and facial reduction.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Yellow River loess plateau and Yangtze basin lowlands remain the primary occupation zones; river corridors and mountain passes structure movement between northern and southern populations; friction moderate across river valleys, higher across upland barriers.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Glacial-interglacial oscillations drive monsoon variability; interglacial phases bring warmer and wetter conditions with expanded forest cover; glacial phases reduce precipitation and shift vegetation toward grassland-steppe; variability considerable but within adaptive range.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Flooding along river channels remains the primary acute hazard; seismic activity in tectonically active zones occasional; populations retain mobility sufficient to evacuate; margin against acute hazards adequate.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Quartz, quartzite, and local chert remain accessible for lithic production; faunal biomass supports foraging; wood fuel and plant foods seasonally abundant; no drawdown pressure evident given low population densities.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Organic refuse and lithic debitage disperse across landscape with no accumulation pressure; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited relative to load; no saturation evident.<br><br>
