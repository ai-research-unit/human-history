# __1,700,000 BCE – 1,200,000 BCE__ — Early Pleistocene Hominin Dispersal Across Northern China
__Summary:__ Long-term trend documenting hominin expansion from the Nihewan Basin heartland southwestward to the Yuanmou and Lantian regions over approximately half a million years. The binding constraint is successful adaptation to diverse temperate environments across the loess plateau and intermontane basins of northern and southwestern China. Margin against shock remains thin given small, scattered populations, but directional spread indicates net demographic success; the trend is one of gradual geographic expansion and environmental diversification.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Dispersal corridor extends from the Nihewan Basin across the loess plateau of the middle Yellow River to the Wei River Valley near Lantian and southwestward to the Yuanmou Basin in Yunnan; rugged terrain and river gorges impose friction but mountain passes and river valleys provide transit routes.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Temperate monsoon regime with strong seasonality; glacial-interglacial oscillations drive cycles of warming and cooling over the window; southwestern sites like Yuanmou experience milder, more subtropical conditions than northern basins.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seasonal flooding along major rivers, periodic drought during weak monsoons, and loess slope instability constitute primary hazards; seismic activity along basin-margin faults adds background risk; populations hold minimal buffer.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ River gravels throughout the corridor provide knappable cobbles; diverse ecological zones from grassland-steppe in the north to subtropical forest in Yunnan expand the resource base; overall endowment supports low-density foraging.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Anthropogenic waste negligible; vast landscapes absorb scattered lithic debitage and organic refuse with effectively unlimited capacity.<br><br>
