# __1,200,000 BCE – 900,000 BCE__ — Lantian and Early Homo erectus in the Wei River Valley
__Summary:__ A snapshot of early Homo erectus occupation along the loess plateau margins of the Wei River Valley, centered on the Gongwangling cranium dated to approximately 1.15 Ma. The primary constraint is the narrow habitable zone where loess meets riverine resources. Populations hold moderate margin against seasonal stress but remain exposed to climatic oscillation. The system is stable with no directional drift.

__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Wei River valley forms a corridor through the eastern Qinling foothills where loess deposits meet alluvial floodplains; movement follows the river axis with lateral access constrained by plateau escarpments.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Temperate continental conditions prevail with warm summers, cold winters, and monsoonal rainfall concentrated in summer months; interannual variability is moderate with occasional drought years.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seasonal flooding along the Wei River poses the principal hazard; loess terrain subject to gullying and slope failure; seismic exposure present but infrequent acute events.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Locally abundant quartzite and quartz cobbles in river gravels; animal biomass concentrated along riparian corridors; plant foods seasonally available on plateau margins.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Effectively unlimited absorptive capacity across an open landscape; negligible organic residue from small foraging populations disperses without accumulation.<br><br>
