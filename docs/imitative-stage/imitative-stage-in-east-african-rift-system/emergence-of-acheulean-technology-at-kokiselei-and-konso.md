# __1,760,000 BCE – 1,400,000 BCE__ — Emergence of Acheulean Technology at Kokiselei and Konso
__Summary:__ A long-term trend documenting the earliest appearance of Acheulean biface technology in the East African Rift, centered on Kokiselei 4 (West Turkana, 1.76 Ma) and Konso (Ethiopia, 1.75 Ma), and its gradual establishment alongside persisting Oldowan assemblages through 1.4 Ma. The binding constraint is cognitive-motor: producing large cutting tools with bifacial shaping demands extended reduction sequences, three-dimensional mental rotation, and precise platform management transmitted entirely through imitative learning. The transition proceeds without displacement — Oldowan and early Acheulean co-occur at multiple sites — suggesting parallel toolmaking traditions rather than simple replacement. Margin for error in biface production is low; failed attempts accumulate as debitage while successful forms become the new technical standard, gradually diffusing across Homo ergaster populations through observational learning.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The corridor spans the Turkana Basin in Kenya and the Konso highlands in southern Ethiopia, connected by rift-valley lowlands and volcanic uplands; lava flows and cobble-strewn stream margins provide abundant raw material; movement friction varies with terrain, higher in dissected volcanic landscapes, lower along lakeshores and river courses.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Fluctuating between wetter and drier phases on orbital timescales; lake-level oscillations document interannual variability; trend within the window moves toward increased aridity, with woodland retreating and open grassland expanding; seasonal water availability concentrates fauna and hominins at persistent sources.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Volcanic activity continues across the Ethiopian and Kenyan Rift, depositing tuffs useful for dating but posing episodic ashfall hazard; drought cycles impose periodic resource stress; lake flooding affects marginal occupation sites; exposure remains chronic but within absorptive capacity of mobile populations.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Lava cobbles and phonolite outcrops supply large blanks suitable for biface manufacture; basalt, quartzite, and obsidian available regionally; herbivore biomass concentrated along drainage systems supports scavenging and early hunting; fuel wood available in wooded patches.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris disperses across open sites; faunal processing waste cleared by scavengers; no evidence of sink saturation; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited relative to waste volumes produced by small, mobile populations.<br><br>

## __P03. Economic Environment__

__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle remains the sole motive power; biface technology represents an efficiency gain in converting carcass mass to usable nutrition through more effective cutting; fire use remains unattested for this window; per-capita energy ceiling slightly elevated by improved tools.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Tool manufacture and foraging organized by individual skill and age; no coercive labor structures; coordination emerges through co-activity and imitative learning; division of labor likely exists between skilled knappers and less proficient individuals learning through observation.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Production consists of tool manufacture and food acquisition for immediate use; bifaces represent increased investment per tool (longer reduction sequences) but no surplus accumulation beyond portable blanks and functional implements; no durable surplus storage.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No systematic storage; fat reserves and behavioral flexibility buffer against scarcity; curated bifaces may serve as portable reserves of cutting capacity, carried between sites rather than manufactured anew at each location.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Raw material transport distances increase relative to earlier Oldowan, with some blanks carried over 10 km; no inter-group exchange networks documented; logistics limited to individual or small-group carrying within extended foraging radius.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No formalized value storage; reciprocity within kin groups operates without accounting; bifaces may embody stored labor value, carried against future butchery need, but no medium of exchange exists.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ Surplus extraction absent; food sharing operates through immediate distribution within the band, likely prioritizing dependents; no authority extracts or reallocates beyond direct provisioning.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Material inequality negligible; "wealth" consists of skill embodied in individuals and portable tools; no defensible accumulation; concentration structurally impossible.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Knapping produces substantial debitage at manufacture sites; bone refuse accumulates at butchery locations; waste disperses through natural processes and scavenger activity; no routing decisions required; load trivial relative to absorptive capacity.<br><br>
