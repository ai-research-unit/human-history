# __2,600,000 BCE – 2,300,000 BCE__ — Emergence of the Oldowan at Gona
__Summary:__ Long-term trend documenting the first systematic core-and-flake reduction in the Ethiopian Afar, beginning at Gona (OGS-6, OGS-7, EG-10, EG-12) around 2.58–2.55 Ma and spreading to multiple sites including Fejej by the window's close; the binding constraint remains the strictly imitative transmission of knapping skill — no linguistic encoding, all technique passed by demonstration and guided practice; the system carries wide margin against shock given low population density and abundant raw material; directional change runs toward geographic expansion and standardization of reduction sequences across the Afar drainage.
