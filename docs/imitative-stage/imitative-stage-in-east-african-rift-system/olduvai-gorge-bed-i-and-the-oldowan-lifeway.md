# __2,000,000 BCE – 1,800,000 BCE__ — Olduvai Gorge Bed I and the Oldowan Lifeway
__Summary:__ A static snapshot of classic Oldowan occupation in the Tanzanian Rift at the type locality of Olduvai Gorge during Bed I deposition; small hominin groups — Homo habilis alongside Paranthropus boisei — occupy lake-margin sites (DK, FLK Zinj, FLK North) where they process scavenged carcasses with simple core-and-flake tools; the binding constraint is access to animal tissue in competition with a formidable carnivore guild; the system holds moderate margin through mobility and dispersed resources across the paleolake basin; the configuration is stable with no directional technological or demographic shift visible within the window.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Olduvai Gorge lies in the eastern branch of the East African Rift within the Ngorongoro volcanic highlands; the Bed I paleolake and associated wetlands create a mosaic of open water, marsh, gallery woodland, and grassland; transit follows lake margins and seasonal stream channels with low friction across the basin floor.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Warm tropical climate with pronounced wet-dry seasonality; lake levels fluctuate with monsoon variability; no sustained aridification or humidification trend within the window; interannual volatility moderate.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Volcanic activity in adjacent Ngorongoro highlands produces periodic tephra falls (Tuff IB, Tuff IC) that preserve archaeological horizons; no catastrophic eruption impacts hominin occupation; flood pulses along seasonal drainages create localized disturbance but exposure remains manageable.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Abundant lava cobbles, quartzite, and chert available in stream beds and outcrops provide knappable raw material; lake-margin ecotone concentrates herbivore biomass; resource endowment effectively unlimited relative to extraction rate.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Bone and lithic debris scattered across open landscapes; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited; no detectable environmental degradation.<br><br>
