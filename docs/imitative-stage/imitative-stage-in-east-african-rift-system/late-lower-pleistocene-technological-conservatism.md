# __900,000 BCE – 600,000 BCE__ — Late Lower Pleistocene Technological Conservatism
__Summary:__ A static snapshot of the East African Rift during the prolonged plateau of Late Lower Pleistocene stability, when Homo erectus populations maintained Classic Acheulean technology with minimal innovation across Olduvai upper Bed IV, Olorgesailie middle members, and associated Kenyan and Ethiopian sites. The binding constraint remains imitative transmission: the motor-copying channel that preserves complex reduction sequences also filters against deviation, locking the repertoire in place. Demographic stability, adequate provisioning, and low environmental pressure leave wide margins across all domains; direction of change is near-zero, and the system coasts in equilibrium with no detectable stress pushing toward transformation.
