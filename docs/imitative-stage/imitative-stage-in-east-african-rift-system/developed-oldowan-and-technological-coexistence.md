# __1,700,000 BCE – 1,400,000 BCE__ — Developed Oldowan and Technological Coexistence
__Summary:__ A 300,000-year trend across upper Olduvai Beds I–II in which the established core-and-flake Oldowan tradition persists alongside newly emergent Acheulean biface technology, with neither replacing the other during this interval. The binding constraint is raw-material availability and nodule size: small cobbles favor Oldowan reduction while larger blanks permit handaxe shaping, so local lithic endowment determines which tradition dominates any given assemblage. Demographic pressure remains well below carrying capacity, absorptive margins are wide, and the system trends toward technological diversification rather than replacement.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Olduvai paleolake basin and surrounding volcanic highlands in northern Tanzania anchor occupation, with riverine corridors linking to the wider Rift System; broken terrain channels movement along drainage valleys and lake margins, imposing moderate friction on cross-landscape transit.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Semi-arid to sub-humid conditions with marked wet-dry seasonality; orbital forcing drives long-term oscillation between wetter lake-high and drier lake-low phases; climate is variable but not trending strongly in either direction during this window.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Active volcanism in the Ngorongoro highlands deposits tuffs that both bury and preserve sites; seismic activity occurs; flooding follows seasonal rains; hominin groups remain mobile enough that local shocks impose disruption rather than catastrophe.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Volcanic lavas, quartzites, and quartz cobbles occur in streambeds and outcrops at varying nodule sizes; lava provides large blanks for bifaces while quartz cobbles suit Oldowan flaking; water concentrates at lakes and springs; faunal biomass is high.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Lithic debitage and bone refuse accumulate at occupation sites; quantities remain trivial against the open landscape; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited; no saturation or degradation evident in the sedimentary record.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Endemic parasites and waterborne pathogens impose background mortality; no epidemic signature appears in the skeletal or taphonomic record; death rates are elevated by predation and injury rather than disease.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Homo ergaster/erectus body size and robusticity suggest adequate protein and caloric intake; dental wear patterns indicate mixed diet; no widespread nutritional stress markers appear; physiological vitality stable or improving.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Reduced sexual dimorphism relative to australopiths suggests pair-bonding or cooperative breeding; prolonged juvenile dependency inferred from developmental markers; reproductive success tied to group provisioning.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density low relative to carrying capacity; birth-to-death ratios support slow but positive growth; multiple hominin taxa (Homo, possibly Paranthropus remnants) may overlap in parts of the window; migration along the Rift adds gene flow.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Juvenile dependency extended; aged individuals rare in the fossil record; orphan and dependent loads absorbed within small group structure; no evidence of dependency overhang exceeding support capacity.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Mixed foraging economy combining plant collection, scavenging, and early carcass access; lakeside and riverine habitats concentrate resources; seasonal variation buffered by mobility and dietary breadth; provisioning floor stable.<br><br>
__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle provides primary motive power; fire use during this window uncertain and unattested at Olduvai; energy capture limited to metabolic throughput and tool-assisted butchery efficiency.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Task structure remains undifferentiated by sex or age beyond physical capability; no coercion mechanism beyond kinship obligation; labor is cooperative and embedded in foraging rounds.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Stone-tool manufacture generates implements sufficient for immediate processing needs; surplus in durable form limited to cached tools if any; production matches consumption within short cycles.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No archaeologically visible storage; buffering accomplished through fat reserves, food sharing, and landscape knowledge; material reserves negligible; resilience rests on mobility and dietary flexibility.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Raw materials sourced within daily foraging range (5–15 km); no evidence of systematic long-distance transport; distribution is direct, within-group, and immediate.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No medium of deferred exchange; reciprocity operates through immediate or near-term food sharing; obligations are personal and embedded in kin relations.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ No coordinating authority extracts surplus; redistribution occurs informally through food sharing at consumption sites; no taxation or tribute mechanism exists.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Portable tools and knowledge constitute assets; concentration minimal; defense consists of retention and use rather than hoarding.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Knapping debris and bone refuse discarded at processing locales; no routing decision beyond abandonment; quantities trivial relative to landscape absorptive capacity.<br><br>
