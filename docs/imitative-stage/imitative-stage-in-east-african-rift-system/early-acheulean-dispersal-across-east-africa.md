# __1,500,000 BCE – 1,000,000 BCE__ — Early Acheulean Dispersal across East Africa
__Summary:__ A 500,000-year trend in which Acheulean bifacial technology expands geographically from initial centers in the Turkana Basin and Ethiopian Rift southward through Tanzania, establishing handaxes and cleavers as the dominant heavy-duty toolkit across the East African Rift System. The binding constraint is access to suitable large blanks — lavas and coarse-grained raw materials capable of yielding workable flakes for bifacial shaping — so dispersal follows lithic source distribution along volcanic corridors. Population density remains low, absorptive margins wide, and the system trends toward technological standardization and geographic homogeneity.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The East African Rift System provides a north-south corridor linking Ethiopian highlands, the Turkana Basin, and the Olduvai-Serengeti region; broken escarpments impose lateral friction while valley floors and lakeshores facilitate movement; distances between key sites span 500–1000 km.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Orbital-driven wet-dry oscillations continue; interglacial phases support expanded lakes and woodland mosaics while glacial phases shift toward arid grassland; variability is high but no unidirectional trend dominates the window; climate sensitivity to precession cycles remains pronounced.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Volcanism remains active along the Rift axis, depositing tuffs and basalts; seismic activity accompanies rifting; seasonal flooding affects lakeshore occupations; hazards are episodic and localized; mobility provides adequate buffering.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Volcanic lavas (basalt, phonolite, trachyte), quartzites, and cherts occur at outcrop and secondary sources throughout the Rift; large nodules suitable for handaxe production are available in stream gravels and volcanic exposures; faunal biomass includes megaherbivores (Elephas recki, equids, large bovids).<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris, bone refuse, and organic waste disperse across an open landscape with effectively unlimited absorptive capacity; no saturation; no environmental degradation signal.<br><br>
