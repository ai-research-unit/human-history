# __75,000 BCE – 60,000 BCE__ — Late Pleistocene Transition and Population Turnover
__Summary:__ The Toba supervolcanic eruption around 74 ka delivers catastrophic ashfall across Sunda and beyond, disrupting vegetation and potentially imposing severe demographic bottleneck; Homo erectus populations on Java face extinction pressure from combined volcanic impact, climate deterioration during MIS 4, and competitive displacement by modern humans; by window close erectus is extinct or functionally so; modern humans establish via coastal dispersal routes along the exposed shelf; the binding constraint is post-Toba environmental recovery capacity; margin against shock is severely reduced at window opening before gradually recovering; direction of change is toward complete taxonomic turnover and modern human establishment.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Sunda Shelf extensively exposed during MIS 4 glacial lowstand, creating continuous land from mainland Southeast Asia through Java, Sumatra, and Borneo; coastlines distant from modern positions; transit friction low across exposed coastal plains, moderate through forest interiors.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Toba eruption at window opening injects massive sulfate aerosols, inducing severe multi-year cooling and vegetation die-off; subsequent MIS 4 glacial conditions bring sustained cooler and drier climate; volatility extreme at opening, stabilizing to glacial baseline thereafter.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Toba ashfall deposits meters thick across Sumatra and substantial across wider region; volcanic hazard episodically catastrophic; seismic activity continues along arc; hominin populations hold minimal margin at eruption impact, recovering capacity through later window.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Forest and faunal resources severely depleted in eruption aftermath; gradual recovery over millennia; coastal resources along exposed shelf edges provide alternative subsistence base; endowment contracted at opening, slowly rebuilding.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Anthropogenic waste negligible; volcanic tephra constitutes massive natural deposit requiring landscape recovery; absorptive capacity for hominin waste effectively unlimited.<br><br>
