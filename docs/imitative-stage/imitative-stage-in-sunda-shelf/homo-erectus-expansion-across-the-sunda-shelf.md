# __1,000,000 BCE – 700,000 BCE__ — Homo erectus Expansion Across the Sunda Shelf
__Summary:__ A long-term trend of Homo erectus demographic growth and geographic spread across the intermittently exposed Sunda landmass, driven by glacial-lowstand land bridges connecting Java to Sumatra and Borneo; the binding constraint is coastal and riverine corridor availability during lowered sea levels; margin against population isolation remains positive while the shelf stays exposed; the trend direction is outward dispersal and population increase.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Sunda Shelf lies exposed during glacial lowstands, linking Java, Sumatra, and Borneo into a continuous tropical landmass drained by large river systems emptying northward into the South China Sea; transit friction is low along riverine corridors and coastal plains but rises sharply in montane interiors.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Tropical conditions prevail with reduced monsoon intensity during glacials; rainfall is lower than interglacial peaks but sufficient to sustain mixed forest-savanna mosaics; interannual variability moderate; no secular drift toward aridity within this window.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Volcanic activity along the Sunda Arc poses episodic hazard through ashfall and pyroclastic events; seismic risk moderate; flood pulses seasonal along major rivers; populations hold adequate margin by dispersed settlement across a large landmass.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Siliceous and volcanic stone for tool manufacture locally available; freshwater abundant along river systems; faunal biomass substantial in forest-edge and riverine ecotones; no evidence of drawdown pressure on standing stocks.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Organic waste and knapping debris negligible relative to landscape scale; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited; no saturation.<br><br>
