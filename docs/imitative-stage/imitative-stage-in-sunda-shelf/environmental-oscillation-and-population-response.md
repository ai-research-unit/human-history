# __200,000 BCE – 100,000 BCE__ — Environmental Oscillation and Population Response

__Summary:__ Across one hundred millennia the Sunda Shelf undergoes repeated glacial-interglacial transitions, each swing redrawing coastlines by hundreds of kilometers and alternately uniting and fragmenting Homo erectus populations; the binding constraint is this rhythm of connection and isolation, which pulses gene flow and local adaptation without any capacity for cultural buffering beyond what bodies can demonstrate and hands can copy.

## D01. Physical Domain

__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ During glacial maxima sea level drops 80–120 meters, exposing a vast lowland plain linking Java, Sumatra, and Borneo to mainland Asia; river systems extend across what are now shallow seas, and the coastline retreats hundreds of kilometers. Interglacial high stands submerge these corridors, fragmenting continuous habitat into island chains and creating the modern island configuration. Transit friction oscillates: low stands permit unimpeded terrestrial movement across Sundaland, high stands impose sea barriers that only driftwood or swimming can cross.

__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ The region remains tropical throughout, but precipitation patterns shift with glacial state; lowland grassland-forest mosaics expand during cooler drier phases, while closed-canopy rainforest contracts to upland refugia. Interglacials restore wetter conditions and forest dominance. Temperature swings are modest by high-latitude standards but sufficient to alter plant and animal communities.

__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Volcanic activity along the Sunda arc persists; Krakatau and the Sumatran chain episodically blanket portions of the shelf in ash. Flooding accompanies monsoon intensification during warm phases. Drought risk rises during glacial maxima as monsoons weaken. Populations hold no engineered margin against these shocks.

__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Large mammal biomass tracks habitat area; low stands that expand Sundaland also expand grazing landscapes supporting Stegodon, rhinoceros, and bovids. Plant resources shift with forest cover. Freshwater availability fluctuates with river course changes as drainage reorganizes across the exposed shelf.

__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ At the population densities and subsistence modes of Homo erectus bands, anthropogenic waste loads are negligible; natural decomposition and fluvial dispersal clear organic refuse without saturation.

## D02. Biological Domain

__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Tropical pathogen pressure remains high; malaria, intestinal parasites, and waterborne infections exact a baseline toll. The relatively low population density limits epidemic transmission but does not eliminate endemic disease. Mortality is elevated relative to temperate zones but stable across the window.

__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Robust skeletal remains from the Ngandong series indicate large-bodied, physically powerful adults adapted to active foraging. Nutrition tracks game availability; low stands that expand savanna likely improve protein access, while forest-dominated interglacials shift diets toward plant foods and smaller game.

__Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity:__ Care is limited to what group members can provide through direct assistance: wound cleaning, immobilization, food sharing. No pharmacological knowledge can be stored or transmitted except through observed imitation. Recovery depends on individual constitution and the group's willingness to carry injured members.

__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Pair-bonding and extended juvenile dependence characterize the species; sexual maturation is slow, and interbirth intervals are long relative to body size. Mating patterns cannot be reconstructed from skeletal evidence but likely involve polygyny and male competition.

__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density is low, perhaps a few individuals per hundred square kilometers. Band sizes are small, likely 20–50 members. Replacement is slow; high infant and juvenile mortality limits net growth. Population tracks available habitat area, expanding during low stands and contracting during high stands.

__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Few individuals survive to old age; the dependency load is primarily young children. The productive cohort absorbs this load through food sharing without accumulating a significant elder population.

## D03. Economic Domain

__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Foraging provides the provisioning floor; there is no cultivation, no storage beyond daily carry capacity. The population lives close to the margin, with surplus rare and transient. Household pooling occurs through food sharing within the band.

__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle provides all motive power. Fire is present but its use for cooking, warmth, and predator deterrence is opportunistic rather than systematic. No tools amplify energy conversion beyond sharpened stone and shaped wood.

__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Division of labor follows age and sex without formal specialization. There is no coercion beyond dominance hierarchies; labor is mobilized by immediate need and group cohesion, not command or exchange.

__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Production is consumption; nothing is produced beyond immediate use. No surplus accumulates, and no capacity exists to store or transmit production methods except through direct observation.

__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ Body fat is the only buffer against shortage. There are no granaries, caches, or reserves. A failed season means reduced intake, increased mortality, and possible band dissolution.

__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ No trade exists. Raw materials are locally sourced; tool stone travels only as far as the band carries it. There is no exchange network linking dispersed groups.

__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No medium of value exists. Reciprocity operates through immediate food sharing and remembered obligation, but memory cannot outlast the parties involved.

__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ Dominant individuals may claim priority access to food or mates, but no institutionalized extraction occurs. Redistribution is ad hoc sharing, not systematic allocation.

__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ There are no durable assets to distribute or defend. Positional advantage is ephemeral, tied to individual strength and social standing.

__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Organic waste disperses naturally; bone and lithic debris accumulate at occupation sites but without exceeding the landscape's absorptive capacity.

## D04. Social Domain

__Kinship, Family & Inheritance:__ Kinship is recognized through maternal bonds and sibling familiarity; no formal marriage rules or inheritance customs can be documented. Pair bonds form and dissolve without ceremony. Property does not exist to be inherited.

__Association & Mutual Aid:__ The band is the sole unit of association. Mutual aid is expressed through food sharing, collective defense, and cooperative hunting. No institutions exist beyond face-to-face interaction.

__Leisure, Play & Sociability:__ Play among juveniles transmits foraging and social skills through imitation. Adults engage in grooming and proximity maintenance. Festive structure, if any, is not recoverable from the archaeological record.

__Stratification, Status & Group Structure:__ Dominance hierarchies exist based on physical prowess and age. Status is individual and transient, not heritable. No castes, classes, or permanent factions exist.

__Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity:__ No administrative apparatus exists. Decision-making is distributed and immediate, resolved through dominance, consensus, or fission of the band.

__Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force:__ Coercion is individual and interpersonal. There are no specialized fighters, no weapons beyond hunting tools, and no organized violence between groups recoverable from the record.

__Discharge, Disorder & Decay:__ Conflict resolves through submission, avoidance, or band fission. Grievance cannot accumulate across generations because there is no medium to transmit it. Disorder is absorbed by the flexibility of band structure.

## D05. Emotional Domain

__Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper:__ Affective states are immediate and interpersonal. Trust extends to familiar band members; strangers provoke wariness. Mood tracks environmental conditions and social standing without cultural mediation.

__Leadership & Personal Authority:__ Leadership is informal, situational, and based on demonstrated competence in hunting, defense, or social negotiation. No office or title exists; authority must be continually earned through action.

__Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance:__ Allegiance is to the band and its dominant members, maintained through daily interaction. There is no abstract order to legitimate and no ideology to justify hierarchy.

__Political Will & Collective Resolve:__ Collective action is coordinated through imitation and response to dominant individuals. There is no deliberation, no persuasion through argument, only demonstration and following.

__External Relations & Posture:__ Relations between bands are unknown; population density is low enough that encounters may be rare. Where bands meet, outcomes likely range from avoidance to interbreeding to conflict, resolved without lasting institutions.

__Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will:__ Individual withdrawal, depression, or exhaustion occurs but does not accumulate as a social fact. The band's affective tone resets with personnel turnover and environmental change.

## D06. Informational Domain

__Communication Accessibility:__ Communication is gestural, postural, and possibly vocal but not linguistic. Meaning is carried by demonstration, not symbol. Mutual legibility depends on shared experience and proximity.

__Channels & Transmission Velocity:__ Information travels no faster than a walking individual. There is no way to send a message ahead or store one for later retrieval. Knowledge is tied to bodies and present context.

__Record, Storage & Persistence:__ There is no record. Memory is individual and dies with the carrier. Collective knowledge persists only through overlapping generations of imitation.

__Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms:__ There is no discourse to frame or censor. Communication is situational and immediate, not propositional.

__Reception, Attention & Amplification:__ Attention is captured by movement, sound, and social cues. There is no mechanism for amplification beyond the reach of voice and gesture.

__Signal Integrity & Noise Load:__ Signal decay is rapid; any technique not continually demonstrated risks loss. The noise floor is the limit of imitative fidelity across generations.

## D07. Moral Domain

__Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice:__ Moral intuitions exist — fairness in food sharing, outrage at cheating — but cannot be articulated or codified. Justice is enacted through immediate response, not rule application.

__Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution:__ There is no law, no code, no rule that can be stated or stored. Conduct is regulated by dominance, habit, and immediate sanction.

__Adjudication, Liability & Sanction:__ Disputes are resolved by force, submission, or avoidance. There is no third-party adjudication, no formal liability, no graduated sanction beyond individual retaliation.

__Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden:__ No rights exist as claims on others. Protection comes from the band's collective interest in each member's contribution. Compliance is to immediate social pressure, not abstract obligation.

__Impunity & Unredressed Wrong:__ Wrongs are addressed or forgotten within the lifespan of the parties. There is no accumulated backlog of injustice because there is no record to maintain it.

## D08. Intellectual Domain

__Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm:__ There is no inquiry, no theory, no paradigm. Understanding is practical and immediate: which plants are edible, which animals can be taken, which places are dangerous. Knowledge is know-how, not know-that.

__Applied Knowledge & Technical Method:__ Tool-making persists through imitation; the Oldowan/Mode 1 tradition continues with little elaboration. Bone and bamboo may serve as tools but leave minimal archaeological trace. The technical repertoire is stable across the entire window.

__Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution:__ Skills are transmitted by observation and practice. There is no teaching in the sense of intentional pedagogy; learning occurs through participation. The knowledge stock is the sum of living practitioners' competencies.

__Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay:__ Techniques are lost when their practitioners die without successors. The sparse lithic record on Java may reflect repeated loss and reinvention as much as reliance on organic materials.

## D09. Cultural Domain

__Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity:__ There is no mythos, no narrative identity, no origin story. Identity is immediate: this band, these individuals, this place. Memory is episodic and does not aggregate into collective history.

__Legitimating Narrative & Ideology:__ No order exists that requires legitimation. Dominance is self-evident; it needs no story to justify it.

__Artistic Expression & Material Culture:__ No unambiguous art survives from this window. Material culture is utilitarian: tools, food refuse, fire-cracked rock. If symbolic behavior exists, it leaves no durable trace.

__Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay:__ There are no forms to exhaust, no symbols to hollow out. The cultural dimension is minimal — the floor beneath which nothing lies.

## D10. Spiritual Domain

__Cosmology & Existential Outlook:__ No cosmology can be attributed. The existential outlook is immediate: present danger, present need, present satisfaction. There is no recoverable evidence of metaphysical concern.

__Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice:__ No ritual is attested. If repeated actions serve social bonding, they do not leave archaeological signatures distinguishable from practical activity.

__Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority:__ There are no institutions, no doctrine, no authority. The spiritual dimension is empty or unrecoverable.

__Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement:__ There is nothing to diffuse, blend, or displace. If beliefs exist, they are local, transient, and invisible to the archaeological record.

__Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge:__ The concept does not apply. There is no enchantment to lose, no sacred frame to erode. The spiritual register is silent.
