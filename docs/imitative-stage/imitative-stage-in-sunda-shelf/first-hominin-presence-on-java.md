# __1,600,000 BCE – 1,400,000 BCE__ — First Hominin Presence on Java
__Summary:__ Static snapshot of the earliest Homo erectus colonization of Java, representing the first hominin presence in insular Southeast Asia. The window captures initial arrival at Sangiran and Mojokerto, with hominins establishing viable populations in tropical volcanic landscapes. The binding constraint is the absence of any prior hominin infrastructure or accumulated local knowledge, requiring full reliance on African-derived biological and behavioral capacities. Margin against environmental shock is minimal given small founder populations; the principal direction is establishment rather than expansion.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Java's volcanic spine creates north-south barriers; lowland corridors along river valleys and coasts provide primary transit routes; exposed Sunda Shelf connects Java to mainland Southeast Asia during glacial lowstands, enabling pedestrian colonization.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Tropical equatorial climate with year-round warmth and seasonal monsoon rainfall; interannual variability tied to volcanic eruptions and El Nino-Southern Oscillation cycles; stable within the window with no secular drift detected.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ High volcanic and seismic exposure from active Indonesian arc; lahars, pyroclastic flows, and earthquakes pose recurrent acute threats; small, dispersed populations hold minimal buffer against catastrophic eruption.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Abundant volcanic soils support dense vegetation; riverine systems provide water and aquatic resources; lithic raw materials include volcanic cobbles and silicified wood; stock is effectively unlimited relative to tiny hominin demand.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Negligible hominin waste load; tropical forest and riverine systems absorb all organic and lithic discard without detectable impact; absorptive capacity effectively infinite at this population scale.<br><br>

__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Tropical pathogen environment with endemic parasites, vector-borne diseases, and bacterial infections; mortality baseline elevated relative to temperate zones; hominins carry African-adapted immune profiles of uncertain local efficacy.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Homo erectus body size and robusticity indicate adequate nutritional intake; tropical fruits, tubers, and animal protein support physiological maintenance; no evidence of chronic nutritional stress in available skeletal material.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Hominin reproductive biology follows African Homo erectus patterns; extended juvenile dependency requires stable pair or group provisioning; mating dynamics unattested but presumed to involve multi-male, multi-female groupings.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Founder population likely small, with slow initial demographic growth; density extremely low across Java; birth-to-death ratio marginally positive, sufficient for establishment but vulnerable to stochastic loss.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ High infant and juvenile mortality typical of early Homo; elderly dependents rare given short adult lifespans; productive cohort absorbs limited dependency load without apparent strain at this population scale.<br><br>
