# __700,000 BCE – 400,000 BCE__ — Middle Pleistocene Expansion across Sunda

__Summary:__ A long-term trend spanning three hundred millennia during which Homo erectus populations, established on Java, expanded across the emergent Sunda shelf during glacial lowstands when falling sea levels transformed the Java Sea into a traversable tropical savanna. The central tension is the interplay between environmental opportunity (land bridges opening) and the limits of imitative transmission: each daughter band that fissions and disperses carries only the skills its members have personally witnessed, and no technique can travel faster than the people who hold it, so the expanding frontier is a series of local copies, not a coordinated colonization.

## D01. Physical Domain

__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Sunda Shelf is a shallow continental platform underlying the modern Java, South China, and Celebes Seas. During glacial maxima, sea levels drop 100-120 meters below present, exposing a landmass ("Sundaland") larger than the Indian subcontinent. Java, Sumatra, Borneo, and the Malay Peninsula become continuous, connected by broad tropical lowlands dissected by the paleo-Sunda, paleo-Mekong, and paleo-Chao Phraya river systems. Spatial friction drops sharply during lowstands: what are today sea crossings become overland walks. During interglacials, rising seas fragment the shelf, isolating populations on the emergent islands. The Wallace Line to the east remains a permanent biogeographic barrier; the deep water between Bali and Lombok is never bridged by land.

__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Tropical conditions persist throughout, but glacial-interglacial cycles drive oscillations in rainfall and vegetation. Glacial phases are cooler and drier; open savanna-woodland expands at the expense of closed rainforest. Interglacials are warmer and wetter, with dense forest cover. The 100,000-year orbital cycle modulates these shifts, superimposed on shorter-period Milankovitch rhythms. Within any human generation the climate appears stable, but across the trend the landscape transforms repeatedly.

__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Volcanic eruptions along the Sunda arc (Sumatra, Java) deposit tephra across wide areas. Earthquakes accompany subduction-zone tectonics. Flooding is recurrent in the low-relief exposed shelf during interglacials, as river systems adjust to changing sea level. Populations hold no engineered buffers; exposure is absorbed by mobility and post-shock recolonization.

__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ The exposed Sunda lowlands offer productive savanna and gallery-forest habitats with diverse fauna (Stegodon, orangutan, tiger, rhinoceros, deer, cattle) and plant resources (tubers, fruits, bamboo). Stone resources are unevenly distributed; volcanic Java and Sumatra have adequate raw material, while the sedimentary lowlands of the exposed shelf may lack good flaking stone, possibly reinforcing reliance on organic (bamboo, wood) tools. The endowment supports dispersed hominin populations but does not concentrate them.

__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ At band-scale population densities distributed across a subcontinent, anthropogenic impact is negligible. Organic waste is recycled by decomposers; bone and stone accumulate slowly in sediments. The sink is effectively unlimited; no saturation feedback occurs.

## D02. Biological Domain

__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Tropical pathogens (malaria precursors, intestinal parasites, arboviruses) impose endemic background mortality. Epidemic events may occur when bands encounter novel disease reservoirs in new territories, but population densities are too low for sustained epidemic transmission. Mortality is high by modern standards, with life expectancy at birth in the mid-twenties; most deaths occur in infancy and childhood.

__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ The expanding populations draw on diverse resources across ecological gradients. Nutritional adequacy varies with local abundance; dental and skeletal stress markers in the Trinil-era fossils suggest episodic but not chronic shortage. Body size remains robust, indicating sufficient protein and caloric intake to sustain large-bodied adults.

__Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity:__ Healing depends on bodily resilience and whatever wound-care behaviors have been transmitted by demonstration. Medicinal plant use cannot be documented but is plausible given erectus cognitive capacity. Some individuals survive serious injury (healed fractures), implying social support during convalescence.

__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Sexual dimorphism suggests male-male competition for mates. Prolonged infant dependency constrains female mobility and ties reproduction to social support. Pair-bonding or polygynous structures are possible; evidence is insufficient to distinguish.

__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population growth is slow but positive on net across the trend, driving the expansion. As bands grow beyond optimal size (perhaps 25-50 individuals), daughter bands fission and disperse into unoccupied territory. Total population across Sundaland during lowstands may reach several thousand individuals distributed in scores of bands; during highstands, populations contract to refugia on the emergent islands.

__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ High infant mortality absorbs most of the dependent load. Few individuals survive to old age; the elderly dependent overhang is light. Widows, orphans, and the injured are absorbed by the band or perish; there is no slack capacity for unproductive dependents.

## D03. Economic Domain

__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Foraging persists as the sole subsistence mode: gathering plant foods, scavenging, and opportunistic hunting. The expanding range provides access to new resource patches, potentially raising the per-capita provisioning floor as bands move into unoccupied territory with intact game populations and ungathered plant stands. The subsistence floor is above starvation in most years, but thin enough that local drought or prey depletion prompts movement.

__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle remains the sole motive power. Fire is used for cooking, warmth, and possibly landscape burning to encourage savanna growth and concentrate game. Cooking increases caloric yield from plant foods. Energy capture is sufficient for survival and reproduction but leaves little surplus for elaborated technology.

__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Labor is organized informally by sex and age within the band. No coerced labor; cooperation is voluntary and kin-based. Division of labor likely sees females focused on gathering and child care, males on hunting and defense, but all adults are generalists capable of basic survival tasks.

__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Production is for immediate consumption. No surplus accumulates; no storage technology exists. The economy is hand-to-mouth, buffered only by body fat and band-level food sharing.

__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ Body fat is the reserve. Social reciprocity obligations within the band pool daily variance. No granaries, no preserved foods, no artificial storage. Risk is managed by mobility: when local resources fail, the band moves.

__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Exchange is intra-band sharing and inter-band gifting during rare encounters. No trade networks, no transport infrastructure. Raw materials are obtained locally; stone transport distances are measured in kilometers, not hundreds of kilometers.

__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No medium of exchange. Reciprocity obligations are held in memory and social relationship. Credit is personal and non-transferable; there is no way to store or compound value across generations.

__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ No authority extracts surplus. Sharing is normatively enforced within the band. Redistribution is horizontal (among band members), not hierarchical.

__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Material wealth differences are minimal. Status may confer priority access to food or mates, but there is no accumulated property to defend. Territorial defense is at the band level, maintained by presence and occasional confrontation.

__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Organic waste is scattered and decomposed. Lithic debitage accumulates at knapping sites but at densities too low to constitute a disposal problem. No waste management is required.

## D04. Social Domain

__Kinship, Family & Inheritance:__ Kinship is the primary social structure. Bands are composed of related individuals; membership is determined by birth and possibly by immigration through pair-bonding. Inheritance is biological (genetic) and behavioral (imitated skills); there is no material property to bequeath.

__Association & Mutual Aid:__ The band is the sole durable association. Mutual aid within the band is obligatory for survival. Inter-band relations are episodic and variable: kin-linked bands may cooperate; unrelated bands may avoid each other or compete. The expansion disperses bands into empty territory, minimizing inter-band conflict.

__Leisure, Play & Sociability:__ Leisure is embedded in the foraging routine. Play among juveniles transmits motor and social skills. Adults rest and socialize during non-foraging hours. No formal festive calendar, but sociability is constant within the band.

__Stratification, Status & Group Structure:__ Status hierarchies exist based on age, sex, and individual reputation. No classes, castes, or formal ranks. Egalitarianism prevails in the sense that no offices or hereditary distinctions exist, but dominance relationships structure daily interaction.

__Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity:__ There is no administration. Bands are self-organizing, with decisions made by consensus or dominant individuals. No offices, no bureaucracy, no record-keeping. Administrative capacity is zero by later standards.

__Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force:__ Coercion is personal, exercised through physical strength and threat display. The band as a whole can exclude or punish deviant members. No organized military, no weapons beyond general-purpose hunting tools. Conflict resolution is face-to-face.

__Discharge, Disorder & Decay:__ Social friction is discharged by fission: bands that grow too large or conflict-ridden split, with daughter bands dispersing. The expansion itself is a discharge mechanism, channeling population pressure outward rather than letting it accrete as internal disorder. When bands collapse (through disease, predation, or resource failure), the territory is eventually recolonized by neighboring bands.

## D05. Emotional Domain

__Leadership & Personal Authority:__ Leadership is situational. Individuals with relevant skills (navigating new territory, leading hunts, resolving disputes) may lead in those contexts. No permanent leaders; authority is personal and demonstrated, not inherited or appointed.

__Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance:__ Allegiance is to kin and band-mates, renewed daily through interaction and reciprocity. There is no abstract legitimacy, no ruler to obey or defy. Belonging is immediate and practical.

__Political Will & Collective Resolve:__ The capacity for collective action is limited to band-scale tasks: group foraging, defense, childcare. Inter-band coordination is rare and ad hoc. Political will as a concept does not apply; there is no polity, only bands. Succession is non-problematic because there are no offices.

__External Relations & Posture:__ Relations with other bands are episodic, ranging from avoidance to occasional cooperation to conflict. The expansion is not coordinated; it is a diffusion of autonomous bands into empty space. No alliances, no treaties, no diplomacy.

__Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper:__ The emotional baseline combines vigilance (predator awareness), social attunement (tracking band-mates' states), and the satisfactions of successful foraging and sociality. Trust is high within the band, low or absent toward outsiders. The expanding frontier may generate a behavioral temper of exploration among dispersing bands, but caution remains adaptive in novel environments.

__Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will:__ Disaffection leads to departure, not internal decay. Individuals who cannot integrate leave or are expelled. Apathy is not adaptive; the demands of survival enforce engagement. The affective load is discharged through social interaction and the mobility the expansion allows.


## D06. Informational Domain

__Communication Accessibility:__ Communication is gestural, postural, vocal (non-linguistic), and demonstrative. Skills are transmitted by showing, not telling. The shared code is bodily, intelligible only to those who have grown up observing the same demonstrators. Each band develops local variants in technique; there is no pan-Sunda standard. Mutual intelligibility with distant bands is uncertain; cultural drift accompanies geographic dispersal.

__Channels & Transmission Velocity:__ Information travels at walking pace. There are no signals that outpace the bearer. When bands fission and disperse, each daughter band carries only what its members have personally learned; no technique travels ahead of the people who know it.

__Record, Storage & Persistence:__ There is no external record. All knowledge is stored in living bodies and minds. When an individual dies without having demonstrated a skill to a learner, that skill is lost. Transmission is lossy; each generation reinvents some techniques and loses others. Across three hundred millennia, this drift is significant: local repertoires diverge, and no net accumulation of complexity occurs.

__Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms:__ Without language, there is no discourse to frame or censor. Social norms are enforced by immediate reaction (approval, punishment), not verbal prohibition.

__Reception, Attention & Amplification:__ Attention is captured by movement, novelty, and social cues. A skilled demonstrator can teach multiple observers, but only those present. There is no broadcast, no amplification beyond the reach of a voice or gesture.

__Signal Integrity & Noise Load:__ Signal integrity depends on imitation fidelity. Copying errors accumulate; there is no correction mechanism beyond trial and result. The cultural ratchet slips as well as advances. Across the trend, techniques drift without necessarily improving.

## D07. Moral Domain

__Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice:__ Moral intuitions are social-emotional: fairness, reciprocity, kin favoritism, punishment of cheaters. These are felt, not articulated. Right and wrong are immediate reactions, not debated principles.

__Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution:__ There is no law, no code, no articulated rule. Behavioral norms are transmitted by example and enforced by social response. "Law" in any formal sense does not exist.

__Adjudication, Liability & Sanction:__ Disputes are resolved by dominance, reconciliation, or avoidance. There are no courts, no judges, no formal procedures. Sanction is immediate: exclusion, retaliation, or withdrawal of cooperation.

__Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden:__ There are no formal rights. Protection is provided by the band to its members as a matter of mutual interest. Compliance is with social expectations, enforced by the continuous presence of others.

__Impunity & Unredressed Wrong:__ The physically dominant can act with relative impunity within the band. Wronged individuals have recourse to alliance-building, retaliation, or departure. The expansion provides an exit option: an aggrieved individual can leave and join or found another band. There is no accumulating justice deficit because there is no formal justice to fall short.

## D08. Intellectual Domain

__Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm:__ There is no formal inquiry. Understanding is practical: what works is repeated, what fails is abandoned. Cause and effect are grasped at the level of immediate experience. There are no theories, no paradigms, no explanatory frameworks beyond embodied know-how.

__Applied Knowledge & Technical Method:__ Technical knowledge is embodied: how to knap a stone, identify an edible plant, track an animal, maintain a fire. The toolkit is simple and static across the trend: core and flake tools, possibly wooden spears and digging sticks (not preserved), fire. The sparse lithic record in Sunda suggests reliance on organic tools (bamboo, wood, rattan) that decay. No innovation accumulates; the repertoire is maintained, not expanded.

__Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution:__ Education is apprenticeship by observation. Children learn by watching adults. The knowledge stock is local and shallow: each band holds a repertoire of survival skills specific to its territory. Skill distribution is relatively even; all adults master basic competencies. Specialists, if any, are individuals with particular aptitudes, not trained professionals.

__Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay:__ Skills are lost when their holders die without demonstrating them. Local extinctions of technique occur when bands disappear. There is no net accumulation across the trend; the cultural repertoire circa 400,000 BCE is not discernibly more complex than that of 700,000 BCE. Obsolescence is not a problem because there is nothing to supersede.

## D09. Cultural Domain

__Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity:__ There is no verbal mythos, no origin story, no historical memory beyond individual lifetimes. Identity is immediate: this band, these kin, this territory. The expansion creates geographic differentiation but no articulated cultural identity.

__Legitimating Narrative & Ideology:__ Without language, there is no narrative legitimation. The social order is accepted as given. Authority rests on presence and power, not story.

__Artistic Expression & Material Culture:__ Material culture is sparse: stone flakes, fire, possibly organic tools. No art objects, no ornaments, no pigment use is documented for Sunda erectus in this period. Expression, if any, is in gesture and bodily display. The made-object world is functional, not decorative.

__Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay:__ Without symbolic forms, there is no form exhaustion. Behaviors may become routine, but there is no symbolic meaning to hollow out.

## D10. Spiritual Domain

__Cosmology & Existential Outlook:__ There is no documented cosmology. Existential outlook, if any, is not articulated. The world is experienced directly; death is observed but not ritualized.

__Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice:__ No evidence of ritual behavior survives in the archaeological record. If repeated coordinated behaviors exist, they are not distinguishable from functional activities. The sacred, as a category, may not yet exist.

__Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority:__ There are no religious institutions, no priesthood, no doctrine.

__Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement:__ Without articulated belief systems, there is nothing to diffuse or blend. The spiritual dimension is, so far as evidence permits, absent or archaeologically invisible.

__Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge:__ There is no enchantment to lose. The residue of disenchantment does not register because the sacred has not been articulated in a form that can decay.
