# __1,400,000 BCE – 1,000,000 BCE__ — Sangiran and the Early Javanese Homo erectus Population
__Summary:__ Static snapshot of the established Homo erectus population centered on the Sangiran dome during the early Pleistocene. The window captures a stable tropical adaptation with a growing fossil record documenting cranial morphology and faunal associations. The binding constraint is the technological conservatism and cognitive ceiling of imitative transmission, which maintains behavioral continuity across generations without cumulative innovation. Margin against environmental shock remains limited by small population size; the principal direction is demographic consolidation rather than expansion or change.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Sangiran dome rises from the Solo River basin in Central Java, providing elevated terrain amid floodplains; volcanic highlands to the south and coastal lowlands to the north define the spatial arena; river corridors facilitate movement across otherwise densely vegetated terrain.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Tropical monsoon climate with distinct wet and dry seasons; temperatures consistently warm year-round; interannual variability moderate; no significant secular drift within the window; climate regime stable and predictable.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Active volcanism from Javanese arc generates periodic ashfall and lahar events; seismic activity accompanies volcanism; exposure high but frequency of catastrophic events low within any given generation; populations dispersed enough to survive localized disasters.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Volcanic soils support rich tropical forest and savanna mosaic; the Solo River provides freshwater and aquatic resources; lithic raw materials abundant in river gravels and volcanic outcrops; resource stock far exceeds demand.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Negligible hominin waste output absorbed by tropical decomposition cycles; river transport disperses organic and lithic discards; no measurable impact on sink capacity; absorptive margin effectively unlimited.<br><br>
