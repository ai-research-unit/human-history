# __130,000 BCE – 100,000 BCE__ — Interglacial Conditions and Hominin Distribution
__Summary:__ A static snapshot of the Sunda region during MIS 5e, the last interglacial, when high sea stands fragment the formerly continuous Sunda landmass into isolated islands; hominin populations, if surviving from the Ngandong phase, face severe geographic constriction and reduced connectivity; the binding constraint is marine transgression eliminating overland corridors; the system holds minimal demographic margin on fragmented refugia with no evidence of watercraft or island-hopping capability; the principal direction is population isolation and probable local extinction on smaller islands.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Rising seas inundate the Sunda Shelf lowlands, converting continuous landmass into the archipelagic pattern resembling modern Indonesia; Java, Sumatra, and Borneo become separate islands, severing overland transit.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Full interglacial warmth brings increased precipitation and expanded tropical rainforest; sea surface temperatures rise; seasonal variability is moderate; climate is stable at the warm end of Pleistocene oscillation.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Volcanic hazards persist on Java and Sumatra; tsunami risk increases for coastal refugia; flooding from higher rainfall impacts riverine settlements; overall hazard exposure rises for populations confined to shrinking coastal margins.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Dense rainforest canopy reduces ground-level foraging productivity relative to more open glacial-stage vegetation; marine resources become more accessible for coastal populations but require new extraction techniques.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Absorptive capacity remains effectively unlimited given minimal population density; organic waste disperses into expanding forest and marine systems without accumulation.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Warmer, wetter conditions may elevate vector-borne disease load; endemic tropical pathogens persist; mortality baseline remains moderate to high for hominin populations without cumulative medical countermeasures.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Rainforest expansion reduces availability of preferred open-country resources; dietary stress may increase for populations adapted to more open habitats; physiological vitality is under pressure where adaptation lags.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Reproductive biology unchanged; mating and pair-bonding patterns continue as before; reduced population connectivity may elevate inbreeding risk on smaller islands.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density declines as habitat fragments; isolated island populations fall below viable thresholds on smaller landmasses; renewal rates likely drop toward negative territory on constrained refugia.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Dependency ratios may rise as productive adults face resource stress; the small population cannot absorb elevated dependency without demographic contraction.<br><br>

__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Surplus generation is negligible or absent; daily foraging meets immediate needs with no accumulation; productive capacity cannot expand against environmental constraints.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No storage technology; buffering depends on body reserves and fallback foods; margin against shortage is thinner than in glacial periods with more diverse habitats.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Inter-island exchange is impossible without watercraft; overland exchange networks collapse with rising seas; populations on separate islands are economically isolated.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ Deferred reciprocity operates within surviving groups; no medium of stored value; the system contracts along with the social network that supports it.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ No surplus to extract; communal sharing operates at band level as before but with smaller resource base to distribute.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Wealth differentiation remains minimal; portable tools and embodied knowledge are the only defensible assets; no accumulation or stratification.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Waste load is negligible; discard disperses into forest and coastal matrices; no environmental degradation from economic activity.<br><br>
