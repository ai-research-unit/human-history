# __1500000 BCE – 1000000 BCE__ — Early Acheulean Colonization of Peninsular India
__Summary:__ The earliest confirmed hominin presence on the Indian Peninsula, dated to 1.51 Ma at Attirampakkam in the Kortallayar Basin; Large Flake Acheulean technology involving handaxes and cleavers exceeding 20 cm; Homo erectus or related hominins establishing persistent occupation across the wider Deccan; the binding constraint is raw material availability at suitable cobble and boulder sources along seasonal watercourses; margin against environmental shock remains high given low population density and abundant territory; directional change is one of gradual territorial expansion from initial footholds.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Deccan Plateau and coastal Tamil Nadu present a gently undulating peneplain dissected by east-flowing river systems including the Kortallayar; low spatial friction for movement across open woodland and grassland; rocky outcrops and seasonal streambeds provide the cobble and boulder sources required for large flake production.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Early Pleistocene conditions under oscillating glacial-interglacial cycles (MIS 37–27); monsoonal rainfall varies interannually but sustains seasonal watercourses; no secular drift dominates the window; volatility is moderate and well within hominin adaptive range.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seismic activity along the peninsula is minimal; no volcanic hazard; flood pulses along seasonal channels are predictable and localized; drought risk exists during weak monsoon years but vast territory permits relocation; margin against acute shock is wide.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Abundant quartzite and siliceous limestone cobbles and boulders in riverine and colluvial contexts; no evidence of drawdown — stock is effectively inexhaustible at prevailing extraction rates; flora and fauna sufficient for subsistence.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Negligible waste output; knapping debris disperses across open terrain with no detectable accumulation; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited.<br><br>
