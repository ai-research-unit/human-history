# __800000 BCE – 400000 BCE__ — Acheulean Quarry-Workshops of the Karnataka Plateau
__Summary:__ The Hunsgi-Baichbal landscape in northern Karnataka with over 200 documented Acheulean sites including the Isampur quarry-workshop (ESR dated 730 +/- 100 ka); a window into the complete chaîne opératoire from raw material extraction to finished handaxe; seasonal mobility patterns organized around perennial seep springs; the binding constraint is access to quality siliceous limestone at quarry outcrops; margin against environmental shock remains high with multiple water sources and alternative quarry locations; directional change shows stable technological tradition with incremental refinement over 400,000 years.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The northern Karnataka plateau presents a semi-arid peneplain at 400–600 m elevation; the Hunsgi and Baichbal valleys cut through Deccan basalt and limestone formations; spatial friction is low across open grassland and scrub; siliceous limestone outcrops provide concentrated raw material nodes that anchor site distribution.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Semi-arid conditions with seasonal monsoon rainfall; interannual variability in monsoon strength affects surface water availability; no dominant secular drift across the window; multiple glacial-interglacial cycles (MIS 19–12) impose background oscillation but perennial springs buffer local conditions.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Seismic risk minimal on the stable Deccan craton; no volcanic hazard; drought is the primary risk during weak monsoon years; perennial seep springs at valley bottoms provide refugia; margin against acute shock is adequate given multiple fallback locations.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Siliceous limestone of excellent flaking quality outcrops at Isampur and comparable localities; stock is effectively inexhaustible at prevailing extraction rates; fauna includes medium and large mammals; perennial springs ensure year-round water access at key points.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Knapping debris accumulates at workshop sites — over 15,000 artifacts at Isampur alone — but dispersal across the open landscape is unconstrained; absorptive capacity remains unlimited; no sink saturation.<br><br>
