# __385000 BCE – 170000 BCE__ — The Acheulean-to-Middle Paleolithic Transition
__Summary:__ A multi-generational technological transformation documented at Attirampakkam: biface frequency declines from dominance to under ten percent, handaxe mean dimensions shrink roughly forty percent, Levallois prepared-core knapping emerges, and blade production increases — a shift contemporaneous with African MSA and European transitions, completed by 172 ± 41 ka; the binding constraint is the cumulative precision of motor-skill transmission across generations without language, operating through hand-guiding-hand demonstration; margin against disruption rests on overlapping generations preserving technique in living bodies; trajectory is unidirectional toward smaller, more portable, more standardized tool kits.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Kortallayar River basin in the northeastern Tamil Nadu coastal plain, ~60 km northwest of present-day Chennai; low-gradient terrain with laterite and granite exposures providing raw material at moderate transit costs.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ The window spans multiple glacial-interglacial cycles (MIS 11 through MIS 6); interglacials brought enhanced Indian Summer Monsoon and expanded vegetation, glacials reduced rainfall and tree cover; secular drift moves toward the MIS 6 glacial maximum at window close.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Low seismic and volcanic exposure; principal hazard is prolonged drought during glacial phases; margin adequate given mobility and low population density.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Laterite and quartzite cobbles abundant; no drawdown pressure on lithic stocks at this extraction intensity; perennial and seasonal streams supply water.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Negligible knapping debris across an open landscape; effectively unlimited absorptive capacity for inorganic refuse at this population scale.<br><br>

__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle and controlled fire; fire use presumed but not directly attested at Attirampakkam; per-capita work ceiling constrained to somatic energy output.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Egalitarian task-sharing within small bands; no coercive hierarchy detectable; specialization limited to age and strength gradients.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Production is immediate-return; no surplus accumulation beyond single-day provisioning; handaxe manufacture represents low overhead relative to foraging time.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ No durable storage technology; reserves are embodied fat and cached knowledge of fallback resources; buffering margin narrow.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Raw material transported up to tens of kilometers from source to discard location; no evidence of inter-band exchange networks; logistics confined to individual mobility range.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No monetary or proto-monetary medium; reciprocal obligations within bands ("share now, owe later") carry all deferred exchange; obligations reset within short cycles.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ Band-scale sharing of large kills distributes windfall gains; no extraction hierarchy above immediate consumption pooling.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Portable tool kits the only durable asset; wealth concentration negligible; no defense strategies required beyond personal retention.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Knapping debris and organic refuse dispersed across open landscape; no routing decision needed; waste volume trivial relative to sink.<br><br>
