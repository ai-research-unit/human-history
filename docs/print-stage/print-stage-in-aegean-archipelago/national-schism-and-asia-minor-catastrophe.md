# __1913 CE – 1922 CE__ — National Schism and Asia Minor Catastrophe
__Summary:__ Greece enters its most convulsive decade: the National Schism (Ethnikos Dichasmos) splits the kingdom between Venizelist republicans and royalist conservatives over World War I alignment, producing parallel governments and purges; postwar Greek forces overextend into Anatolia under the Treaty of Sevres, only to collapse before Kemalist armies in 1922, ending with the destruction of Smyrna and the flight of over a million Orthodox refugees — the binding constraint is political fragmentation preventing coherent war strategy, and the system exits shattered, its irredentist project terminated.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The Greek state now spans peninsula, Aegean islands, Crete, and newly acquired Thessaloniki-Macedonia; the Anatolian campaign extends Greek forces across the Aegean into roadless Anatolian interior, logistically overextending a small power.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Mediterranean climate stable; Anatolian plateau interior harsher in winter; seasonal campaigning constraints shape military timetables.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ No major natural disasters; principal hazards are wartime fires and urban destruction, culminating in the Smyrna conflagration of September 1922.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Greece lacks coal and iron; dependent on British coal imports; Anatolian campaign strains already thin resource base; petroleum negligible.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Urban concentration in Athens and Piraeus increases sanitary strain; Thessaloniki absorbs military traffic; Smyrna's destruction leaves environmental and humanitarian wreckage on Turkish soil.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ War mortality compounds endemic disease; typhus, malaria, and dysentery afflict troops and refugees; Spanish influenza (1918-1919) adds excess deaths.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Wartime rationing, blockades, and displacement degrade nutrition; soldiers on extended Anatolian campaign suffer supply shortages; refugee columns in 1922 arrive malnourished.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Prolonged mobilization separates families; wartime marriages delayed; refugee trauma disrupts household formation.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population doubled by Balkan Wars gains; war casualties and refugee flight alter composition; 1922 exodus removes Greek populations from Asia Minor permanently.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ War widows, orphans, and wounded accumulate; 1922 catastrophe generates massive dependent refugee population overwhelming absorptive capacity.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Agricultural output disrupted by mobilization; Macedonia's grain helps but distribution uneven; refugee influx in 1922 overwhelms provisioning.<br><br>
__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Steam and animal power dominate; coal imports vulnerable to wartime disruption; army logistics depend on limited rail and animal transport.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Mass conscription removes male labor from farms and workshops; women and elderly substitute; demobilization after 1922 releases embittered veterans.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Industrial base remains small; wartime demand strains capacity; postwar contraction as military orders cease and refugees arrive.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ State reserves exhausted by decade of warfare; treasury dependent on Allied subsidies during war, on loans after; 1922 collapse finds reserves depleted.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Allied blockade during schism period disrupts trade; postwar commerce resumes but refugee crisis absorbs capital; Smyrna's loss removes major commercial hub.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ Drachma under pressure; inflation during war years; postwar instability continues; foreign debt accumulates.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ State extracts through taxation and conscription; war expenditure absorbs all surplus; postwar reconstruction demands exceed revenue.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ War profiteers coexist with impoverished veterans; 1922 refugees arrive destitute, widening inequality; land reform pressures rise.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Military waste, abandoned equipment, and urban rubble accumulate; sanitary infrastructure strained; Smyrna's ruins left to Turkish administration.<br><br>
## __P04. Social Environment__
__Social Trust & Mutual Aid:__ National Schism poisons social trust; families divide along Venizelist-royalist lines; purges and counter-purges create lasting vendettas.<br><br>
__Stratification, Status & Group Identity:__ Pre-existing class divisions overlay new political cleavage; military officers gain prominence; 1922 refugees form distinct social stratum.<br><br>
__Kinship, Family & Inheritance:__ War deaths and refugee displacement disrupt kinship networks; Asia Minor families arrive severed from ancestral property and kin left behind.<br><br>
__Civic Association & Self-Organization:__ Political clubs, veterans' associations, and refugee committees proliferate; civil society fragmented along schism lines.<br><br>
__Leisure, Play & Sociability:__ Public festivities reflect political division; coffeehouses become factional meeting points; refugee arrival brings Anatolian musical traditions.<br><br>
__Grievance Channels & Social Discharge:__ Political violence, street clashes, and assassination (King George I, 1913; politicians later) indicate grievance exceeding discharge capacity; schism wounds unhealed.<br><br>
## __P05. Political Environment__
__Power Structure & Authority:__ Constitutional monarchy in crisis; King Constantine and Prime Minister Venizelos represent opposed visions; parallel governments 1916-1917; Constantine exiled twice (1917, 1922).<br><br>
__Succession & Power Transfer:__ Royal succession contested; Constantine forced out, Alexander dies 1920, Constantine returns, abdicates 1922; Venizelos defeated 1920, returns after catastrophe; transfer mechanism broken.<br><br>
__Legitimacy & Political Narrative:__ Venizelists claim liberal reform and Allied victory; royalists claim national unity and neutrality; both narratives collapse in 1922 disaster, discrediting leadership.<br><br>
__Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity:__ Bureaucracy politicized by purges; military command divided; intelligence and logistics suffer from schism-era disruptions.<br><br>
__Political Participation & Mobilization:__ Expanded electorate; intense partisan mobilization through press and rallies; 1920 plebiscite returns Constantine; military intervenes repeatedly.<br><br>
__Geopolitical Position & External Relations:__ Greece enters World War I late (1917); gains at Sevres (1920) reversed at Lausanne (1923); Allied support conditional and withdrawn when Constantine returns; isolated during final Anatolian collapse.<br><br>
__Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force:__ Army expanded for Balkan Wars; further mobilization for Anatolia; equipment French-supplied; command fractured by schism; 1922 defeat triggers revolutionary committee and "Trial of the Six."<br><br>
__Internal Order & Threat Environment:__ Political assassination, military coups, and street violence mark the period; 1922 military revolt executes scapegoat politicians; public order precarious.<br><br>
## __P06. Informational Environment__
__Channels & Transmission Velocity:__ Telegraph and postal service operational; newspapers reach Athens same day; Anatolian front news delayed; censorship during war.<br><br>
__Record, Storage & Persistence:__ Government archives maintained despite schism disruptions; newspapers document controversy; Asia Minor records lost in 1922 destruction.<br><br>
__Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms:__ Press intensely partisan; Venizelist and royalist papers attack opponents; wartime censorship imposed and lifted; public discourse poisoned by schism rhetoric.<br><br>
__Reception, Attention & Amplification:__ Athenian public closely follows political drama; provincial and diaspora press relay positions; 1922 disaster dominates attention, crowding out nuance.<br><br>
__Signal Integrity & Noise Load:__ Partisan distortion high; rumor and propaganda obscure military realities; accurate information on Anatolian campaign scarce until collapse undeniable.<br><br>
## __P07. Judicial Environment__
__Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice:__ Public sense of justice polarized; each faction views the other as traitors; 1922 trials satisfy vengeance rather than legal process.<br><br>
__Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution:__ Legal framework nominally stable; emergency decrees during war; martial law periods; constitutional provisions suspended under crisis.<br><br>
__Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability:__ Courts function but politicized; extraordinary military tribunals after 1922 execute six royalist leaders (Trial of the Six) without adequate process.<br><br>
__Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden:__ Civil liberties suspended during emergencies; press freedom curtailed by both sides; political prisoners held by each regime.<br><br>
__Impunity & Unredressed Wrong:__ Schism-era purges and post-catastrophe executions create unredressed grievances; families of executed leaders harbor resentment for decades; justice perceived as victor's revenge.<br><br>
## __P08. Intellectual Environment__
__Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm:__ Academic institutions continue; University of Athens produces scholars; historiography intensely nationalist; science secondary to national crisis.<br><br>
__Applied Knowledge & Technical Method:__ Military technology imported; medical and engineering training continues; no major innovations; applied knowledge focused on war needs.<br><br>
__Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution:__ Education system expanding but disrupted by mobilization; literacy rising; katharevousa remains official language; demotic movement continues.<br><br>
__Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay:__ 1922 catastrophe destroys Greek institutional presence in Asia Minor; Smyrna's schools, libraries, and learned societies lost; knowledge stock contracts.<br><br>
## __P09. Cultural Environment__
__Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity:__ Megali Idea reaches apogee and collapses; the 1922 catastrophe becomes foundational trauma; "Asia Minor Hellenism" memorialized as lost paradise.<br><br>
__Artistic Expression & Material Culture:__ Literature and poetry engage war and schism; folk songs lament defeat; material culture disrupted by displacement; refugee objects become relics.<br><br>
__Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition:__ Public mood swings from war enthusiasm (1912-1913) to schism bitterness to catastrophe despair; 1922 trauma shapes collective psychology for generations.<br><br>
__Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay:__ Irredentist rhetoric exhausted by defeat; patriotic symbols tainted by failure; new meanings sought in refugee experience and republican experiment.<br><br>
## __P10. Spiritual Environment__
__Cosmology & Existential Outlook:__ Orthodox Christianity remains framework; catastrophe interpreted through religious lens as divine punishment or test; fatalism deepens.<br><br>
__Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice:__ Church services continue; refugee populations bring Anatolian liturgical traditions; saints' days observed amid displacement.<br><br>
__Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority:__ Church of Greece aligned with state; Ecumenical Patriarchate in Constantinople survives but weakened; clergy participate in national mobilization.<br><br>
__Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement:__ Population exchange will remove remaining Muslims from Greece and Christians from Turkey; window ends with homogenization in progress; Anatolian Orthodox traditions transplanted.<br><br>
__Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge:__ The catastrophe challenges providential national narratives; some disillusionment with institutional religion; spiritual needs channeled into refugee solidarity and mourning rituals.<br><br>
