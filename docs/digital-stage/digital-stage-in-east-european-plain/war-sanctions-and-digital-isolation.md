# __2022 CE – 2026 CE — War, Sanctions, and Digital Isolation__

__Summary:__ The Russian Federation under comprehensive Western sanctions following the February 2022 invasion of Ukraine, with the binding constraint being the rapid construction of a digitally isolated information sphere that severs remaining connections to global platforms while accelerating domestic surveillance and control infrastructure, producing a wartime digital autarky that approaches the long-theorized "sovereign internet" but at severe cost to economic capacity and informational openness.

## D01. Physical Domain

__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ The East European Plain's vast extent now works against Russia as sanctions sever western trade corridors while infrastructure targeting in Ukraine demonstrates vulnerability of concentrated digital nodes; the physical distance to Asian alternatives increases friction for rerouting commerce and data flows.

__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Climate conditions remain secondary to the conflict, though energy infrastructure attacks in Ukraine expose civilian populations to winter hardship; Russian territory experiences no direct climate-related digital disruption.

__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ The war itself constitutes the dominant hazard; Ukrainian infrastructure faces repeated missile and drone strikes targeting power grids and communications nodes; Russian territory experiences limited direct exposure beyond border regions.

__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Russia retains substantial energy reserves but faces constraints on technology imports for extraction; sanctions target the semiconductor and computing components essential for digital infrastructure maintenance and expansion; Ukraine loses access to occupied territory's resources while receiving Western equipment flows.

__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Physical environmental concerns recede behind wartime priorities; the destruction of industrial facilities in Ukraine creates localized contamination; Russian industrial output shifts toward military production with reduced environmental oversight.

## D02. Biological Domain

__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ COVID-19 pandemic consequences fade as wartime mortality dominates; Russian excess deaths from mobilization and combat remain opaque due to information control; Ukrainian civilian and military casualties documented more transparently through international monitoring.

__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Ukrainian populations in occupied and contested zones face humanitarian stress; Russian civilian nutrition remains stable though quality declines with import restrictions; refugee populations experience displacement-related health challenges.

__Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity:__ Ukrainian medical system adapts to mass casualty treatment with Western support; Russian military medical capacity strains under combat losses; digital health systems in both countries continue operating though under pressure.

__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Wartime separation disrupts family formation in both countries; Russian emigration skews young and male among IT professionals; Ukrainian refugee flows predominantly female and child-heavy.

__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Russia experiences emigration of perhaps 500,000-1,000,000 educated professionals in 2022 alone, heavily concentrated in technology sector; Ukraine's population drops from 44 million to approximately 37 million through emigration, displacement, and occupation.

__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ War casualties create immediate and long-term disability burdens; both states face caring for wounded veterans while productive cohorts are depleted by death, emigration, and mobilization.

## D03. Economic Domain

__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Basic provisioning in Russia continues despite sanctions through domestic production and Asian imports; Ukraine maintains food security through international support despite agricultural disruption in occupied zones; both populations experience inflation pressure.

__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Russia's energy sector faces technology sanctions limiting future extraction capacity while current production continues; Ukraine suffers systematic infrastructure attacks requiring constant repair; Starlink satellite internet becomes critical energy-independent communications backup.

__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Russian IT sector experiences mass voluntary emigration alongside coercive retention efforts; partial mobilization pulls workers from civilian economy; Ukrainian economy adapts to wartime conditions with remote work where possible.

__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Russian economy shifts toward military production; sanctions limit access to advanced manufacturing inputs; Ukrainian industrial capacity in eastern regions destroyed or occupied; Western support substitutes for domestic production shortfalls.

__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ Russia's sovereign wealth fund and gold reserves provide buffer against sanctions; frozen Western assets create bilateral standoff; Ukraine depends on continuous Western financial support for state function.

__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ SWIFT disconnection for major Russian banks, though not universal; parallel import schemes develop through third countries; cryptocurrency adoption increases for circumventing restrictions; Ukraine maintains Western trade corridors through Poland.

__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ Ruble volatility in 2022 stabilizes through capital controls; Russian population experiences reduced access to international payment systems; cryptocurrency and alternative payment mechanisms expand.

__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ Russian state finances shift to war economy; oil and gas revenues redirected to military; Ukraine's budget substantially funded by Western aid; taxation capacity disrupted in both countries.

__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Russian oligarch assets frozen abroad; domestic wealth redistribution through inflation; Ukrainian refugees lose property in occupied zones; international asset seizure debates continue.

__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Economic waste concerns subordinated to military priorities; sanctions create supply chain inefficiencies generating unusual waste patterns; financial malinvestment in war production accumulates.

## D04. Social Domain

__Kinship, Family & Inheritance:__ War separates families across borders; Russian families divided by emigration and mobilization; Ukrainian families scattered across Europe as refugees; cross-border family communication depends heavily on Telegram and other digital platforms.

__Association & Mutual Aid:__ Russian civil society organizations designated "foreign agents" or disbanded; Ukrainian civil society mobilizes for defense with international support; diaspora communities in both cases form new associational networks abroad.

__Leisure, Play & Sociability:__ Russian access to Western entertainment platforms severed; domestic alternatives insufficient to replace lost content; Ukrainian cultural life disrupted but maintains international connections; online gaming communities fragment along political lines.

__Stratification, Status & Group Structure:__ Russian IT professionals gain status through essential war-avoidance leverage or emigration; Ukrainian tech workers maintain international employment remotely; new social division between those who left and those who stayed in both countries.

__Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity:__ Russian state demonstrates administrative capacity for sanctions evasion and military mobilization; Ukrainian state digitizes governance rapidly through Diia app for wartime adaptation; corruption concerns persist in both systems under wartime pressures.

__Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force:__ Russian security services expand domestic surveillance and enforcement against dissent; Ukrainian forces defend territory with increasing Western equipment integration; digital targeting and drone coordination become central to military operations.

__Discharge, Disorder & Decay:__ Russian society experiences suppressed dissent rather than discharged grievance; Ukrainian society channels disruption into collective defense; both populations accumulate unprocessed trauma; the Wagner mutiny of June 2023 reveals fissures in Russian coercive apparatus briefly before being contained.

## D05. Emotional Domain

__Leadership & Personal Authority:__ Putin's authority maintained through repression and rally-around-flag dynamics; Zelensky transforms from comedian-president to wartime leader with international recognition; both leaders' fates tied to war outcome.

__Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance:__ Russian state legitimacy among opposition destroyed; conformist majority accepts or supports state narrative; Ukrainian state legitimacy strengthened through defense; international legitimacy flows to Kyiv.

__Political Will & Collective Resolve:__ Russian will to sustain prolonged war tested by economic strain and casualties; Ukrainian will to resist demonstrated through multiple defensive successes; Western will to support fluctuates with political cycles.

__External Relations & Posture:__ Russia isolated from Western powers, pivots toward China, Iran, North Korea; Ukraine deepens Western integration with EU candidate status; neutral states face pressure to choose sides.

__Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper:__ Russian public sentiment difficult to measure under repression; apparent majority support for war or resigned acceptance; Ukrainian national unity and morale remain high despite casualties; international sympathy flows to Ukraine.

__Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will:__ Russian opposition either emigrated, imprisoned, or silenced; Navalny's death in February 2024 removes remaining symbolic opposition figure; Ukrainian war fatigue emerges by 2025 but does not overcome defense imperative.


## D06. Informational Domain

__Communication Accessibility:__ Russian internet remains technically accessible but Western platforms blocked; VPN usage widespread for circumvention; Ukrainian communications disrupted by infrastructure attacks but Starlink provides resilient backup; Telegram functions as common platform across the divide.

__Channels & Transmission Velocity:__ Russian state controls television and major online platforms; independent channels operate through exile media accessible via VPN; Ukrainian channels maintain international reach; information warfare operates at high velocity on both sides.

__Record, Storage & Persistence:__ Russian state controls domestic narrative record; international fact-checking and OSINT preserve alternative record; Ukrainian documentation of war crimes compiled for international accountability; satellite imagery provides persistent documentation.

__Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms:__ Russia criminalizes terms "war" and "invasion," requiring "special military operation"; platforms blocked include Facebook, Instagram, and by 2024 YouTube; Ukraine maintains open discourse with wartime security restrictions; information framing becomes explicit war objective.

__Reception, Attention & Amplification:__ Russian propaganda amplified through domestic platforms; Ukrainian narrative dominates Western media; "milblogger" phenomenon on Telegram creates semi-autonomous Russian war commentary; international attention to Ukraine fluctuates with news cycle.

__Signal Integrity & Noise Load:__ Disinformation floods information space from both sides; verification challenges acute; deep fake concerns emerge; the sheer volume of information overwhelms individual processing capacity.

## D07. Moral Domain

__Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice:__ Fundamental moral rupture between Russian and Ukrainian/Western conceptions of the war's legitimacy; Russian narrative frames defensive "denazification" against Western narrative of unprovoked aggression; irreconcilable framing prevents negotiated settlement.

__Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution:__ Russian law expands criminal penalties for dissent and "discrediting" armed forces; Ukraine operates under martial law; international law invoked by both sides with selective application; ICC issues Putin arrest warrant in 2023.

__Adjudication, Liability & Sanction:__ International courts proceed against Russia in absentia; domestic Russian courts adjudicate political cases with predetermined outcomes; Ukrainian courts document Russian violations for eventual accountability.

__Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden:__ Russian citizens face severe restrictions on expression and assembly; digital surveillance enables enforcement; Ukrainian civilians in occupied zones experience rights violations; refugee populations navigate complex international protection regimes.

__Impunity & Unredressed Wrong:__ Russian state enjoys domestic impunity; international accountability limited by lack of enforcement mechanism; Ukrainian victims accumulate claims unlikely to see redress in near term; justice gap widens.

## D08. Intellectual Domain

__Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm:__ Russian academic institutions isolated from international collaboration; brain drain accelerates as researchers emigrate; Ukrainian research disrupted but maintains international connections; military technology development prioritized in both countries.

__Applied Knowledge & Technical Method:__ Russia demonstrates technical capacity in some military domains while showing gaps in others; sanctions limit access to advanced components; Ukraine integrates Western technology rapidly; drone and electronic warfare innovation accelerates on both sides.

__Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution:__ Russian IT education continues but graduates increasingly emigrate; Ukrainian tech sector operates remotely with international clients; wartime conditions disrupt formal education; technical skills remain concentrated in emigrated populations.

__Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay:__ Russian isolation risks technological stagnation over time; dependence on Chinese technology creates new vulnerabilities; accumulated Soviet/Russian technical knowledge relevant to current systems but not advancing; knowledge outflow through emigration not replaced.

## D09. Cultural Domain

__Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity:__ Russian war narrative invokes Great Patriotic War mythology; "Nazi" framing connects 2022 to 1941; Ukrainian identity consolidated through resistance; shared Soviet heritage becomes site of contestation rather than connection.

__Legitimating Narrative & Ideology:__ Russian state promotes multipolar anti-Western narrative; Ukraine frames defense as European civilization defending against authoritarianism; competing historical claims to territory and peoplehood irreconcilable.

__Artistic Expression & Material Culture:__ Russian cultural institutions face international boycott; artists forced to declare positions; Ukrainian culture receives unprecedented international attention and support; war documentation becomes major cultural production.

__Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay:__ Russian patriotic symbolism intensifies but risks hollowing through overuse; Soviet iconography revived but detached from lived experience; Ukrainian national symbols acquire renewed potency through collective sacrifice.

## D10. Spiritual Domain

__Cosmology & Existential Outlook:__ Russian Orthodox Church provides spiritual backing for war; Patriarch Kirill frames conflict in civilizational terms; Ukrainian Orthodox Church splits from Moscow Patriarchate; secular existential stakes dominate public discourse.

__Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice:__ Russian church services incorporate war blessing; Ukrainian churches serve as community centers and shelters; religious practice continues on both sides with intensified significance.

__Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority:__ Russian Orthodox Church hierarchy aligned with state; Ukrainian autocephalous church asserts independence; property disputes between church jurisdictions; clergy face persecution in occupied territories.

__Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement:__ Religious geography shifts with population displacement; diaspora communities establish congregations abroad; ecumenical tensions between Orthodox jurisdictions; some displacement of religious practice by secular nationalism.

__Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge:__ War creates both intensified religiosity and profound disillusionment; the scale of suffering tests theodicy; younger generations in both countries show mixed religious engagement; the sacred mobilized for war purposes risks post-war exhaustion.
