# AI Research on Human History

A large-scale history of the human past, written by AI and organized around a single
idea: the shared, institutional life of any society -- its administration, economy,
law, and public communication -- runs on some primary medium for storing information
across time, sending it across space, copying it, and finding it again. That medium
has changed only a handful of times, and each change reorganized everything built
on top of it.

The aim is a coherent, large-scale, internally consistent view of the human past --
a way of seeing the whole shape at once -- rather than original archival scholarship.

This project traces that sequence of media and, within it, describes the past through
one repeatable lens.

## How the guide is organized

Three nested layers, from the largest frame down to the individual article:

1. The Information Sequence -- seven stages, each named for the substrate that carried
   public life in turn: imitation, speech, manuscript, print, broadcast, digital,
   and a projected AI stage.

2. Regions -- within each stage, the places where that substrate became the dominant
   medium. Regions are named for enduring physical features (a basin, rift, coast,
   or shelf) rather than polities, so the same container reads correctly in deep
   prehistory and today, regardless of which culture or state occupied it. Each
   region reaches a stage on its own schedule; that lag is shown, not hidden.

3. Articles -- within each region, individual Historical Contexts: a state at rest,
   a rapid turning point, or a long structural trend, every one mapped across the
   same ten domains so that any two are directly comparable.

## The seven stages

The sequence runs from the substrate that carried public life before language to one
just emerging. A stage is a substrate, not a span of time: the same stage opens at
very different dates in different places, and a region can remain in an early stage
long after others have moved on. A stage is dated from the institutions that ran on
it -- so a manuscript period can be named even while most people were still illiterate.

1. [Imitative Stage](imitative-stage/index.md) -- knowledge held in living memory,
   passed by showing and copying, before language.
2. [Oral Stage](oral-stage/index.md) -- spoken language as the primary medium; the
   absent, the past, and the abstract become sayable, but everything still rides on
   breath and memory.
3. [Manuscript Stage](manuscript-stage/index.md) -- external records kept outside
   the body; a record now outlasts its maker.
4. [Print Stage](print-stage/index.md) -- mechanically reproduced, identical copies
   carry the public sphere.
5. [Broadcast Stage](broadcast-stage/index.md) -- one source reaches a multitude
   in the same instant.
6. [Digital Stage](digital-stage/index.md) -- every kind of information reduced to
   one common code, networked so any point can reach any other.
7. AI Stage (projected) -- retrieval by meaning, in ordinary language; an emerging
   candidate, not yet dominant.
