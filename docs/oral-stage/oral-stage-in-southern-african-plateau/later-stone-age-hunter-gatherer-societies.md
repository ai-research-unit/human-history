# __20000 BCE – 2000 BCE__ — Later Stone Age Hunter-Gatherer Societies
__Summary:__ An eighteen-thousand-year trend spanning the Last Glacial Maximum, post-glacial warming, and Holocene stabilization across the southern African plateau; San ancestors consolidate a stable foraging lifeway with microlithic technology (Wilton tradition), bow-and-arrow hunting, and seasonal mobility; rock art reaches florescence as a medium for ritual, trance, and cosmological expression; binding constraint is the carrying capacity of patchy environments and the need for inter-band cooperation to buffer risk; margin against shock adequate in normal years but thin against prolonged drought; direction of change is demographic expansion and cultural elaboration as climate ameliorates, then stabilization through mid-Holocene.
## __P01. Physical Environment__
__Geography & Spatial Friction:__ Elevated plateau interior (highveld, Kalahari fringe, Drakensberg foothills) with seasonal pans, ephemeral rivers, and permanent springs as critical nodes; escarpment zones provide shelter and reliable water; spatial friction low for mobile bands, higher during dry season when water concentrates movement.<br><br>
__Climate Regime & Volatility:__ Last Glacial Maximum (~20000–15000 BCE) imposes cold, arid conditions; post-glacial warming accelerates after ~15000 BCE; Holocene (after ~10000 BCE) brings warmer, wetter, more stable climate; interannual variability declines through mid-Holocene; overall trend ameliorating.<br><br>
__Hazard Regime & Exposure:__ Drought remains the principal hazard, acute during LGM, diminishing into Holocene; frost exposure declines with warming; wildfire regime intensifies as vegetation recovers; flooding episodic along major rivers; margin against shock widening as climate stabilizes.<br><br>
__Resource & Energy Endowment:__ Toolstone (silcrete, chalcedony, quartz) abundant; ochre and ostrich eggshell available for pigment and beadwork; large and small game populations recover post-LGM; plant foods (tubers, fruits, seeds) increasingly reliable; overall endowment expanding with Holocene productivity.<br><br>
__Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity:__ Anthropogenic waste load negligible; lithic debitage, ash, and organic refuse dispersed across landscape; rock shelters accumulate stratified deposits but absorptive capacity effectively unlimited at population scale.<br><br>
## __P02. Biological Environment__
__Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline:__ Endemic parasites, gastrointestinal infections, and wound sepsis; no epidemic disease signature; mortality baseline driven by infant mortality, trauma, and occasional starvation; direction improving as nutritional stability increases through Holocene.<br><br>
__Nutrition & Physiological Vitality:__ Broad-spectrum diet (game, fish, shellfish, tubers, fruits) ensures adequate nutrition in most years; stature and robustness within modern human range; physiological vitality stable to improving as climate ameliorates; seasonal stress persists but diminishes.<br><br>
__Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics:__ Pair-bonding with flexible residence; cooperative child-rearing within band; fertility constrained by extended lactation (birth spacing ~4 years ethnographically); selective pressure favoring cooperation, adaptability, and social intelligence.<br><br>
__Demographic Renewal & Density:__ Population density rises from LGM lows (under 0.01/km²) to perhaps 0.05–0.1/km² by mid-Holocene; birth-to-death ratio positive during expansion phase; genetic continuity with later Khoisan peoples confirmed; interior reoccupied as habitats recover.<br><br>
__Attrition & Dependency Overhang:__ Dependency burden absorbed within band kinship networks; lifespan extends slightly with improved nutrition; orphans and elderly supported by pooled resources; no dependency overhang exceeding absorptive capacity.<br><br>
## __P03. Economic Environment__
__Subsistence & Provisioning Floor:__ Hunting with bow and poisoned arrow (post ~10000 BCE); snaring and trapping small game; gathering tubers, mongongo nuts, fruits, and grass seeds; seasonal mobility follows resource pulses; provisioning floor secure in normal years, strained in drought.<br><br>
__Energy Capture & Conversion:__ Human muscle the sole prime mover; fire for cooking, warmth, and landscape burning to attract game; energy capture per capita stable and adequate for foraging lifeway; conversion efficiency unchanged within technological limits.<br><br>
__Labor Organization & Coercion:__ Division of labor by age and sex (men hunt large game, women gather and trap small game); cooperation structured by kinship and reciprocity; no coerced labor; specialization minimal beyond ritual and craft roles.<br><br>
__Production & Surplus Capacity:__ Production oriented toward immediate consumption; episodic surplus from successful hunts shared immediately; no systematic accumulation; surplus capacity intentionally limited by mobility and sharing norms.<br><br>
__Storage, Reserves & Buffering:__ Ostrich-eggshell water containers enable caching at strategic points; dried meat and plant foods stored for short periods; body fat and social reciprocity primary buffers; reserves adequate for normal seasonal stress, thin against multi-year drought.<br><br>
__Exchange, Trade & Logistics:__ Hxaro-type gift exchange networks link bands across hundreds of kilometers; toolstone, ostrich-eggshell beads, ochre, and medicinal plants circulate; logistics pedestrian but efficient along established routes; networks provide drought-refuge access.<br><br>
__Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange:__ No monetary medium; value stored in social relationships and deferred reciprocity; gifts create future claims; beadwork and personal ornaments carry social value but not monetary function.<br><br>
__Surplus Extraction & Redistribution:__ No extractive authority; large-kill proceeds redistributed through sharing norms; redistribution voluntary, immediate, and egalitarian; load on productive base negligible.<br><br>
__Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense:__ Near-egalitarian distribution; property limited to personal toolkits, ornaments, and knowledge; land access rights negotiated through kinship; defense of assets unnecessary beyond personal possession and reputation.<br><br>
__Byproducts & Waste Routing:__ Lithic debitage, bone refuse, and ash concentrated at campsites and rock shelters; organic waste decomposes; waste load trivial relative to absorptive capacity; archaeological deposits accumulate over millennia without environmental stress.<br><br>
## __P04. Social Environment__
__Social Trust & Mutual Aid:__ High interpersonal trust within band; trust radius extends through hxaro networks to allied bands; mutual aid activated during drought, providing refuge access; trust widening as exchange networks mature through Holocene.<br><br>
__Stratification, Status & Group Identity:__ Minimal stratification; status accrues to skilled hunters, healers, and elders; group identity marked by dialect (click-language variation), territory, and kinship; boundaries permeable through marriage and gift exchange.<br><br>
__Kinship, Family & Inheritance:__ Bilateral kinship reckoning; flexible band membership; inheritance limited to personal effects, names, and social standing; kinship structures labor, marriage rules, and access to exchange networks.<br><br>
__Civic Association & Self-Organization:__ Band the primary unit (20–80 persons); seasonal aggregation at water sources for ritual, mating, and exchange; inter-band councils resolve disputes and coordinate territory; no formal institutions above band level.<br><br>
__Leisure, Play & Sociability:__ Leisure abundant in foraging economy (ethnographically ~20 hours/week work); storytelling, music, trance dance, games, and visiting occupy non-work time; sociability reinforces bonds and transmits culture.<br><br>
__Grievance Channels & Social Discharge:__ Fission primary grievance channel; mobility allows disgruntled members to relocate; gossip, ridicule, and ritual sanction manage minor disputes; trance dance provides emotional discharge; residual grievance low.<br><br>
## __P05. Political Environment__
__Power Structure & Authority:__ Acephalous band organization; leadership situational (hunting, ritual, conflict) and achieved through skill and generosity; decisions by consensus; authority distributed and non-coercive; structure stable across millennia.<br><br>
__Succession & Power Transfer:__ No formal offices to transfer; leadership shifts with context and demonstrated competence; succession a non-issue in egalitarian structure.<br><br>
__Legitimacy & Political Narrative:__ Legitimacy rests on reciprocity, demonstrated competence, and adherence to sharing norms; no legitimizing ideology beyond oral tradition; authority sustained by ongoing performance and social approval.<br><br>
__Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity:__ No administrative apparatus; coordination through kinship, oral consensus, and shared memory; information gathered by direct observation; no records beyond human recall; integrity maintained by transparency of small-scale society.<br><br>
__Political Participation & Mobilization:__ All adult band members participate in decisions; mobilization for collective hunts, moves, and ceremonies; women's voices heard though gendered domains exist; no exclusion beyond age-graded learning.<br><br>
__Geopolitical Position & External Relations:__ Inter-band relations structured by kinship, alliance, and gift exchange; territorial boundaries flexible and negotiated; raiding and feuding attested ethnographically but low-level; external threats limited to predators and occasional inter-group violence.<br><br>
__Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force:__ No standing coercive apparatus; all adult males armed with bow and poisoned arrows; women carry digging sticks usable as weapons; command of force distributed equally; violence personal and infrequent.<br><br>
__Internal Order & Threat Environment:__ Internal order maintained by social sanction, gossip, and fission; homicide rate low but present; predator threat (lion, leopard) chronic; banditry and rebellion structurally impossible in acephalous society.<br><br>
## __P06. Informational Environment__
__Channels & Transmission Velocity:__ Face-to-face speech the sole channel; transmission velocity limited to walking pace and inter-band visiting; information circulates across regional networks within weeks; no broadcast medium.<br><br>
__Record, Storage & Persistence:__ Living memory and rock art the sole records; mnemonic structures (song cycles, place-naming, narrative) aid retention; rock paintings and engravings externalize cosmological and historical knowledge but remain interpretive, not phonetic; persistence dependent on continuous oral transmission and panel survival; no writing system; knowledge lost when transmission breaks or panels erode.<br><br>
__Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms:__ Framing shaped by elder authority and ritual context; sacred knowledge restricted to initiates; no formal censorship; discourse norms enforce respect, avoidance of certain names, and caution around dangerous topics; permitted band stable within tradition.<br><br>
__Reception, Attention & Amplification:__ Audiences small (band or aggregation); attention intensive during trance dance, storytelling, and ritual; amplification through repetition and inter-band diffusion; no virality beyond oral networks.<br><br>
__Signal Integrity & Noise Load:__ Signal integrity high in face-to-face transmission; variation introduced by retelling and regional drift; verification through elder authority and consensus; noise load manageable at band scale; rock art provides durable reference point for cosmological schema.<br><br>
## __P07. Judicial Environment__
__Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice:__ Justice grounded in reciprocity, harm avoidance, and kinship obligation; fairness intuitions center on sharing and proportional return; moral baseline widely shared and deeply internalized through enculturation.<br><br>
__Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution:__ No codified law; norms transmitted orally through narrative, proverb, and elder instruction; taboos govern sacred domains, incest, and dangerous behavior; norms evolve slowly through consensus and precedent.<br><br>
__Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability:__ Disputes resolved by informal mediation among kin and band elders; no third-party court; enforcement through social sanction, ridicule, ostracism, and ultimately expulsion; liability assigned by community opinion; throughput adequate for low caseload.<br><br>
__Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden:__ Individual autonomy high; rights implicit in band membership and reciprocity norms; compliance burden light given small-scale, face-to-face accountability; predictability high within known social matrix.<br><br>
__Impunity & Unredressed Wrong:__ Persistent wrongdoers face escalating sanction; impunity limited by close surveillance and gossip; unredressed wrong occasionally escalates to blood feud, but fission and inter-band mediation usually discharge tension; residue low.<br><br>
## __P08. Intellectual Environment__
__Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm:__ Empirical knowledge of animal behavior, plant phenology, weather patterns, and landscape embedded in practical tradition; explanatory paradigm animist and mythopoetic; no formal inquiry separate from subsistence and ritual; knowledge continuously tested against survival outcomes.<br><br>
__Applied Knowledge & Technical Method:__ Sophisticated microlithic technology (backed segments, crescents for composite tools); bow-and-arrow system with plant-derived poison (Diamphidia beetle larvae); hafting with mastic; trap and snare construction; fire management; methods transmitted through apprenticeship and demonstration.<br><br>
__Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution:__ Education informal, experiential, and embedded in daily activity; knowledge stock held in collective memory and rock art; skill distribution universal for core competencies, specialized for healing, tracking, and ritual expertise; stock expanding through Holocene as population grows.<br><br>
__Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay:__ Earlier MSA techniques largely shed; obsolete methods lost as new prove superior; decay risk present when transmission breaks (elder death, band dissolution); knowledge stock resilient given population continuity and inter-band exchange.<br><br>
## __P09. Cultural Environment__
__Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity:__ Foundational identity anchored in ancestral landscape, animal kinship, and origin narratives (Mantis, Eland, First People); collective memory transmitted orally and encoded in rock art; deep time reckoned mythically; identity continuous with later San peoples.<br><br>
__Artistic Expression & Material Culture:__ Rock art florescence: fine-line polychrome paintings in Drakensberg shelters depict trance states, therianthropes, eland hunts, and rain-making rituals; engravings on open rock surfaces; ostrich-eggshell beadwork; ochre body decoration; tortoise-shell containers; material culture portable, symbolically charged, and aesthetically refined.<br><br>
__Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition:__ Mood inferred from stable adaptive success; behavioral disposition cautious yet confident, risk-pooling through social networks; collective mood likely lifting through post-glacial amelioration; resilience evident in millennia-long cultural continuity.<br><br>
__Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay:__ No evidence of form exhaustion; rock art tradition remains vital and productive; symbolic repertoire continuously renewed through trance experience and ritual performance; meaning decay absent given living tradition.<br><br>
## __P10. Spiritual Environment__
__Cosmology & Existential Outlook:__ Animist cosmology: spirit world interpenetrates material world; ancestors, animal spirits, and potency (/n|om, n/um) pervade landscape; existential outlook integrates humans into cosmic order; death a transition, not termination; fatalism balanced by ritual agency.<br><br>
__Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice:__ Trance dance the central ritual: healers enter altered states, harness potency, heal the sick, make rain, and mediate with spirits; initiation rites, first-kill ceremonies, menarcheal seclusion; sacred practice woven into seasonal and life-cycle rhythms.<br><br>
__Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority:__ No formal religious institution; ritual specialists (healers, n/om-kxaosi) hold knowledge through personal experience and apprenticeship; authority achieved, not inherited; doctrine orally transmitted and locally variable; no hierarchy.<br><br>
__Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement:__ Spiritual beliefs diffuse through inter-band contact, marriage, and exchange; regional variation in detail but shared cosmological substrate; no displacement by external belief systems until pastoral/farmer contact after 2000 BCE; direction stable within autochthonous tradition.<br><br>
__Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge:__ No evidence of disenchantment; sacred canopy intact and experientially renewed through trance; spiritual need fully anchored; existential void absent; sacred discharge integrated into healing and ritual performance; equilibrium maintained.<br><br>
