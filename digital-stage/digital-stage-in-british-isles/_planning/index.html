    Planning Document — Digital Stage in The British Isles (2010 CE – 2026 CE)

    Age Definition

    Digital Stage: Every kind of information is reduced to one common code, the same underneath whatever it began as — page, song, and photograph become the same kind of thing, stored, moved, and reshaped without loss. Public information life comes to run on this unified substrate, and through its networked, many-to-many form any point can reach any other: everyone can send to everyone, with no single source at the centre. The current dominant substrate.

    Window: 2010 CE – 2026 CE

    Region: The British Isles — England, Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland, and the Republic of Ireland; from smartphone penetration through social media dominance, Brexit information politics, COVID digital transformation, and AI emergence.

    Note on Evidence: Documentary record includes Ofcom reports, platform data, parliamentary inquiries, media studies, and journalism archives. Smartphone mass adoption 2010s; social media maturation; Brexit 2016 and information warfare; COVID-19 digital acceleration; Online Safety Bill; AI emergence 2022-2026. Window begins with smartphone saturation; continues to present.
