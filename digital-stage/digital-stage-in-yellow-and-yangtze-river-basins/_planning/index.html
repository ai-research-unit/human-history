    Planning Document — Digital Stage in The Yellow and Yangtze River Basins (2012 CE – 2026 CE)

    Age Definition

    Digital Stage: Every kind of information is reduced to one common code, the same underneath whatever it began as — page, song, and photograph become the same kind of thing, stored, moved, and reshaped without loss. Public information life comes to run on this unified substrate, and through its networked, many-to-many form any point can reach any other: everyone can send to everyone, with no single source at the centre. The current dominant substrate.

    Window: 2012 CE – 2026 CE

    Region: The Yellow and Yangtze River Basins — China; the world's largest walled-garden digital ecosystem, from mobile payment revolution through surveillance state expansion, tech crackdown, and AI race with the United States.

    Note on Evidence: Documentary record includes platform data, government policy documents, tech company filings, and journalism. Xi consolidation from 2012; WeChat dominance; mobile payments; Great Firewall; social credit; tech IPOs; 2020-2021 tech crackdown; zero-COVID; AI competition; Xinjiang surveillance. Window begins with Xi era; continues to present.

    Shared Evidence Base

    1. Mobile Internet Explosion (2012–2017 CE)

    Smartphone Adoption: Rapid penetration; Xiaomi, Huawei rise.

    WeChat Launch: 2011; messaging to platform; everything app.

    Alibaba IPO: 2014; largest tech IPO; e-commerce dominance.

    Mobile Payments: Alipay vs WeChat Pay; cashless acceleration.

    Platform Ecosystem: BAT (Baidu, Alibaba, Tencent) dominance.

    4G Rollout: Infrastructure expansion; mobile-first users.

    2. Surveillance Expansion (2017–2021 CE)

    Social Credit: Pilot systems; behavior scoring; blacklists.

    Facial Recognition: Skynet; Sharp Eyes; ubiquitous cameras.

    Xinjiang Model: Mass surveillance; detention; tech-enabled repression.

    Tech IPOs: Ant Financial planned IPO; valuations peak.

    COVID Response: Health codes; contact tracing; zero-COVID.

    Hong Kong: 2019 protests; national security law; digital crackdown.

    3. Tech Crackdown (2021–2026 CE)

    Ant IPO Cancellation: November 2020; regulatory intervention.

    Platform Regulation: Antitrust; data security; algorithm rules.

    Gaming Restrictions: Youth limits; license freeze; industry impact.

    Education Tech: Tutoring ban; sector destruction.

    US Decoupling: Chip restrictions; entity lists; tech war.

    AI Competition: ChatGPT response; domestic models; compute constraints.

    4. WeChat Ecosystem (2012–2026 CE)

    Super-App Model: Messaging; payments; mini-programs; services.

    User Scale: 1.3+ billion monthly active users.

    Daily Life Integration: Essential for commerce, communication, services.

    Official Accounts: Business; media; government communication.

    Censorship: Content monitoring; account deletions; self-censorship.

    International Limitations: Blocked abroad; diaspora use persists.

    5. Alibaba E-Commerce (2012–2026 CE)

    Taobao/Tmall: E-commerce platforms; merchant ecosystem.

    Singles Day: November 11; shopping festival; record sales.

    Ant Financial: Alipay; Yu'e Bao; lending; wealth management.

    Jack Ma: Entrepreneur icon; Bund speech 2020; disappearance.

    Regulatory Action: Antitrust fine; restructuring; humbling.

    Competitor Rise: Pinduoduo; JD.com; market fragmentation.

    6. Great Firewall (2012–2026 CE)

    Blocking: Google; Facebook; Twitter; Wikipedia; thousands of sites.

    VPN Crackdowns: Periodic enforcement; corporate vs individual.

    Domestic Alternatives: Baidu; Weibo; Youku; self-sufficient ecosystem.

    Information Control: Censorship; propaganda; narrative management.

    Technical Sophistication: Deep packet inspection; AI detection.

    Leakage: Academic; business; persistent circumvention.

    7. Surveillance State (2012–2026 CE)

    Facial Recognition: Megvii; SenseTime; ubiquitous deployment.

    Social Credit: Municipal pilots; blacklists; reward/punishment.

    Xinjiang: Uyghur surveillance; predictive policing; detention.

    COVID Infrastructure: Health codes; tracking; repurposing.

    Smart Cities: Integrated monitoring; data fusion.

    Export: Surveillance technology; Global South markets.

    8. Mobile Payments (2012–2026 CE)

    Alipay: E-commerce origin; financial services expansion.

    WeChat Pay: Social integration; QR code; merchant ubiquity.

    Cashless Dominance: Urban areas nearly cash-free.

    Financial Inclusion: Rural access; elderly challenges.

    Data Collection: Transaction surveillance; behavioral profiles.

    Regulatory Control: Reserve requirements; interoperability mandates.

    9. Douyin TikTok (2012–2026 CE)

    ByteDance: Founding 2012; algorithm-driven content.

    Douyin: Chinese version; short video dominance.

    TikTok: International version; global phenomenon.

    Algorithm Power: Recommendation engine; engagement optimization.

    US Battles: National security concerns; potential ban.

    Content Ecosystem: Creator economy; e-commerce integration.

    10. Tech Worker Culture (2012–2026 CE)

    996 Culture: 9am-9pm, 6 days; normalized overwork.

    Burnout: Health impacts; sudden deaths; public attention.

    Jack Ma Defense: "Blessing" comment; backlash.

    Lying Flat: Tangping; youth rejection; consumption retreat.

    Labor Law: Theoretical protections; enforcement gaps.

    Post-Crackdown: Reduced hours; industry contraction; layoffs.

    11. AI Development (2012–2026 CE)

    Research Output: Papers; talent; university programs.

    Applications: Facial recognition; natural language; autonomous vehicles.

    National Strategy: 2017 AI plan; strategic priority.

    US Chip Restrictions: 2022 onwards; compute limitations.

    Domestic Response: SMIC; chip self-sufficiency; limited progress.

    LLM Race: Baidu Ernie; Alibaba Tongyi; catching up.

    12. Gaming Industry (2012–2026 CE)

    Tencent Dominance: Honor of Kings; League of Legends; global investments.

    Youth Restrictions: 2021 limits; 3 hours weekly; industry impact.

    License Freeze: 2021-2022; regulatory uncertainty.

    Esports: Professional leagues; Olympics consideration.

    Global Expansion: Acquisitions; publishing; studios.

    Cultural Concerns: Addiction; spiritual opium; moral panic.

    13. Digital Yuan (2012–2026 CE)

    CBDC Development: Digital currency electronic payment (DCEP).

    Pilot Programs: Shenzhen; Suzhou; Beijing Olympics 2022.

    Adoption: Slow uptake; platform competition.

    Financial Control: Programmable money; surveillance capability.

    Dollar Challenge: International ambitions; limited progress.

    Technical Innovation: Offline capability; smart contracts.
