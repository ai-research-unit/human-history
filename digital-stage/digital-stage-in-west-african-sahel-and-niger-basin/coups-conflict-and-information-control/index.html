    2022 CE – 2026 CE — Coups, Conflict, and Information Control

    Summary: The West African Sahel and Niger Basin experience a cascade of military coups, intensifying jihadist insurgency, and systematic information control across a four-year window marked by French military withdrawal and Russian influence expansion; the binding constraint is the collapse of civilian governance and constitutional order across the Sahel belt, with digital infrastructure weaponized for regime consolidation; the system holds almost no margin against further state fragmentation and external manipulation; the direction is toward authoritarian entrenchment, information closure, and geopolitical realignment away from Western partnerships.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Sahel corridor from Mali through Niger to Burkina Faso remains characterized by vast, sparsely governed territory where state reach attenuates rapidly beyond capitals; fiber backbones remain coastal, leaving interior connectivity dependent on satellite and microwave links vulnerable to physical disruption and state control.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Rainfall variability intensifies; Lake Chad continues shrinking; drought cycles displace agropastoral populations southward, compounding conflict drivers and straining the resource base of states already at governance limits.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Flooding in the Niger inland delta and Sahel's seasonal watercourses disrupts infrastructure and displaces populations; drought and locust pressure persist; physical hazards compound the conflict-induced humanitarian crisis without themselves being the primary destabilizer.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Niger's uranium remains a strategic asset contested between French, Russian, and Chinese interests; Nigeria's oil and gas continue to underwrite regional fiscal capacity; solar potential across the Sahel is largely untapped as instability deters infrastructure investment.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Conflict disrupts waste-management systems in urban centers; e-waste and plastic accumulation in displaced-person camps and peri-urban areas saturates local sinks without formal mitigation; environmental degradation is secondary to survival priorities.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic malaria, cholera outbreaks in displacement camps, and intermittent Ebola and Lassa fever alerts maintain elevated mortality; conflict zones experience healthcare collapse, raising death rates beyond disease baselines.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Acute malnutrition rises in conflict-affected Sahel zones (Burkina Faso, Mali, Niger); WFP and humanitarian access is restricted by insecurity and regime policies; Nigerian and Ghanaian urban populations remain comparatively nourished but face inflation-driven food stress.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: High fertility persists; conflict-driven displacement disrupts family formation and increases gender-based violence; early marriage intensifies in insecure areas as a coping mechanism, sustaining demographic pressure.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population growth continues at 2.5–3% annually in Sahelian states; youth-bulge pressures intensify without economic absorption; urban migration accelerates as rural security collapses, straining capital cities' carrying capacity.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Conflict produces growing orphan, widow, and disabled populations; internally displaced persons exceed five million across the Sahel; dependency burdens outpace social-absorption capacity and humanitarian provision.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Food insecurity reaches crisis levels in conflict zones; markets are disrupted by insecurity and border closures; ECOWAS sanctions on coup states (Mali, Niger) compound economic stress before partial relaxation.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Grid electricity remains unreliable; solar-micro-grid projects stall in conflict areas; diesel-generator dependence persists in cities; Nigeria's power crisis continues with less than 5,000 MW available against demand exceeding 20,000 MW.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Formal employment contracts in coup states; gig economy persists in Nigeria and Ghana; forced recruitment by armed groups (jihadist and state-aligned militias) constitutes coerced labor in conflict zones.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Agricultural output declines in conflict regions; mining (gold in Burkina Faso and Mali, uranium in Niger) continues under shifting foreign partnerships; Nigerian tech sector faces capital outflows and talent emigration amid naira collapse.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: Nigeria's foreign reserves decline below $30 billion under import pressure and capital flight; Sahelian states lack meaningful reserves; household savings erode as inflation accelerates across the region.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: ECOWAS sanctions disrupt Niger's imports via Benin; alternative routes through Togo and Libya expand; illicit gold and contraband flows bypass formal channels; mobile-money transactions provide exchange continuity where banks fail.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: The naira collapses to over 1,500/$ on parallel markets by 2024; CFA franc stability is contested as Sahelian states discuss currency exit; cryptocurrency use persists despite restrictions, serving as remittance and store-of-value alternative.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Military juntas redirect resource rents (gold, uranium) toward regime consolidation and Russian partnership payments; Nigerian federal revenues decline with oil production drops; redistribution to populations contracts.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Elite capital flight intensifies; real-estate investment in Dubai and London accelerates; middle-class asset erosion through inflation widens inequality; defense of holdings shifts to hard assets and offshore accounts.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Artisanal gold mining expands with mercury pollution unmitigated; conflict debris accumulates; e-waste management collapses in unstable areas; waste is externalized without remediation.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Trust in state institutions collapses in coup states; ethnic and community networks intensify as survival mechanisms; inter-communal tensions rise as resource competition sharpens under climate and conflict stress.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Military elites ascend; civilian professional classes emigrate or lose status; ethnic identities (Tuareg, Fulani, Mossi, Bambara) harden as conflict polarizes; youth identity increasingly defined against both Western and domestic elites.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Displacement disrupts extended-family structures; remittances from diaspora become lifelines; inheritance disputes intensify as death rates rise and property is abandoned or seized.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: NGOs face restrictions in coup states; grassroots organizing shifts to encrypted platforms (Signal, Telegram); youth movements persist but operate under surveillance; associational life contracts under authoritarian pressure.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Football and social media remain sociability anchors; cultural events contract under curfews and insecurity; digital sociability persists but is shadowed by surveillance awareness and shutdown risk.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Protest is suppressed in coup states; social-media criticism faces prosecution; grievances accumulate without legitimate discharge, building latent instability; diaspora amplification provides external voice but no domestic relief.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: Military juntas consolidate in Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger, and Guinea; civilian governance survives in Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal with varying stability; power concentrates in security establishments with constitutional order suspended or constrained.

    Succession & Power Transfer: Coups replace electoral succession; promised transition timelines extend repeatedly; Nigeria's 2023 election (Tinubu victory) proceeds amid controversy; Senegal's 2024 election installs Faye after detention crisis; transfer mechanisms remain unstable across the region.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: Juntas invoke anti-French sovereignty and security restoration; pan-African and anti-colonial rhetoric legitimates military rule; civilian governments struggle to counter narrative with democratic-governance appeals.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: State capacity contracts in coup states as sanctions bite and civil servants emigrate; corruption persists; digital systems (ID, taxation) stagnate or regress; Nigerian government capacity remains low but functional.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: Opposition suppressed in coup states; social-media activism criminalized; Nigerian and Senegalese civil society remains active but under pressure; youth political energy is channeled toward emigration rather than domestic participation.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: France withdraws military forces; Russia (via Wagner/Africa Corps) expands; China maintains infrastructure investments; ECOWAS fractures over coup responses; regional integration stalls; great-power competition intensifies.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Juntas rely on presidential guards and Russian-backed units; jihadist groups (JNIM, ISWAP) contest territory; vigilante and ethnic militias proliferate; state monopoly on violence fragments across the Sahel.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Jihadist expansion reaches coastal states (Benin, Togo, Ghana's northern border); mass atrocities documented in Burkina Faso and Mali; kidnapping and banditry surge in Nigeria; disorder exceeds coercive absorption capacity throughout the region.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Mobile networks remain operational but under state control; internet shutdowns accompany coups and protests (Niger 2023, Senegal 2024); Starlink emerges as bypass option for elites and armed groups; velocity is high when channels are open, zero when severed.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: Social-media documentation of atrocities and protests provides volatile record; cloud storage remains offshore and accessible to those with VPNs; local archives are vulnerable to regime seizure; institutional memory degrades under turnover.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Juntas expel foreign journalists (RFI, France 24 banned in Mali, Burkina, Niger); state media dominates; Russian information operations (RT Afrique, Telegram channels) amplify anti-Western and pro-junta narratives; self-censorship intensifies among domestic journalists.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Audiences fragment between state media consumers and those accessing information via VPN and diaspora sources; TikTok and Telegram grow as platforms; algorithmic amplification favors polarizing and sovereignty-coded content.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Disinformation campaigns intensify around coups, elections, and atrocity events; Russian-affiliated networks produce coordinated inauthentic behavior; fact-checking capacity is overwhelmed; noise load exceeds verification resources.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Popular desire for security and order legitimates strongman rule in some constituencies; human-rights norms retain support among civil society but lose practical force; justice expectations shift toward immediate protection over procedural rights.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: Constitutional order suspended by decree in coup states; emergency laws expand executive power; Nigeria's cybersecurity regulations tighten; legal frameworks evolve toward surveillance and control rather than rights protection.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: Courts are subordinated to military authority in junta states; ICC and ECOWAS Court jurisdictions are rejected; Nigerian courts function but enforcement remains weak; liability for atrocities is deferred indefinitely.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Detention without trial expands; press freedom contracts; digital-rights protections are nominal; compliance burden shifts to citizens proving loyalty rather than states justifying restrictions.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Massacres in Moura (Mali, 2022) and multiple Burkina Faso villages go unprosecuted; security-force impunity is near-total in coup states; the impunity stock grows without any credible mechanism for future redress.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: Critical security studies and political-economy analysis of Sahel crisis intensify in academic and think-tank circles; pan-African sovereignty discourse gains intellectual traction; techno-optimism recedes as digital tools are weaponized.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Surveillance and shutdown technologies are imported and deployed; drone and counter-IED techniques are transferred via Russian and Turkish partnerships; technical method serves state coercion more than civilian development.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: University closures and insecurity disrupt education in conflict zones; brain drain accelerates; digital skills concentrate among those who emigrate; knowledge stock contracts in affected populations.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Institutional knowledge of democratic governance atrophies under military rule; civil-service expertise degrades through purges and emigration; local conflict-resolution traditions are lost as communities disperse.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: Anti-colonial memory is mobilized to legitimate rupture with France; pan-African solidarity rhetoric anchors junta legitimacy; national identities fragment along ethnic and regional lines under stress; #EndSARS memory persists in Nigeria as generational marker.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Afrobeats continues global expansion; Sahel cultural production contracts under insecurity; diaspora artists dominate; material culture reflects austerity and improvisation rather than investment.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Fatalism and resignation spread in conflict zones; survivalism dominates; Nigerian urban mood oscillates between cynicism and adaptation; emigration ("japa") becomes aspirational norm for youth.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Revolutionary and sovereignty rhetoric risks emptying through repetition without delivery; hashtag activism loses mobilizing force; cultural forms recycle without renewal; anomie spreads where meaning frameworks collapse.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: Eschatological and apocalyptic themes resonate as crisis deepens; jihadist cosmology frames conflict as sacred struggle; Pentecostal prosperity theology adapts to hardship narratives; secular outlooks retreat under existential pressure.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: Mosque and church attendance persists as community anchors; traditional protective rituals revive among displaced populations; digital worship continues but competes with in-person spiritual community as survival networks.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: Islamic brotherhoods retain influence in Senegal; Pentecostal networks expand despite crisis; jihadist doctrinal authority competes for allegiance in occupied territories; institutional religion fragments rather than consolidates.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Salafi-jihadist expansion displaces syncretic and Sufi practice in controlled zones; evangelical growth continues in non-conflict areas; traditional religion persists in fragments; the sacred landscape is contested and unstable.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Some survivors report spiritual exhaustion; trauma accumulates without ritual processing; disenchantment emerges among those who expected divine protection; sacred frameworks strain under the weight of unmetabolized suffering.
