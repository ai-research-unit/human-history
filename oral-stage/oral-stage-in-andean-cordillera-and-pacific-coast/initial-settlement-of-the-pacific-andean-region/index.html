    14,000 BCE – 11,000 BCE — Initial Settlement of the Pacific-Andean Region

    Summary: The first human populations enter the Pacific-Andean corridor, establishing coastal and highland footholds during terminal Pleistocene conditions; the binding constraint is the oral transmission of navigational, ecological, and subsistence knowledge required to colonize radically unfamiliar vertical environments without prior mapped routes or accumulated local lore—every technique spoken forward or lost with the speaker.

    D01. Physical Domain

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Pacific-Andean corridor presents extreme verticality: hyperarid coastal desert rising within 150 km to cordilleran peaks above 6,000 m, then dropping to humid montaña. Transit requires crossing multiple ecological thresholds—coastal fog zones, steep quebradas, high passes, and puna grasslands—each imposing distinct survival demands. The coast follows the cold Humboldt Current, creating fog-drenched lomas oases amid desert. Highland basins and quebrada corridors channel movement; no navigable rivers cross the cordillera.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Terminal Pleistocene conditions: cooler and wetter than the Holocene, with expanded glaciers on high peaks, more vegetation in now-hyperarid zones, and different lomas distributions. Sea level approximately 60–80 m lower, exposing now-submerged coastal plains. Climate variability is high, with shifts between wetter and drier phases over centuries; predictability is low, forcing adaptive flexibility.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seismic activity constant along the subduction zone; tsunamis possible on exposed coasts. Volcanic hazards limited to highland areas near active cones. Flooding in quebradas during rare precipitation events. Coastal fog unreliable; highland frost and hypoxia present physiological challenges above 3,500 m.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Marine resources abundant where Humboldt upwelling delivers nutrients: fish, shellfish, sea mammals, kelp. Coastal lomas support seasonal plant gathering and small game. Highland puna offers camelid herds (guanaco, vicuña), high-altitude tubers, and lithic raw materials (obsidian, basalt). Montaña slopes provide tropical resources. No metals exploited at this stage; energy derives entirely from human muscle and fire.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Population density negligible; waste absorption is effectively unlimited. No anthropogenic degradation detectable; midden accumulation begins at occupied sites but imposes no sink pressure. The landscape absorbs all discard without feedback.

    D02. Biological Domain

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Pathogen load low in newly colonized territory; no accumulated endemic human diseases. Mortality driven by accident, exposure, and nutritional shortfall rather than epidemic. Parasites and zoonotic exposure increase with megafaunal contact but remain limited by population sparsity and mobility.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Diet broad-spectrum: marine protein on the coast, supplemented by plant gathering in lomas; megafauna and smaller game in highlands. Nutritional status likely adequate where resources accessed, but precarious at ecological margins. Physiological adaptation to high altitude not yet developed in founding populations; hypoxia stress limits highland settlement intensity.

    Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity: Healing relies on kin care and orally transmitted botanical and procedural knowledge. No specialized healers distinguishable archaeologically. Recovery capacity constrained by small group size: loss of a knowledgeable elder eliminates healing knowledge for the band. Childbirth, injury, and infection carry high mortality.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Small founding populations risk inbreeding depression; mate exchange between bands necessary but constrained by low population density and high mobility. Reproductive rates likely limited by nutritional stress and infant mortality during colonization phase.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population extremely sparse: perhaps hundreds to low thousands across the entire region. Density highest at resource concentrations (coastal estuaries, highland basins). Growth slow, constrained by carrying capacity uncertainty and the learning costs of new environments. Migration inflows continue from the north.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependency load low in mobile foraging bands; the aged and infirm cannot be carried across difficult terrain. High infant mortality and short adult lifespans limit dependency accumulation. The able-bodied bear minimal attrition drag.

    D03. Economic Domain

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Economy entirely extractive: hunting, fishing, and gathering with no cultivation or herding. Coastal sites (Monte Verde) show exploitation of marine resources, seaweed, and littoral plants. Highland sites (Guitarrero Cave) show broad-spectrum hunting and plant collection. Provisioning floor precarious during colonization, stabilizing as local knowledge accumulates.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Energy derives from human muscle and controlled fire. No animal traction; no mechanical advantage beyond simple tools. Per-capita work ceiling very low. Fire enables cooking, warmth, and hardening of wooden tools; its maintenance and transport are critical skills.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Labor organized by kinship within small bands (20–50 persons). Division by age and sex: men hunting, women gathering, though roles overlap. No coercion structures beyond familial authority. Cooperation essential; no surplus permits exploitation.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: No surplus beyond immediate consumption; production is extraction. Tool manufacture (lithic reduction) requires skill but produces no storable wealth. Surplus capacity zero; all effort directed at provisioning.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: Storage minimal: dried fish or meat, cached plant foods. No architecture for storage. Buffering depends on resource diversity and seasonal movement rather than accumulated reserves. Shock absorption relies on spatial flexibility—moving to better-provisioned areas.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Exchange limited to inter-band reciprocity: raw materials (obsidian from highland sources), food sharing, and mate exchange. No trade networks in the developed sense. Logistics constrained by human portage; all goods carried on the body.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Value storage nonexistent; no durable medium of deferred exchange. Reciprocity operates on oral memory of obligations. Deferred claims encoded in kinship and alliance, not objects.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No extraction apparatus; surplus does not exist to extract. Redistribution occurs through sharing within the band, enforced by oral norm and immediate social pressure.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Wealth concentration impossible; portable property is personal tools and clothing. No property to defend beyond the body and the campsite.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Waste minimal and biodegradable. Midden accumulation at occupied sites; bone, shell, and lithic debitage discarded on-site. No routing decisions required; the environment absorbs all discharge.

    D04. Social Domain

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Social organization based on bilateral kinship and band membership. Marriage likely exogamous between bands to maintain genetic and social ties. Inheritance limited to portable goods and orally transmitted knowledge. No property accumulation to transmit.

    Association & Mutual Aid: The band is the primary unit of association and mutual aid. Reciprocity is universal and immediate; free-riding is not tolerated. Cooperation in hunting, childcare, and movement is survival-critical. No structures beyond the band.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Sociability centered on fireside gathering, storytelling, and shared meals. Play and ritual likely present but archaeologically invisible. Oral performance transmits cosmology, technique, and history in leisure contexts.

    Stratification, Status & Group Structure: Stratification minimal: age and skill confer respect, but no institutionalized hierarchy. Status achieved through demonstrated competence in hunting, navigation, or healing. No permanent leadership; decision-making situational and consensual.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administrative apparatus. Coordination achieved through oral consensus. Institutional integrity is the integrity of the band as a cooperating unit; no offices, no bureaucracy.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: No specialized coercive apparatus. Interpersonal violence possible but not institutionalized. Weaponry (spears, darts) serves hunting; no fortification or military organization.

    Discharge, Disorder & Decay: Friction minimal in small groups with strong kin ties. Conflict resolved through fission—disaffected individuals or families leave to join other bands. No accumulated grievance; no apparatus to decay.

    D05. Emotional Domain

    Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper: Sentiment shaped by the uncertainty and wonder of colonizing unknown landscapes. Trust necessarily high within the band—survival depends on cooperation. Anxiety modulated by oral transmission of navigational and ecological knowledge; confidence builds as local lore accumulates.

    Leadership & Personal Authority: Leadership situational and personal: the skilled hunter leads the hunt, the elder with memory leads the route. Authority is expertise-based, not hereditary or permanent.

    Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance: Allegiance is to kin and band, not to abstract authority. Legitimacy is immediate: the competent leader retains followers; the incompetent is abandoned.

    Political Will & Collective Resolve: Collective will is band consensus. Major decisions—where to move, when to hunt—are discussed and agreed orally. Resolve is high where survival is immediate; dissent leads to fission rather than coerced compliance.

    External Relations & Posture: Relations with other bands range from alliance (for mate exchange, resource sharing) to avoidance. No warfare at this population density; competition for resources is spatial rather than violent.

    Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will: Disaffection rare in high-stakes survival contexts. Apathy and despair possible in failed groups facing starvation; such groups either relocate, merge, or perish. No standing reservoir of spent will.

    D06. Informational Domain

    Communication Accessibility: Language is the exclusive medium. Founding populations bring developed speech with full grammar—the capacity to narrate the absent, the past, the possible. Mutual intelligibility within the band is total; inter-band communication depends on linguistic proximity or learned contact languages.

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Transmission is face-to-face oral speech. Reach limited to voice range; velocity is walking speed for carried messages. No channels beyond the human voice.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: No external record; all knowledge lives in memory. Persistence depends on generational transmission—the elder speaks to the young, or knowledge dies. The binding constraint of the oral stage: spoken or nonexistent, remembered or lost.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Discourse norms embedded in oral tradition: what may be spoken, when, and to whom. Sacred or dangerous knowledge may be restricted by age or initiation. No formal censorship; control is social.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Reception is immediate and personal. Attention spans shaped by fireside storytelling and practical instruction. Amplification is repetition: important knowledge is told and retold.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Signal integrity depends on accurate repetition and the absence of competing transmissions. Noise load low in sparse populations. Corruption accumulates as generations pass without correction.

    D07. Moral Domain

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Moral foundations are reciprocity, kinship loyalty, and respect for life-sustaining knowledge. Justice is immediate: the offender is sanctioned by the band, the generous are honored. Fairness is face-to-face.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No written law; taboos and norms transmitted orally. Codification is narrative: "we do not do this because the ancestors learned its danger." Regulatory evolution is slow, embedded in retold stories.

    Adjudication, Liability & Sanction: Adjudication is collective and oral. Liability is personal; the band assigns blame through discussion. Sanction ranges from shaming to expulsion; lethal sanction possible but rare.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Rights are relational, not abstract: the right to a share of the kill, the right to protection by kin. Compliance burden low; norms are internalized and enforced by immediate social presence.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Impunity rare in small transparent groups. Unredressed wrong is uncommon; social pressure ensures response. The residue is minimal.

    D08. Intellectual Domain

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: Inquiry is practical and empirical: which plants are edible, where animals move, how to work stone. Theory is embedded in oral tradition—explanatory narratives that encode observed regularities. Paradigm is animist and experiential, not abstract.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Technical knowledge covers lithic reduction (Fishtail and later points), fire maintenance, cordage and netting, wooden tool manufacture, and navigation across unfamiliar terrain. Methods transmitted by demonstration and oral instruction.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Education is apprenticeship within the band. Knowledge stock is entirely oral; skill distribution is uneven, concentrated in experienced adults. Loss of a skilled individual is loss of their knowledge.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Obsolescence is not yet relevant; accumulation has barely begun. Knowledge decay is the constant risk: death, forgetting, or failed transmission erases technique. The binding constraint operates here—spoken forward or lost.

    D09. Cultural Domain

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: Mythos carried from ancestral populations, adapted to new landscapes. Origin narratives likely invoke ancestral journeys and the discovery of the new territory. Memory is oral and genealogical, reaching back a few generations.

    Legitimating Narrative & Ideology: Legitimation is narrative: the band exists because the ancestors traveled and survived. No ideology of rule; authority is practical competence.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture is functional: lithic tools, bone and wood implements, simple cordage. Artistic expression likely includes body decoration, perishable ornaments, and oral performance (song, narrative). No monumental or durable art at this stage.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Forms are nascent, not exhausted. Meaning decay is the risk of forgetting—symbols and stories lost with their tellers. No accumulated cliché; tradition is being built, not hollowed.

    D10. Spiritual Domain

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: Cosmology likely animist: the world is alive with spirits, ancestors, and forces requiring negotiation. Existential outlook shaped by the encounter with unknown landscapes—awe, danger, and the possibility of belonging.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: Ritual invisible archaeologically at this stage but almost certainly present: rites of passage, hunting magic, propitiation of spirits. Devotion is oral and enacted, not built.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: No institutions; no hierarchy. Doctrinal authority rests with elders who remember the proper forms. The oral transmission of sacred knowledge is survival-critical—rituals spoken or lost.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Spiritual traditions brought from ancestral populations, adapting to new landscapes and species. Syncretism occurs as bands encounter each other. No displacement yet; the field is being populated.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Disenchantment absent; the world is fully enchanted. No secular drift. The sacred is immediate and unquestioned. No residue of lost transcendence.
