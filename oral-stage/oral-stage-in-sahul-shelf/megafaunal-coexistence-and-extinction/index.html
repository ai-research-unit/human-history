    35000 BCE – 20000 BCE — Megafaunal Coexistence and Extinction

    Summary: A long-term trend tracking the final millennia of human-megafauna overlap across Sahul, as giant marsupials, reptiles, and birds disappear from the landscape while human populations consolidate their continental presence. The binding constraint is the shrinking resource base as large-bodied prey vanishes; the central tension lies between hunting pressure, fire-regime alteration, and climate deterioration as interacting causes. Margin against shock is moderate — populations remain sparse and mobile, able to shift subsistence focus — and the principal direction is toward a post-megafaunal ecology where medium and small game, plants, and fire-managed landscapes become the subsistence foundation.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Sahul remains a single landmass connecting Australia, New Guinea, and Tasmania; interior deserts, tropical north, temperate southeast, and highland valleys impose distinct friction on movement, though low sea levels keep Tasmania accessible via land bridge and open corridors persist between major biomes.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Late Pleistocene climate oscillates between cooler-drier and warmer-wetter phases, trending toward increasing aridity as the Last Glacial Maximum approaches; interannual variability high, with drought cycles stressing water-dependent fauna.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Bushfire frequency rises as humans apply fire to manage vegetation; drought remains the principal acute hazard; seismic and volcanic activity negligible for most of the continent; exposure moderate given mobile settlement patterns.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Large-bodied megafauna represent a concentrated protein-fat resource declining toward exhaustion; stone raw materials (flint, chert, quartzite), ochre deposits, and plant foods remain stable; wood fuel abundant in forested zones.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Organic waste and ash from hearths disperse across open landscape with effectively unlimited absorptive capacity; butchery refuse and fire-cleared vegetation leave localized signatures but no systemic saturation.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic pathogens present but no evidence of epidemic disease; isolated continent limits external pathogen introduction; background mortality low relative to later eras.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Megafaunal hunting supplements plant and smaller-game diet with high-quality protein and fat; nutritional status likely robust where large prey available, with regional variation as megafauna decline.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Pair-bonding and reproductive patterns follow mobile forager norms; birth spacing long due to extended lactation and mobility constraints; selective pressures favor endurance, cognitive flexibility, and social cooperation.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density extremely low — perhaps tens of thousands across the continent; replacement rates positive but slow; gradual growth within carrying capacity.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependency load minimal in mobile band society; elderly hold valued knowledge; orphan and widow absorption handled through kin networks; no systemic overhang.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Hunting-gathering economy targets megafauna where available alongside smaller game, reptiles, shellfish, and plant foods; provisioning floor stable but reconfiguring as megafauna disappear, shifting toward intensified plant processing and medium game.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle power exclusive; fire the sole external energy source, used for cooking, landscape clearing, and driving game; efficiency low but adequate for band-scale needs.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Division of labor by age and gender; hunting typically male, gathering often female, with overlap; cooperation voluntary within kin groups; no coerced labor.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production immediate-return; surplus generated episodically through large kills, consumed rapidly or shared; no systematic accumulation.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No durable food storage; fat reserves on body serve as buffer; mobility and diet breadth provide resilience against localized failure.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Long-distance movement for raw materials (ochre, quality stone) attested; exchange embedded in social obligation rather than market logic; logistics constrained by human portage.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Value stored in social obligation and reciprocity; gifts create future claims; no material currency or formal credit.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Large kills shared through kinship and camp-wide distribution; no extraction by superordinate authority; redistribution governed by custom and status.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Wealth minimal and largely egalitarian; prestige goods (ochre, ornaments) circulate; no defensible accumulation.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Bone, shell, and organic refuse discarded at campsites; ash and charcoal from fires dispersed; waste volume negligible against landscape scale.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: High within-band trust; reciprocity networks extend to neighboring bands through kinship and ceremony; mutual aid obligatory among kin.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Egalitarian bands with achieved status based on hunting skill, knowledge, and oratory; no hereditary rank; group identity tied to territory and Dreaming affiliation.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship systems structure marriage, residence, and obligation; bilateral or classificatory kin reckoning; property inheritance minimal — knowledge and status transmit through initiation.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: Bands self-governing through consensus and elder authority; periodic aggregation for ceremony creates inter-band coordination; no formal institutions beyond kinship.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Leisure time substantial; storytelling, song, dance, and games occupy non-subsistence hours; sociability maintained through campfire gatherings.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Disputes resolved through mediation, temporary avoidance, or ritualized combat; sorcery accusations provide idiom for grievance; mobility allows exit from conflict; unresolved tensions rare but possible.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: Authority diffuse, situational, and achieved; elders and skilled hunters hold influence but not command; no permanent leadership offices.

    Succession & Power Transfer: No formal succession; influence accrues with age, knowledge, and demonstrated competence; transitions fluid and non-contentious.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: Authority legitimized through demonstrated ability and adherence to Dreaming law; narrative frames leaders as custodians of ancestral precedent.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administrative apparatus; coordination achieved through oral consensus and customary procedure; record-keeping absent.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: All adult members participate in decision-making within gendered and age-graded spheres; mobilization for collective action (hunts, ceremonies) achieved through persuasion.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Inter-band relations governed by kinship, marriage alliance, and ceremonial obligation; territorial boundaries recognized through Dreaming geography; conflict episodic and ritualized.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: No standing coercive apparatus; weapons (spears, clubs) individually held; collective violence rare and small-scale.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Internal order maintained through custom and social pressure; sorcery accusations channel tension; disorder minimal in low-density, kin-based society.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information travels at walking pace through face-to-face contact; ceremonial gatherings accelerate inter-band transmission; reach limited to oral network extent.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: All information stored in living memory and oral tradition; no writing; knowledge persists through repeated recitation, song, and ritual; vulnerable to elder death but redundantly held across initiated cohort.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Sacred knowledge restricted by initiation grade, gender, and clan; public discourse governed by respect protocols; taboo subjects enforced through social sanction.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention concentrated during ceremony and storytelling; amplification through repetition and performance; memorable narrative forms aid retention.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Oral transmission maintains high signal integrity through formulaic structures; noise low in small-group settings; contradictions resolved through elder authority; no significant information overload.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Moral order rooted in Dreaming law — ancestral beings established proper conduct; reciprocity, kinship obligation, and territorial respect form ethical bedrock.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: Law unwritten but orally codified through Dreaming narrative; stable over generations with gradual regional variation; taboos govern food, marriage, and sacred sites.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: Disputes adjudicated by elders or resolved through ritualized combat; liability assigned through kinship — offense against one is offense against group; sorcery belief provides explanatory framework for misfortune.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Rights embedded in kinship status and territorial affiliation; compliance burden low in small-scale society where norms are known and surveillance constant.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Impunity rare in face-to-face society; wrongs typically redressed through reciprocal action or compensation; chronic feuds possible but contained by kinship cross-cutting.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: Explanatory paradigm embedded in Dreaming cosmology — natural phenomena explained through ancestral action; empirical observation of animal behavior, plant cycles, and landscape extensive but framed mythologically.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Sophisticated tracking, hunting, and processing techniques; fire management alters vegetation structure; tool-making traditions stable with regional variation; knowledge transmission through apprenticeship and demonstration.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Education through observation, participation, and initiation; knowledge stock held collectively in oral tradition; specialized knowledge (ceremony, healing, navigation) restricted by gender and grade; widespread practical competence.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Knowledge loss occurs through elder death without transmission; megafaunal hunting knowledge becoming obsolete as prey disappears; slow decay rate in stable transmission chains.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: Dreaming narratives structure identity, mapping ancestral journeys onto landscape; megafauna may figure in some traditions (contested); deep time memory encoded in song and story.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Rock art (engravings, stencils, painted figures) records beings and events; ochre used for body decoration and art; material culture portable and functional — wooden tools, stone implements, fiber objects.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Mood oriented toward continuity and ancestral precedent; confidence in Dreaming order; pragmatic adaptation to changing game availability.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Cultural forms remain vital; no evidence of exhaustion; symbols and rituals renewed through performance; megafauna-related meanings may fade as referents disappear.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: Dreaming cosmology posits eternal ancestral present underlying ordinary time; land, animals, and humans interconnected through ancestral creation; existence meaningful and ordered.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: Ceremonies mark life transitions, seasonal events, and territorial maintenance; increase rituals ensure animal and plant abundance; sacred sites require custodial attention.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: No formal religious institution; initiated elders hold doctrinal authority; regional variation in practice and narrative within shared cosmological framework.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Spiritual traditions regionally distinct but sharing Dreaming structure; diffusion through intermarriage and ceremony; no displacement events in this window.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: No disenchantment; cosmology retains full explanatory force; megafaunal extinction absorbed into Dreaming narrative rather than undermining it.
