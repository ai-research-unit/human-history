    Planning Document — Broadcast Stage in The Japanese Archipelago (1935 CE – 2008 CE)

    Age Definition

    Broadcast Stage: One source reaches a multitude in the same instant: radio then television radiate to countless receivers who take it in together, wherever they are. A population can be addressed as one audience and held in step in real time, and public information life comes to run on the broadcast signal. Dominant until the digital substrate takes over.

    Window: 1935 CE – 2008 CE

    Region: The Japanese Archipelago — Japan from late imperial period through war, occupation, economic miracle, and digital transition; NHK public broadcasting and commercial networks.

    Note on Evidence: Documentary record includes NHK archives, government broadcasting policies, commercial network records, and media studies. NHK radio monopoly 1925; wartime propaganda; occupation broadcasting reform; NHK television 1953; commercial television 1953; color 1960; Tokyo Olympics 1964; satellite 1989; digital transition 2000s. Window begins with mature radio and television experimentation; ends as digital broadcasting replaces analog.

    Shared Evidence Base

    1. Imperial Radio and War (1935–1945 CE)

    NHK Monopoly: Nippon Hoso Kyokai; state-controlled; single broadcaster.

    Television Experiments: Pre-war research; Olympics 1940 (cancelled); wartime pause.

    Propaganda Broadcasting: Greater East Asia; military communiques; morale.

    Overseas Services: Empire broadcasting; occupied territories; Radio Tokyo.

    Air Raids: Warning systems; civilian morale; destruction coverage limits.

    Surrender Broadcast: August 15, 1945; Emperor's voice; gyokuon hoso.

    2. Occupation Reform (1945–1955 CE)

    SCAP Broadcasting: Occupation authorities; censorship; democratization.

    NHK Reorganization: 1950 Broadcasting Law; public corporation; independence.

    Commercial Licensing: 1951; Nippon TV; multiple broadcasters permitted.

    Television Launch: NHK 1953; Nippon TV 1953; Tokyo base.

    Radio Transition: From monopoly to competition; AM networks.

    Korean War: 1950–1953; censorship; Japan as base; news limits.

    3. Television Expansion (1955–1964 CE)

    Rapid Adoption: Crown Prince wedding 1959; mass set purchases.

    Commercial Networks: TBS, Fuji TV, NET; regional affiliates.

    Tokyo Olympics: 1964; international satellite broadcast; color trials.

    Economic Growth: Consumer affluence; advertising revenue surge.

    Program Development: Drama; variety; quiz shows; talent system.

    Regional Coverage: National network completion; transmitter expansion.

    4. Color Television Era (1964–1975 CE)

    Color Expansion: Mass color adoption; technical leadership.

    Variety Shows: Entertainment dominance; talent agencies; music shows.

    Anime Development: Astro Boy; television animation industry.

    News Broadcasting: Vietnam coverage; Okinawa reversion; political journalism.

    Oil Shock: 1973; economic impact; broadcasting adjustment.

    NHK Expansion: Educational channel; FM; comprehensive service.

    5. Bubble Era (1975–1989 CE)

    Satellite Broadcasting: BS-2 1984; NHK satellite channels; new capacity.

    Trendy Dramas: Urban romance; fashion; lifestyle programming.

    Commercial Dominance: Advertising peak; ratings competition; production values.

    News Evolution: Anchor-based shows; soft news; infotainment.

    Cable Beginning: Limited penetration; urban cable systems.

    Economic Bubble: 1986–1991; excess; broadcasting reflects affluence.

    6. Satellite and Diversification (1989–2000 CE)

    CS Expansion: Communications satellite; multiple channels; niche programming.

    Heisei Era: 1989; Emperor's death; new era broadcasting.

    Economic Stagnation: Lost decade begins; advertising decline.

    Internet Emergence: 1995 onwards; new competition anticipated.

    News Events: Kobe earthquake 1995; Aum attacks; crisis coverage.

    Regulatory Change: Broadcasting deregulation; new entry permitted.

    7. Digital Transition (2000–2008 CE)

    Digital Terrestrial: 2003 launch; ISDB standard; high definition.

    One Seg: Mobile television; phone viewing; new consumption patterns.

    Analog Shutdown Planning: 2011 target; regional preparation.

    Internet Competition: Broadband; online video; audience fragmentation.

    Content Export: Anime; drama; international licensing; soft power.

    Media Convergence: Broadcasting-communications integration debates.

    8. NHK Institution (1935–2008 CE)

    License Fee: Mandatory receiver fee; universal collection; debates.

    Public Service: Comprehensive programming; disaster; education.

    Political Pressure: Government influence; editorial independence.

    Governance: Board structure; reform cycles; scandals.

    Technical Leadership: Standards development; international role.

    International Broadcasting: NHK World; Radio Japan; global service.

    9. Commercial System (1935–2008 CE)

    Key Station Network: Tokyo key stations; regional affiliates.

    Advertising Model: Sponsor system; ratings competition; agencies.

    Cross-Ownership: Newspaper-broadcaster ties; media groups.

    Talent System: Agency control; exclusive contracts; star creation.

    Production Companies: Outsourcing; independent production growth.

    Regional Broadcasting: Local stations; community role; network dependence.

    10. Broadcasting and Identity (1935–2008 CE)

    Cultural Export: From import to export; anime global reach.
