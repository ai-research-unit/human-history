    1,200,000 BCE – 800,000 BCE — Early Acheulean Persistence in the Jordan Rift

    Summary: A four-hundred-thousand-year span of stable Acheulean occupation extending northward from 'Ubeidiya along the Jordan Rift Valley; multiple open-air sites at Evron Quarry, Latamne, and other lakeside and riverside localities attest geographic spread without technological transformation; the binding constraint remains access to the rift corridor with its water, lithic raw material, and prey concentrations; population density stays extremely low and the system holds wide margin against shock, trending toward gradual territorial expansion with no discernible shift in toolkit or subsistence strategy.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Jordan Rift extends as a continuous north-south corridor from the Dead Sea basin toward the Lebanese Beqa'a; open-air sites cluster along lakeshores and river terraces where water and cobbles concentrate; lateral movement up escarpments is high-friction, but rift-floor transit remains easy; the corridor is widening as a settlement zone through gradual northward range expansion.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Early Pleistocene glacial-interglacial oscillations produce alternating wetter and drier phases; within this window conditions remain broadly favorable for gallery woodland and grassland mosaics; no single climatic shock registers as system-breaking; volatility is moderate and absorbed by mobile foraging.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seismic activity continues along the Dead Sea Transform; episodic fault displacement is recorded in sediment deformation; volcanic fields to the east produce basalt flows but do not directly impact occupation zones; hazard exposure is present but margin against acute loss remains wide given low population density.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Basalt, flint, and limestone for toolmaking are abundant in stream gravels and primary outcrops throughout the rift; freshwater is perennial along rivers and lakes; vegetation provides fuel and plant foods; the endowment far exceeds draw and no resource scarcity constrains the trend.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Artifact scatters and bone accumulations disperse across open fluvial terraces; no sink approaches saturation; absorptive capacity is effectively unlimited given minimal population load across a vast landscape.
