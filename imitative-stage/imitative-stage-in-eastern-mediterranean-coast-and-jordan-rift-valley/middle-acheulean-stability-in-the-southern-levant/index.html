    700,000 BCE – 400,000 BCE — Middle Acheulean Stability in the Southern Levant

    Summary: Homo erectus populations occupy the southern Levant continuously across three hundred millennia with minimal technological change; Acheulean biface production remains the dominant industry at open-air and cave sites throughout the Jordan Rift and coastal plain; the binding constraint is the stable relationship between low population density and abundant resources that removes selective pressure for innovation; margin against environmental fluctuation is wide given diverse ecological zones accessible within short distances; the system exhibits deep stasis with no directional trend.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Jordan Rift Valley provides a north-south corridor; the coastal plain and Mount Carmel foothills offer alternative habitats; spatial friction low across open terrain but increases in dissected highlands; multiple ecological zones accessible within a day's travel.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Climate oscillates with glacial-interglacial cycles but remains within a temperate-Mediterranean envelope; interglacials bring wetter conditions; glacials impose cooler, drier regimes; volatility moderate but not disruptive to foraging viability.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seismic activity along the Rift persists; drought episodes occur during glacial phases; exposure mitigated by mobility and access to refugial zones near permanent water sources; no catastrophic hazard recorded in this window.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Flint nodules abundant in chalk formations; basalt available in volcanic regions; fauna includes large and medium game; plant resources vary seasonally; endowment exceeds demand at prevailing population levels.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Anthropogenic waste negligible; knapping debris and bone accumulate at sites but are absorbed by natural sedimentation; no sink stress at any point in the window.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic parasites and zoonotic infections present but at low density-dependent rates; mortality dominated by trauma and predation; no epidemic disease attested; baseline stable.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Diet includes hunted and scavenged game, marrow, and gathered plant foods; nutritional adequacy supports robust skeletal development where remains are available; vitality stable across the window.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Reproductive biology typical of Homo erectus; pair-bonding and mating patterns unattested directly; fertility presumed adequate to sustain low-density populations.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density remains very low; groups small and mobile; birth and death rates approximately balanced; no demographic pressure evident; turnover slow and stable.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Juvenile dependency substantial given extended maturation; no skeletal evidence of elderly or chronically disabled individuals surviving; dependency load absorbed within band-level care without strain.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Foraging and hunting provide the subsistence base; access to multiple ecological zones buffers seasonal shortfalls; provisioning floor secure and stable throughout the window.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle remains the primary energy source; fire use, established by this period, extends caloric extraction from cooked foods; conversion efficiency stable with no attested improvement.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Labor organized through informal cooperation within small bands; task division minimal and flexible; no coercion structures; participation voluntary and immediate.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production oriented toward immediate consumption; surplus generation minimal; occasional large kills produce short-lived abundance distributed promptly through sharing.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No durable storage technology; reserves limited to body fat and short-term caching; buffering provided by ecological diversity and mobility rather than accumulated stocks.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Exchange limited to within-band sharing; raw material transport distances modest, typically under ten kilometers; no evidence of inter-group exchange networks.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No symbolic or material value storage; deferred reciprocity operates within kinship and band relationships; obligations short-term and interpersonal.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No extractive hierarchy; redistribution occurs through immediate sharing at kill and processing sites; allocation egalitarian by default.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No accumulated wealth; inequality undetectable; asset defense limited to personal retention of portable tools during movement.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Knapping waste, bone refuse, and hearth ash accumulate at occupation sites; dispersal passive through sedimentation and weathering; no active routing required; load trivial.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship systems unattested but presumed central to social organization; inheritance consists of imitative transmission of technical skill; property inheritance inapplicable.
