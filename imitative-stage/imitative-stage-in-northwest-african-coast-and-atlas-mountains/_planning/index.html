    Shared Evidence Base

    Primary Archaeological Sites

    Ain Boucherit / Ain Hanech Complex, Algeria - Location: Setif Province, northeastern Algeria, ~100 km from Mediterranean - Dating: ESR and paleomagnetic dating place Ain Boucherit at ~2.4 Ma and ~1.9 Ma (two levels); Ain Hanech at ~1.83 ± 0.13 Ma (Sahnouni et al., 2018, Science) - Artifacts: Oldowan industry — limestone and flint choppers, polyhedra, subspheroids, sharp-edged cutting flakes - Evidence: Stone tool cutmarked bones indicating carcass processing; no hominin fossils recovered - Significance: Oldest archaeological evidence in North Africa; contemporaneous with East African Oldowan; challenges East Africa-only cradle hypothesis - Source: Sahnouni et al. (2018), "1.9-million- and 2.4-million-year-old artifacts and stone tool–cutmarked bones from Ain Boucherit, Algeria," Science 362:1297-1301

    Thomas Quarry I, Casablanca, Morocco - Location: Western outskirts of Casablanca (33.568° N, 7.696° W) - Dating: Unit L (oldest Acheulean) dated to ~1.3 Ma; Hominid Cave (OH4) at ~500-420 ka - Artifacts: Over 3,800 Acheulean artifacts in Unit L1 alone — quartzite and flint handaxes, picks, trihedrons, cleavers - Hominin remains: Half-mandible discovered 1969 (Ennouchi); four teeth from Hominid Cave; attributed to Homo erectus s.l. / Homo rhodesiensis - Fauna: Open woodland indicators; carnivore-processed bones; absence of cutmarks raises questions about hominin role - Significance: Earliest unambiguously dated Acheulean in North Africa; benchmark for First Regional Acheulean - Source: Raynal et al. (2001), "The earliest occupation of North-Africa: the Moroccan perspective," Quaternary International; Recent publications on 40 years of Franco-Moroccan research

    Tighenif (Ternifine), Algeria - Location: ~20 km east of Mascara, northern Algeria, ~60 km from Mediterranean, ~500 m elevation - Dating: ~700 ka based on comparative biochronology - Discovery: Site discovered 1872; major excavations by Arambourg 1954-1956 - Hominin remains: Nine isolated teeth, two nearly complete adult mandibles (Tighenif 1, 3), one hemi-mandible (Tighenif 2), parietal fragment (Tighenif 4); originally named Atlanthropus mauritanicus, now referred to Homo erectus s.l. / Homo rhodesiensis - Artifacts: Sophisticated Acheulean bifaces; entame core method suggesting links to Iberian Acheulean - Fauna: Open dry environment with permanent water; Theropithecus oswaldi, Loxodonta atlantica, Ceratotherium mauritanicum, Equus mauritanicus, Hippopotamus sirensis, Metridiochoerus compactus, Camelus thomasi, various bovids and gazelles - Significance: Oldest North African Homo erectus fossils associated with Acheulean and fauna; key for Middle Pleistocene paleoecology - Source: Geraads et al. (1986), "The Pleistocene Hominid Site of Ternifine, Algeria: New Results on the Environment, Age, and Human Industries," Quaternary Research

    Sidi Abderrahman Quarries, Casablanca, Morocco - Location: Casablanca coastal zone, near Thomas Quarry - Sites: Littorina Cave (Grotte des Littorines), Cap Chatelier, Ours Cave, Sidi Abderrahman Extension, Sidi Al Khadir - Cultural sequence (Neuville & Ruhlmann): Clacto-Abbevillian → Abbevillian → Acheulean → later industries - Hominin remains: Human mandible from Grotte des Littorines, associated with Acheulean industry - Significance: Long stratigraphic sequence documenting Lower Paleolithic evolution; First and Second Regional Acheulean phases identified - Source: Biberson (1961); Raynal & Texier, ongoing Franco-Moroccan project since 1978

    Climate and Paleoenvironment

    North African Humid Periods (NAHPs) - Mechanism: Precession-driven (~23,000-year cycle) monsoon intensification; obliquity (~41,000-year) and eccentricity (~100,000-year) modulation - Effect: Periodic "Green Sahara" conditions — grassland expansion, lake formation, river activation; corridors for hominin dispersal - Glacial suppression: NAHPs suppressed during glacial maxima due to ice sheet influence on atmospheric circulation - Evidence: Ocean sediment cores (ODP sites); dust flux records; speleothem records - Contested: Exact thresholds for green/desert transitions; spatial extent of each humid phase; pre-800 ka record less resolved - Source: Armstrong et al. (2023), "North African humid periods over the past 800,000 years," Nature Communications; Larrasoaña et al. (2013), "Green Sahara: African Humid Periods Paced by Earth's Orbital Changes," Nature Education

    Regional Fauna Indicators - Open woodland to savanna conditions predominant at Acheulean sites - Large herbivore presence (elephant, hippo, rhinoceros, equids, large bovids) indicates productive grasslands with permanent water sources - Theropithecus (gelada-related baboon) indicates open grassy habitats - Carnivore assemblages (sabertooth Homotherium at Tighenif early levels, hyenas, felids) indicate prey abundance

    Technological Framework

    Oldowan Industry (~2.6 Ma – ~1.7 Ma) - Core types: Choppers, polyhedra, discoids, subspheroids - Flake production: Simple hard-hammer percussion; opportunistic flaking - Function: Carcass processing (cutmarks), plant processing (inferred) - Cognition: Basic cause-effect understanding; no imposed form beyond edge production

    Acheulean Industry (~1.76 Ma – ~150 ka) - Diagnostic artifacts: Bifacial handaxes, picks, cleavers, trihedrons - First Regional Acheulean (Casablanca): Picks, trihedrons, handaxes, cleavers; less standardized - Second Regional Acheulean: Symmetrical handaxes, standardized cleavers; increased refinement - Raw materials: Quartzite, flint, limestone depending on local availability - Cognition: Imposed symmetry requires mental template; hierarchically structured action sequences - Transition: Abrupt appearance in the record (contested whether truly abrupt or preservational bias)

    Hominin Taxa

    Homo erectus sensu lato - North African representatives: Tighenif mandibles and teeth; Thomas Quarry mandible and teeth - Synonymy: Originally Atlanthropus mauritanicus; now often referred to Homo rhodesiensis or archaic Homo - Brain size: ~900-1100 cc for Middle Pleistocene specimens - Association: Consistently associated with Acheulean technology in North Africa - Contested: Exact taxonomic placement; relationship to African Homo ergaster and Asian Homo erectus

    Contested Points (flag in articles)

    1. Maker of Ain Boucherit tools: No hominin fossils recovered; species unknown
    2. Abruptness of Oldowan-Acheulean transition: Whether "Developed Oldowan" exists as valid category
    3. Taxonomic status of North African Homo erectus: Placement within Homo erectus s.l. vs. separate species
    4. Extent and timing of individual NAHPs before 800 ka: Record less resolved for earlier Pleistocene
    5. Hominin role in Tighenif bone accumulations: Carnivore processing documented; cutmarks less clear
    6. Gibraltar vs. Sinai dispersal route: No evidence of water-crossing capability in Lower Pleistocene
