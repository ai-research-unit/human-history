    1900000 BCE – 1700000 BCE — Ain Boucherit Oldowan Occupation

    Summary: Static snapshot of the earliest attested hominin presence in North Africa, centered on the Ain Boucherit and Ain Hanech sites in northeastern Algeria. Oldowan lithic industry — choppers, polyhedra, subspheroids, sharp-edged flakes — persists in dynamic equilibrium with no transition toward bifacial forms. Cutmarked bones attest carcass processing; no hominin remains recovered. Binding constraint is the cognitive ceiling of pre-linguistic imitative transmission, which limits technique to what can be shown and copied hand-to-hand. Shock-buffer margin rests on group mobility across a semi-arid grassland-savanna mosaic during a precession-driven humid phase; direction of change is effectively null — the system reproduces itself without detectable drift across the window.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Setif Province plateau, northeastern Algeria, ~100 km inland from Mediterranean; terrain of dissected wadis and limestone outcrops; transit friction moderate, with seasonal watercourses channeling movement toward flint- and limestone-bearing exposures.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Precession-driven North African Humid Period yields expanded C4 grassland and open woodland; interannual variability moderate; no secular drift detected within window — humid phase stable across the two-hundred-thousand-year span.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seismic activity along Tell Atlas fault system poses low-frequency ground-shaking risk; drought pulses possible but buffered by mobility; no volcanic exposure; wildfire regime present but non-catastrophic in open grassland.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Abundant limestone and flint nodules in local exposures provide raw material for Oldowan cores and flakes; large herbivore biomass supplies carcass resources; wood and plant fuel adequate; drawdown negligible against stock.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Discharge limited to scattered lithic debitage and bone refuse at processing sites; sink capacity of open landscape effectively unlimited; no degradation imprint detectable.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic pathogen load inferred from tropical-African vectors moving north during humid phases; baseline mortality high but balanced by reproductive turnover; no epidemic signature recoverable.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Cutmarked bones indicate access to large-mammal protein and marrow; nutritional status inferred adequate for reproduction; stature and robustness not directly attested — no hominin fossils recovered at Ain Boucherit.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Reproductive biology follows early Homo pattern inferred from East African comparanda; pair-bonding and mating structure not directly attested; selective pressures stable within window.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density very low, likely small mobile bands; replacement ratios sufficient to maintain occupation across window; no crowding against carrying ceiling.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependency load — juveniles, injured, aged — absorbed within band mobility constraints; no evidence of accumulating surplus dependents; attrition cleared by mortality and mobility.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Energy Capture & Conversion Regime: Energy capture limited to human muscle and controlled fire (fire use inferred but not directly attested at these sites); conversion efficiency low; per-capita work ceiling gates all activity to immediate subsistence needs.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Labor organization informal, task-based, and non-coercive; specialization minimal beyond age and sex differentiation; coordination achieved through imitative learning and co-presence.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production confined to lithic manufacture and carcass processing; no durable surplus generated beyond immediate consumption; output ceiling set by raw-material access and butchery opportunity.

    Storage, Reserves & Risk-Buffering: No storage technology; reserves consist of body fat and cached knowledge of resource patches; risk buffered by mobility and landscape familiarity rather than accumulated stock.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Exchange limited to within-band sharing; no evidence of inter-group trade; logistics constrained to portable toolkit carried during seasonal rounds.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No monetary or proto-monetary instruments; deferred reciprocity operates informally within band — share now, expect return later — but leaves no recoverable trace.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No coordinating authority extracts surplus; redistribution occurs through immediate sharing at kill or processing site; communal allocation clears each windfall within the consumption cycle.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Wealth effectively absent as a category; portable toolkit is personal but not defended through institutional means; no concentration or stratification.

    Byproduct Streams & Material Discharge: Lithic debitage and bone refuse left at processing sites; discharge volume negligible; no recycling or sequestration — material abandoned in place.
