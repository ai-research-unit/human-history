    700000 BCE – 600000 BCE — Tighenif Homo erectus Settlement

    Summary: Static snapshot of a major Homo erectus occupation at Tighenif (Ternifine), Algeria, representing the oldest firmly associated hominin fossils in North Africa; sophisticated Acheulean bifaces produced using the entame core method document advanced lithic technology; rich savanna-like fauna — Theropithecus oswaldi, Loxodonta atlantica, hippopotamus, rhinoceros, camelids, large bovids — indicates open dry grassland with permanent water; the binding constraint is access to permanent water in a semi-arid landscape; knowledge transmission is purely imitative, held in living memory with no external record.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Inland basin ~20 km east of Mascara, ~60 km from the Mediterranean coast, at ~500 m elevation; permanent spring or lake attracted fauna and hominins, creating a resource node amid open terrain with low friction for movement.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Semi-arid savanna conditions inferred from faunal assemblage; precession-driven cycles produce episodic humidity but the snapshot captures a relatively dry, stable grassland phase with permanent local water.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Drought is the primary hazard; no volcanic or seismic signature; margin against drought buffered by the permanent water source and large-mammal prey base — exposure moderate but not acute.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Abundant large-mammal fauna provides high-quality protein; lithic raw material locally available; permanent water is the critical limiting resource, concentrating occupation at this node.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Bone accumulation and lithic debitage are the primary discharge; organic waste disperses or is scavenged; absorptive headroom unlimited at attested density — no saturation pressure.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Zoonotic exposure from dense fauna contact (hippopotamus, bovids, suids) inferred; waterborne parasites probable near permanent water — mortality baseline elevated relative to drier sites but unquantified.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Large-bodied Homo erectus supported by high-protein diet from megafauna; dental wear patterns on recovered teeth suggest mixed abrasive diet; vitality adequate for population persistence.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Reproductive biology inferred from skeletal morphology consistent with other Homo erectus populations; pairing dynamics unrecoverable — physiological fertility adequate.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Multiple individuals represented (nine teeth, three mandibles, parietal fragment) suggest repeated or extended occupation; density low but site reuse indicates demographic continuity across generations.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Juvenile and adult remains present; no evidence of differential treatment or care — dependency load absorbed or lost, unrecoverable in detail.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Energy Capture & Conversion Regime: Human muscle is the sole motive force; fire use plausible but not directly attested at Tighenif — energy ceiling low, work limited to what bodies can perform unaided.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Cooperative hunting of megafauna inferred from prey size; division of labor by age and sex probable — coordination is face-to-face, no coercion apparatus.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Sophisticated Acheulean bifaces (entame core method) represent accumulated technical investment; meat surplus from large kills shared immediately — no durable surplus storage.

    Storage, Reserves & Risk-Buffering: No storage technology attested; risk buffered by landscape knowledge, mobility, and social sharing — reserves exist only as body fat and knowledge.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Raw-material transport limited to local procurement; no inter-group exchange recoverable — logistics are hand-carried within daily foraging radius.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No durable value medium; deferred reciprocity within kin group inferred but unrecorded — obligations are personal and immediate.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No authority above the band; large-kill sharing is the primary redistribution mechanism, inferred from ethnographic analogy and prey size.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No differential wealth accumulation; tools are personal possessions defended by presence and social sanction — no recoverable asset concentration.

    Byproduct Streams & Material Discharge: Dense bone accumulation and lithic debitage mark the site; discharge is localized but absorbed by landscape processes — no overload.
