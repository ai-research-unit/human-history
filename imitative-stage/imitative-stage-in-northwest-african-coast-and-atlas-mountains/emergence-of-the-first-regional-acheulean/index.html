    1300000 BCE – 1000000 BCE — Emergence of the First Regional Acheulean

    Summary: Long-term trend documenting the transition from Oldowan to Acheulean technocomplex along the Casablanca littoral of Atlantic Morocco, centered on the Thomas Quarry I Unit L assemblage dated to ~1.3 Ma. The window tracks the appearance of bifacially worked picks, trihedrons, handaxes, and cleavers — diagnostic Acheulean forms requiring imposed symmetry and hierarchically structured reduction sequences absent in the preceding Oldowan. Over 3,800 artifacts in Unit L1 alone make this one of the largest early Acheulean assemblages in Africa. Binding constraint remains the imitative transmission ceiling: technique passes hand-to-hand without verbal instruction, capping the complexity and standardization achievable. Direction of change is toward greater formal elaboration, but progress is slow, measured in tens of thousands of years per incremental refinement.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Casablanca littoral, Atlantic Morocco; coastal plain with low limestone ridges and marine terraces; raw-material outcrops of quartzite and flint accessible within short foraging radius; transit friction low along shoreline corridor.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Precession-driven humid-arid oscillation regime; window spans multiple cycles, with grassland expansion during humid phases and contraction during arid intervals; interannual variability moderate; secular drift not pronounced within bounds.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seismic risk low along passive Atlantic margin; coastal flooding possible during storm surge; drought pulses buffered by littoral resource diversity; wildfire regime present in adjacent scrubland but non-catastrophic.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Abundant quartzite cobbles and flint nodules in marine terraces and river gravels; coastal shellfish, marine mammals, and terrestrial fauna provide protein; wood and plant fuel adequate; drawdown negligible against standing stock.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Discharge limited to lithic debitage concentrations at quarry and processing loci; sink capacity of coastal landscape effectively unlimited; no degradation imprint detectable.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Pathogen load inferred moderate — coastal zone may reduce some tropical vectors but introduces marine-borne parasites; baseline mortality high but balanced by reproductive turnover; no epidemic signature recoverable.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Access to diverse protein sources — terrestrial game, marine resources, plant foods — supports adequate nutritional status; physiological vitality not directly attested at Thomas Quarry; hominin remains from nearby sites suggest robust early Homo.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Reproductive biology follows Homo erectus pattern inferred from regional comparanda; pair-bonding and mating structure not directly attested; selective pressures stable across window.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density remains low; replacement ratios sufficient to sustain continuous occupation of the littoral; no crowding against carrying ceiling; possible slight demographic expansion trackable through site density, but contested.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependency load absorbed within mobile band structure; attrition cleared through mortality and mobility; no accumulating surplus dependents detectable in the record.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Subsistence based on hunting, scavenging, and gathering across littoral and hinterland zones; provisioning floor met through diversified resource exploitation; household pooling operates at band level with immediate consumption.

    Energy Capture & Conversion Regime: Energy capture limited to human muscle and controlled fire (fire use inferred from regional comparanda though not directly attested at Thomas Quarry Unit L); conversion efficiency low; per-capita work ceiling gates activity to subsistence and toolmaking.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Labor organization informal and task-based; specialization minimal beyond age and sex differentiation; Acheulean reduction sequences may involve sustained individual effort but coordination remains imitative, non-coercive.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production centered on lithic manufacture — picks, trihedrons, handaxes, cleavers — and carcass processing; no durable surplus beyond immediate use; large artifact counts reflect repeated occupation, not stockpiling.

    Storage, Reserves & Risk-Buffering: No storage technology; reserves consist of body fat, cached landscape knowledge, and possibly curated bifaces carried as mobile toolkit; risk buffered by coastal resource diversity and seasonal movement.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Exchange limited to within-band sharing; no evidence of inter-group trade; logistics constrained to portable Acheulean toolkit transported during foraging rounds.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No monetary or proto-monetary instruments; deferred reciprocity operates informally within band; Acheulean bifaces may carry social signaling value, but this remains inferential and contested.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No coordinating authority extracts surplus; redistribution occurs through immediate sharing at kill or processing site; communal allocation clears each resource windfall within the consumption cycle.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Wealth effectively absent as a category; high-quality bifaces may confer status but are not defended through institutional means; no concentration or heritable stratification.

    Byproduct Streams & Material Discharge: Lithic debitage constitutes primary discharge — flakes, cores, failed bifaces — left at quarry and reduction loci; bone refuse at processing sites; discharge volume locally concentrated but landscape-scale impact negligible.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: Living memory only — no external storage medium; knowledge of reduction sequences persists solely in the bodies and habits of skilled practitioners; loss of expert knappers risks technique degradation.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: No symbolic framing or censorship recoverable; communication norms pre-linguistic, governed by attention and imitation rather than verbal instruction or taboo.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Reception limited to direct observation; attention captured by salient knapping demonstration; amplification impossible beyond the witnessing group; Acheulean symmetry may enhance visual salience, aiding transmission.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Signal integrity maintained through repetition and correction during co-action; noise load low given small group size; verification immediate and embodied in the produced artifact.
