    350,000 BCE – 200,000 BCE — Late Acheulean to Middle Stone Age Transition

    Summary: A long-term trend across the Northwest African littoral and Atlas margins in which the Classic Acheulean bifacial tradition gives way to prepared-core (Levallois) flaking technologies that yield standardized flakes and points rather than handaxes; the binding constraint remains the imitative mode of transmission, which permits gradual drift and local innovation but cannot accelerate diffusion or arrest regression, so the transition unfolds unevenly across space and time without any possibility of codified instruction or recorded precedent.

    D01. Physical Domain

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The transition plays out across the Atlantic Moroccan coast, from Casablanca southward through the Doukkala plain, and inland along the flanks of the Middle and High Atlas; the coastal strand offers low-friction movement, while the mountain valleys channel and constrain interior travel.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Marine Isotope Stages 9 through 7 (roughly 330,000–190,000 years ago) bring alternating humid and arid phases; humid intervals open Saharan corridors connecting the Maghreb to sub-Saharan populations, while arid intervals contract habitable range toward coastal and montane refugia.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Drought is the principal hazard, capable of collapsing local carrying capacity; the Atlantic moderates coastal aridity, but interior populations face sharper oscillations; no volcanic or major seismic hazard impinges on the region during this window.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Flint and quartzite remain available in marine-terrace deposits and stream gravels; the shift toward Levallois technique does not require new raw materials but exploits existing nodules more efficiently, yielding more usable edge per unit of stone.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Lithic debitage continues to accumulate at reduction sites; environmental impact remains negligible at prevailing population densities; sink capacity is not a limiting factor.

    D02. Biological Domain

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic disease load persists without major epidemic signature; mortality from trauma, infection, and nutritional stress continues to shape population turnover, but no new pathogen regime emerges.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Access to diverse fauna and coastal resources supports adequate nutrition; body size and robusticity remain high, though climatic oscillations periodically stress provisioning.

    Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity: Wound care and nursing remain within-group, transmitted by observation; there is no advance in healing technique discernible from the archaeological record.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Cooperative breeding and extended juvenile dependency continue; no change in reproductive biology is detectable, though population structure may shift as groups expand or contract with climate.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population remains sparse and fluctuates with climate; humid phases likely see expansion into previously marginal areas, while arid phases concentrate survivors in refugia; replacement ratios hover near parity.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: The dependency load remains light; group mobility constrains the survival of those unable to travel; short-term support of the injured is possible, but chronic dependency is unsustainable.

    D03. Economic Domain

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Immediate-return foraging continues; the transition to Levallois technology may improve hunting efficiency through standardized, hafted points, but the subsistence mode remains unaltered.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle remains the sole prime mover; fire is in routine use; no new energy source is captured during the transition.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Division of labor remains informal; the new reduction technique is more demanding of planning and sequential control, possibly concentrating production among fewer skilled practitioners.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Levallois technique is more economical of raw material than bifacial shaping, extracting more cutting edge per nodule; this efficiency may buffer against raw-material scarcity but does not generate storable surplus.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No formal storage technology emerges; caches of prepared cores or blanks at favored sites may represent proto-reserves, but evidence is ambiguous.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Exchange remains face-to-face and reciprocal; there is no evidence of long-distance trade networks; raw-material transport is limited to what individuals carry.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of exchange or unit of account exists; obligations are tracked through memory and repaid in kind.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Redistribution continues through immediate sharing; no institutionalized extraction emerges.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Personal possessions remain minimal; a toolkit, body knowledge, and social standing constitute individual wealth; defense is situational.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Lithic debitage is left at reduction sites; organic waste decays; no environmental load accumulates.

    D04. Social Domain

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Small bands persist as the basic social unit; kinship ties structure cooperation and resource sharing; inheritance remains experiential, passed through imitation.

    Association & Mutual Aid: Cooperative hunting, shared childcare, and mutual defense continue; the increased cognitive demand of Levallois reduction may favor tighter apprentice-expert relationships within the band.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Play and grooming maintain social bonds; communal rest follows foraging; no new sociability structures emerge.

    Stratification, Status & Group Structure: Status remains fluid, tied to demonstrated competence; mastery of the new reduction technique may confer additional prestige to skilled knappers.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administrative apparatus exists; coordination is achieved through imitation and situational leadership.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Hafted points may improve hunting weaponry, but organized coercion remains absent; disputes are settled by dominance, avoidance, or fission.

    Discharge, Disorder & Decay: Friction dissipates through band fission; the small-group scale keeps grievance personal and manageable.

    D05. Emotional Domain

    Leadership & Personal Authority: Leadership remains situational; mastery of the cognitively demanding Levallois technique may concentrate authority among a smaller set of expert knappers whose competence is directly witnessed.

    Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance: Allegiance attaches to individuals whose competence and generosity are visibly demonstrated; no institutional legitimacy exists.

    Political Will & Collective Resolve: Collective action is mobilized through shared affect and imitative entrainment; no verbal deliberation is possible; resolve is achieved by visible consensus.

    External Relations & Posture: Encounters with other bands remain cautious; mutual assessment precedes interaction; avoidance is the default response to strangers.

    Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper: Group cohesion depends on face-to-face familiarity; sentiment fluctuates with provisioning success and climatic conditions; humid phases may foster expansion and optimism, arid phases caution and contraction.

    Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will: Disaffection expresses itself through departure; the option of fission dissipates accumulated grievance before it crystallizes into chronic conflict.

    D06. Informational Domain

    Communication Accessibility: Communication rests on gesture, posture, vocalization, and facial expression; no articulate language is available; mutual legibility requires prolonged co-presence.

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information travels at the speed of bodily movement; the reach of any message is limited to those within sight or earshot; innovations spread only as fast as their practitioners move.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: There is no external record; all knowledge resides in living heads; the Levallois technique, once mastered, persists only as long as its practitioners live and demonstrate it to successors.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: No discursive framing exists; there is no conceptual censorship, only the implicit constraint of what can be shown.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention is directed by pointing, gaze, and physical guidance; amplification occurs only through repeated demonstration to successive learners.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Each transmission drifts; without a fixed record to correct against, cumulative drift can simplify or alter technique over generations; the prepared-core concept is robust to drift because the core itself serves as a physical template for the next stage of reduction.

    D07. Moral Domain

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Reciprocity remains the operative principle; sharing is expected and free-riding punished by exclusion; no codified ethic exists.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No formal law exists; behavioral norms are implicit in practice and enforced through social consequence.

    Adjudication, Liability & Sanction: Disputes are resolved situationally; there is no institutionalized adjudication; avoidance and fission substitute for formal sanction.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Individual autonomy is constrained only by group pressure; no formal rights are recognized.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongdoing beyond group tolerance results in exclusion; chronic impunity is limited by small-group scale and the impossibility of anonymity.

    D08. Intellectual Domain

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No formal inquiry exists; understanding is procedural, embedded in the motor sequences of tool production and foraging routines.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: The Levallois reduction sequence represents a cognitive advance: the knapper must envision the final flake before removing it, shaping the core to predetermine the product's size and shape; this requires greater planning depth than bifacial shaping and produces standardized, often hafted, flakes and points with predictable morphology.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Skill transmission remains imitative; the increased complexity of prepared-core reduction may narrow the cohort who achieve mastery, concentrating advanced technique among fewer hands; the knowledge stock is the sum of what living practitioners carry.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: When a master knapper dies without demonstrating the full reduction sequence, elements of the technique may be lost; local populations can regress to simpler methods if transmission is interrupted; there is no record to preserve the technique against such loss.

    D09. Cultural Domain

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: Group identity remains grounded in shared experience; there is no narrative mythos, only embodied memory of places, faces, and repeated actions.

    Legitimating Narrative & Ideology: No ideological justification for the social order exists; the order is the way things are done, validated by imitation of predecessors.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: The prepared core and its products display a different aesthetic than the Acheulean handaxe: symmetry is now in the flake rather than the core, and standardization suggests a shared mental template; the transition alters the material-culture signature without introducing symbolic elaboration.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Cultural forms remain unelaborated symbolically; the new technique does not exhaust but rather supplements the repertoire, and both traditions may coexist locally during the transition.

    D10. Spiritual Domain

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No articulate cosmology is possible without language; the world is encountered directly; the subject's place in it is unreflected.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: If proto-ritual exists, it is indistinguishable from habitual action; no deliberate symbolic behavior is attested during this window.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: No religious institution exists; spiritual authority is meaningless in the absence of communicable doctrine.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: There is nothing to diffuse; belief in the conceptual sense does not yet exist.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Disenchantment presupposes prior enchantment; the category does not apply; the spiritual dimension remains latent, awaiting the emergence of language and symbolic thought.
