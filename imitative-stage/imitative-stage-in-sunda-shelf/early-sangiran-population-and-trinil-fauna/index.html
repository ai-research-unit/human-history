    1,400,000 BCE – 1,000,000 BCE — Early Sangiran Population and Trinil Fauna

    Summary: Homo erectus is now established at Sangiran, the population growing slowly within a rich Jetis-fauna environment of megaherbivores, large bovids, and open woodland. The central constraint is the carrying capacity set by tropical foraging returns — the population remains sparse, dispersed, and tightly bound to faunal abundance, with no mechanism to buffer against local depletion or environmental shift.

    D01. Physical Domain

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Solo Basin remains the core habitat, with Sangiran's sedimentary domes preserving the densest fossil record. The Bengawan Solo and its tributaries provide water and movement corridors through mixed woodland and open grassland. The volcanic uplands to the south supply ash and lahar deposits that periodically bury and preserve surfaces. Movement within the basin is easy; the landscape is open enough for large-mammal tracking.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: The climate is tropical monsoonal, warm year-round with a marked wet-dry seasonality. Interglacial conditions prevail for much of this interval, maintaining forest-savanna mosaic. Glacial cycles continue to modulate sea level, but Java remains connected to the Sunda shelf during low-stands, permitting faunal exchange with the mainland.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Volcanic activity continues along the central Javanese arc. Ash falls, lahars, and pyroclastic events are periodic hazards, though they also fertilize soils and create fossil-preserving deposits. Flooding along the Solo drainage poses seasonal risk but also concentrates carcasses for scavenging.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: The Jetis fauna — the older faunal assemblage predating the classic Trinil fauna — includes Stegodon, Hexaprotodon (hippopotamus), large bovids, suids, cervids, and carnivores including tigers and hyenas. Plant resources are abundant: fruiting trees, tubers, and bamboo. Water is reliable.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Population density remains negligible; the environment absorbs all hominin impact without measurable effect. Bone refuse accumulates at processing sites but is scattered and rapidly decomposed or buried.

    D02. Biological Domain

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Tropical disease burden continues — intestinal parasites, vector-borne fevers, bacterial infections. Mortality is high by modern standards but stable; the population persists, indicating adaptation to local pathogens. Large carnivores remain a predation risk.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Sangiran crania and post-cranial fragments show robust, well-nourished individuals with heavy muscle markings and thick cortical bone. Cranial capacity averages 800–900 cc, within the lower Homo erectus range. Dental wear indicates coarse, gritty diets requiring heavy mastication.

    Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity: Healing remains limited to rest, wound-tending, and group tolerance. Some Sangiran specimens show healed fractures, indicating survival of significant injuries — evidence of care or at least non-abandonment of the injured.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Reproductive patterns remain typical of Homo erectus: extended juvenile dependency, likely pair-bonding or serial monogamy, and high infant mortality offset by continued reproduction through the fertile years. The population is now large enough to avoid immediate inbreeding depression.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density remains low — perhaps a few hundred individuals across the Solo Basin and surrounding areas. Growth is slow; the population replaces itself but does not expand rapidly. Age structure is heavily weighted toward reproductively active adults.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Few individuals survive to old age; the dependent load is minimal. Most adults remain productive until death from disease, accident, or predation.

    D03. Economic Domain

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Subsistence is foraging with an emphasis on large-mammal exploitation — hunting and scavenging Stegodon, bovids, and cervids supplemented by plant foods. The megafauna provide high caloric returns per acquisition event. Provisioning is immediate-return; no storage.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Energy capture is human muscle, supplemented by fire where used for cooking and defense. Evidence for controlled fire in Java at this date is ambiguous but plausible. The work ceiling is set by human physiology.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Labor is cooperative and task-specific: group drives or ambush hunting for large game, shared processing of carcasses, collective plant gathering. Division follows age and sex. No coercion beyond informal social pressure.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: No surplus production. Large kills may temporarily exceed immediate consumption but spoil without preservation. Any excess is shared and consumed within the band over days.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No material storage. Buffering is biological — body fat, group sharing — and behavioral — mobility to new foraging patches when local returns decline.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Exchange is intra-band sharing. No evidence of inter-group trade or exchange networks. Logistics is movement of the band to resources, not movement of resources to the band.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No value storage. Reciprocity is immediate and face-to-face; there is no deferred obligation beyond memory of past sharing.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No extraction apparatus. Distribution follows informal sharing norms — successful hunters distribute to kin and band members without formal obligation.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No wealth accumulation. All individuals are materially equivalent. Possessions are tools and perhaps infant carriers; nothing is defended as property.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Bone and plant waste accumulate at processing and habitation sites. No deliberate routing; natural decomposition and burial by sediment clear residues over time.

    D04. Social Domain

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship structures remain simple multi-generational bands with mother-offspring bonds as the stable core. Pair-bonds may exist but are not institutionalized. Inheritance is meaningless absent heritable property.

    Association & Mutual Aid: The band is the unit of cooperation. Mutual aid is obligatory: shared food, collective childcare, group defense against predators. Cooperation is maintained by kinship, familiarity, and reciprocity.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Non-foraging time is spent in rest, grooming, and social interaction. Juvenile play reinforces motor skills and social bonds. Adults engage in low-intensity sociability during rest periods.

    Stratification, Status & Group Structure: Stratification is minimal. Status differences may exist based on age, hunting success, or physical dominance, but these are fluid and situational. No permanent hierarchy; egalitarianism is enforced by the ease of fission.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administration or institutions. Coordination is achieved through imitation, gesture, and direct observation. Group decisions emerge from consensus or follow experienced individuals.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: No organized coercion. Violence is occasional and interpersonal — over mates, food shares, or dominance. Weapons are opportunistic tools. Defense against predators is collective.

    Discharge, Disorder & Decay: Social tension is discharged through spatial fission — disgruntled individuals leave. No accumulated grievance or structural decay; the organization is too simple to ossify.

    D05. Emotional Domain

    Leadership & Personal Authority: Leadership is informal and context-dependent. Experienced hunters lead during hunts; knowledgeable individuals guide foraging routes. Authority dissolves when the task ends.

    Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance: Allegiance is personal and kin-based. There is no abstract order to legitimate; individuals follow those they know through direct relationship.

    Political Will & Collective Resolve: Collective action is coordinated but not deliberated. The band acts together by habit and necessity. There is no political sphere distinct from daily cooperation.

    External Relations & Posture: Other bands are rarely encountered. When contact occurs, it is likely cautious — potential mates, potential competitors, potential threats. No sustained inter-group relations.

    Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper: The prevailing temper is practical — focused on daily subsistence with emotional responses tied to immediate circumstances. Trust is high within the band, cautious toward strangers. Fear of predators is constant; satisfaction follows successful hunts.

    Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will: Disaffection does not accumulate; the option of departure prevents festering grievance. Apathy is incompatible with the demands of foraging life.

    D06. Informational Domain

    Communication Accessibility: Communication remains gestural, postural, and vocal but pre-linguistic. Complex information is conveyed through demonstration and imitation. Vocalization conveys emotional state and alarm. Mutual intelligibility is high within the band.

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information travels at the speed of individuals. There is no communication at distance; all transmission is face-to-face. Range is limited to immediate proximity.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: No external record. All knowledge exists in living memory. The death of an experienced individual erases their accumulated knowledge unless previously demonstrated to others.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: No framing apparatus. Communication is direct, situational, and transparent within the limits of pre-linguistic expression. No censorship or deliberate concealment.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention is captured by movement, sound, and urgency. No amplification; influence is limited to direct presence.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Transmission drift is inherent in imitative learning. Each demonstration introduces variation; there is no mechanism to standardize or correct. Skills and knowledge degrade without reinforcement.

    D07. Moral Domain

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Moral intuitions are embedded in practice — fairness in meat-sharing, protection of young, resentment of cheaters. Justice is emotional and immediate, not codified or reasoned.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No laws exist. Conduct is regulated by habit, imitation, and informal sanction. Norms are implicit in behavior, not articulated as rules.

    Adjudication, Liability & Sanction: Disputes are settled by dominance, avoidance, or departure. There is no formal adjudication. Sanction is social — exclusion, reduced sharing, physical confrontation.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: No concept of rights. Individuals have what they can hold and defend. Compliance is simply conformity to group practice.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongs are addressed immediately or forgotten. No accumulated backlog of injustice; memory is short and departure easy.

    D08. Intellectual Domain

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No formal inquiry or theory. Knowledge is practical and embodied — knowing how to track, butcher, find water, avoid predators. Understanding is embedded in action, not abstracted into propositions.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Technical repertoire includes large-mammal butchery, stone-flaking (minimal in Java), plant processing, fire use (if present), and landscape navigation. All skills are transmitted by demonstration and imitation.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Education is observation and practice alongside experienced individuals. The knowledge stock is limited to what the living population carries. Skill distribution depends on individual aptitude and the chance of exposure to competent demonstrators.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Knowledge decays with death. There is no external storage to preserve superseded techniques; loss is permanent. Transmission drift erodes fidelity over generations.

    D09. Cultural Domain

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No narrative memory or mythos. Identity is immediate — recognition of band members, familiarity with home range. There is no sense of collective history or future.

    Legitimating Narrative & Ideology: No legitimating narrative. The social order requires no justification because no alternative is conceivable.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture is expedient and unadorned — stone flakes, bone fragments used as tools, perhaps wooden implements (unpreserved). No ornamentation, no symbolic marking, no deliberate aesthetic.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: No symbolic forms exist to exhaust. The material repertoire is stable because minimal and purely functional.

    D10. Spiritual Domain

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No articulated cosmology. The world is immediate and present. There is no evidence of reflection on origins, death, or the nature of existence.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: No ritual practice is attested. Repeated behaviors exist — daily routines, seasonal movements — but lack symbolic elaboration.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: No religious institutions or doctrine. The concept does not apply.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: No spiritual beliefs to spread or displace.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: No sacred realm exists. The world is neither enchanted nor disenchanted; the category does not apply to pre-symbolic cognition.
