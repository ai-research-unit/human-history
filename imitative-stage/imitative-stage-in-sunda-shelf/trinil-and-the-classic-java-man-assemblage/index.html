    700,000 BCE – 500,000 BCE — Trinil and the Classic Java Man Assemblage

    Summary: A static snapshot of established Homo erectus populations in the Solo River basin during the Middle Pleistocene, characterized by the Trinil H.K. faunal assemblage and the type specimens of Pithecanthropus erectus; the binding constraint is riverine resource availability in a stable tropical environment; margin against subsistence failure remains adequate given low population density; the system is in equilibrium with no net directional change.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Solo River cuts through volcanic uplands and alluvial plains in East Java, providing a permanent water corridor and transit route; floodplain margins offer foraging habitat; movement friction low along the river, higher in forested interiors.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Tropical conditions with seasonal monsoon rainfall; interannual variability moderate; no secular climate drift evident within this window; temperature and humidity stable.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Volcanic activity along the Sunda Arc poses episodic ashfall risk; seasonal flooding along the Solo River predictable and navigable through mobility; earthquake risk moderate; population margin adequate given dispersed settlement.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Volcanic and siliceous stone available for tool manufacture; freshwater abundant year-round; faunal biomass concentrated in riverine and forest-edge ecotones; no evidence of resource depletion.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Organic waste and lithic debris absorbed by fluvial processes and soil decomposition; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited at prevailing population density.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic tropical pathogens present; waterborne diseases probable along river margins; mortality baseline elevated but sustainable at low density; no epidemic stress evident.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Trinil femur and other skeletal material indicate robust build and adequate nutrition; stature consistent with African Homo erectus; physiological vitality stable.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Physiological fertility sufficient for population maintenance; pairing structure unrecoverable; extended juvenile dependency inferred from brain size and maturation rate.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population at low density relative to carrying capacity; renewal rate adequate for replacement; no evidence of demographic growth or decline within this window; stasis.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Juveniles and occasional injured or aged individuals present; dependency load absorbable by productive adults; no evidence of demographic strain.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Absent; all transactions immediate and within-group; no mechanism for deferred reciprocity recoverable.
