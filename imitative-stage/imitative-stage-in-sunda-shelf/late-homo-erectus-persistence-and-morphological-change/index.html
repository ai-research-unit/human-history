    300,000 BCE – 200,000 BCE — Late Homo erectus Persistence and Morphological Change

    Summary: A long-term trend documenting the persistence and gradual morphological change of Homo erectus populations in Java during the late Middle Pleistocene; Sambungmacan specimens showing mosaic of archaic and derived cranial features; continuity debates unresolved; population stable under unchanged selective pressures with no external disruption; direction of change incremental and endogenous.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Sunda Shelf configuration fluctuating with glacial cycles; Java connected to mainland during lowstands, isolated during highstands; Solo River basin providing consistent habitat corridor; volcanic highlands constraining inland movement.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Tropical climate persisting with glacial-interglacial modulation; cooling and drying during glacials reducing forest cover; interglacials restoring humid conditions; regime oscillating within tolerable bounds without directional trend.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Volcanic activity continuing along Java arc; ashfall events periodic; riverine flooding seasonal; populations adapted to chronic hazard regime with adequate margin; no catastrophic event attested in this window.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Volcanic stone sources stable; freshwater from Solo River system reliable; forest and riverine biomass fluctuating with climate but not depleted; endowment adequate for low-density hominin occupation.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Minimal waste generation; tropical decomposition and riverine flushing clearing organic refuse; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited relative to hominin output; no degradation accumulating.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Tropical disease environment continuing; endemic parasitic load present; mortality baseline stable; no epidemic disruption attested; population turnover absorbing baseline losses.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Sambungmacan crania indicating robust individuals; no skeletal evidence of nutritional deficiency; physiological vitality maintained; morphological changes occurring within well-nourished population.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Reproductive capacity sufficient for population maintenance; pairing dynamics stable; morphological changes not indicating disrupted reproductive patterns; gradual cranial evolution consistent with continuous gene flow.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population persisting at low density across available habitat; renewal adequate without expansion; no demographic pressure detectable; isolation during high sea stands potentially reducing effective population size.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependency load at baseline levels; attrition balanced by renewal; no evidence of elevated dependency straining productive cohort; population structure stable.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Foraging continuing in tropical forest, riverine, and ecotone habitats; diet broad-spectrum; provisioning adequate at subsistence floor; no evidence of resource stress or dietary change.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle as primary energy source; fire use probable but direct evidence sparse in Javanese contexts; conversion efficiency unchanged from earlier periods.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Small group organization persisting; task allocation by age and sex through imitative learning; no formal division of labor; coercion limited to informal group pressure.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Lithic production continuing in established tradition; no technological innovation detectable; tools manufactured as needed without surplus stockpiling; productive capacity matching consumption.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No storage technology; buffering through dietary breadth and mobility; reserves held in body condition and social networks; no material reserve accumulation.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: No inter-group exchange networks attested; raw materials locally procured; logistics confined to daily and seasonal foraging rounds; long-distance movement not evidenced.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Reciprocity obligations within group as sole deferred exchange mechanism; no durable value storage; credit relations informal and face-to-face.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Food sharing as primary redistribution; no surplus extraction beyond immediate consumption needs; communal access to large resource patches.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No wealth accumulation; positional advantage based on skill and knowledge rather than property; no asset defense beyond tool kit maintenance.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Knapping debris and food refuse dispersed at occupation sites; tropical conditions ensuring rapid breakdown; routing by abandonment; no accumulation.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Minimal stratification; status differentiation by age, experience, and skill; group identity through shared practice and co-residence; no hardening of boundaries detectable.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: Band-level organization only; no formal association; self-organization through observation, imitation, and direct coordination; norms implicit in practice.
