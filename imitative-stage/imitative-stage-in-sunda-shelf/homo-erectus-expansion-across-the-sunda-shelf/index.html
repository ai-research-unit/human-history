    1,000,000 BCE – 700,000 BCE — Homo erectus Expansion Across the Sunda Shelf

    Summary: A long-term trend of Homo erectus demographic growth and geographic spread across the intermittently exposed Sunda landmass, driven by glacial-lowstand land bridges connecting Java to Sumatra and Borneo; the binding constraint is coastal and riverine corridor availability during lowered sea levels; margin against population isolation remains positive while the shelf stays exposed; the trend direction is outward dispersal and population increase.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Sunda Shelf lies exposed during glacial lowstands, linking Java, Sumatra, and Borneo into a continuous tropical landmass drained by large river systems emptying northward into the South China Sea; transit friction is low along riverine corridors and coastal plains but rises sharply in montane interiors.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Tropical conditions prevail with reduced monsoon intensity during glacials; rainfall is lower than interglacial peaks but sufficient to sustain mixed forest-savanna mosaics; interannual variability moderate; no secular drift toward aridity within this window.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Volcanic activity along the Sunda Arc poses episodic hazard through ashfall and pyroclastic events; seismic risk moderate; flood pulses seasonal along major rivers; populations hold adequate margin by dispersed settlement across a large landmass.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Siliceous and volcanic stone for tool manufacture locally available; freshwater abundant along river systems; faunal biomass substantial in forest-edge and riverine ecotones; no evidence of drawdown pressure on standing stocks.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Organic waste and knapping debris negligible relative to landscape scale; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited; no saturation.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic tropical pathogens present; waterborne and vector-borne disease likely; mortality baseline elevated but within sustainable limits given low population density; trend stable.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Robust Homo erectus skeletal morphology indicates adequate caloric and protein intake; stature and bone density consistent with nutritional sufficiency; trend stable to improving during expansion phase.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Physiological fertility sufficient for replacement; pairing patterns unrecoverable but small-group structure inferred from living primate analogs; no evidence of reproductive stress.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population growing; density low relative to carrying capacity; outward dispersal indicates successful reproduction and surplus individuals colonizing new territories; age structure unrecoverable but renewal adequate.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependent individuals (juveniles, aged, injured) present but absorbable by productive cohort; no evidence of demographic drag exceeding group capacity.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Generalist foraging combining plant gathering, scavenging, and hunting of Trinil fauna (Stegodon, Axis, Bibos); riverine resources exploited; provisioning floor stable above minimum subsistence.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle is the sole power source; fire use probable but direct evidence sparse; conversion efficiency limited to metabolic and thermal capture; per-capita work ceiling low but adequate for mobile foraging.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Task division by age and strength inferred; no evidence of coercion beyond immediate group dynamics; cooperation coordinated through imitation and gestural demonstration.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production limited to immediate consumption and simple tool manufacture; surplus minimal and episodic; no evidence of sustained accumulation.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No durable storage; buffering achieved through mobility and landscape knowledge rather than stockpiling; reserves effectively zero.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: No evidence of intergroup exchange; raw material procurement local; logistics limited to daily foraging radius.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Absent; all exchange immediate and within-group; no deferred reciprocity recoverable from this window.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No coordinating authority; communal sharing of large kills inferred from group foraging; extraction and redistribution informal and immediate.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No durable wealth; portable tools personally held; no evidence of differential accumulation or asset defense.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Organic waste and lithic debitage dispersed across landscape; volume trivial; no concentration or routing strategy required.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Small-group living requires high intragroup trust; mutual aid in foraging and childcare inferred; radius of trust likely confined to immediate band.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Minimal stratification; status differentiation by age and physical capability; group identity defined by co-residence and shared imitative repertoire.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kin-based group structure probable; pair-bonding and extended juvenile dependency inferred; inheritance limited to learned skills transmitted through imitation.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: Band-level cooperation the only associational form; coordination achieved through direct interaction and behavioral copying; no formal structures.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Non-work time spent in grooming, play, and rest; social bonding through shared activity; no evidence of structured festivity.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Interpersonal conflict resolved through dominance displays, avoidance, or group fission; no formal mediation; unabsorbed friction discharged through dispersal.
