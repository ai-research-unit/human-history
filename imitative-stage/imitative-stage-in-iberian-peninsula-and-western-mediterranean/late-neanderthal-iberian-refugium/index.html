    100,000 BCE – 50,000 BCE — Late Neanderthal Iberian Refugium

    Summary: During a fifty-thousand-year span dominated by glacial conditions (MIS 5d–3), the Iberian Peninsula functions as a refugium for Neanderthal populations, with southern and coastal zones sustaining occupation while northern and interior regions become seasonally or permanently uninhabitable. The binding constraint remains the imitative transmission ceiling: technical knowledge and adaptive responses cannot be pooled, codified, or transmitted faster than hand-to-hand copying permits, leaving each isolated group to rediscover or lose solutions independently.

    D01. Physical Domain

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The peninsula's topographic diversity becomes selectively important as climate deteriorates. The southern coastal strip, the Guadalquivir lowlands, and the eastern Mediterranean littoral retain habitable conditions; the Cantabrian coast offers sheltered valleys and marine resources. The central Meseta and Pyrenean uplands become seasonally harsh or abandoned. Caves and rockshelters — El Sidrón, Bolomor, Bajondillo — concentrate occupation evidence. The Gibraltar corridor remains a theoretical link to Africa but no crossing evidence exists.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: The window spans MIS 5d through early MIS 3, including the pronounced cold of MIS 4 (71–57 ka). Glacial conditions push tree lines downslope, replace forest with steppe and scrubland in the interior, and shift fauna toward cold-adapted assemblages. Southern Iberia maintains warmer, more stable conditions, creating a refugium effect. Interannual volatility is high; short warm episodes interrupt stadial cold.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Drought during cold-dry phases stresses water sources and concentrates fauna at remaining springs and river courses. Cold snaps at altitude can trap or kill exposed groups. Coastal storm surge affects littoral camps. Fire risk increases in dry scrubland.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Lithic raw material availability remains stable; flint and quartzite outcrops continue to supply tools. Fuel wood becomes scarcer in deforested interiors, concentrating occupation near remaining woodland. Prey biomass shifts: red deer decline in interior zones while horse, ibex, and cold-adapted species increase.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Occupation densities remain too low to saturate any sink. Ash and bone accumulate at long-occupied caves, forming stratified deposits valuable to archaeology but negligible as environmental burden.

    D02. Biological Domain

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic parasite loads persist. Nutritional stress during MIS 4 may elevate mortality; skeletal evidence from El Sidrón shows dietary stress markers. Wound infection and hunting trauma continue to claim lives. Life expectancy remains short; few individuals survive past 40.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Isotopic analysis from El Sidrón (~49 ka, near the window's end) indicates heavy reliance on terrestrial herbivores with minimal plant or marine input. Caloric requirements remain high due to cold-adapted body plans. Nutritional shortfalls during harsh phases leave enamel hypoplasia and growth disruption in dental remains.

    Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity: Healed injuries on Neanderthal skeletons demonstrate continued group support for the wounded. At El Sidrón, individuals with debilitating conditions survived long enough to leave skeletal evidence, indicating sustained care. Knowledge of wound treatment remains imitative — no cumulative medical tradition.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Genetic analysis from El Sidrón reveals a small group with low diversity and evidence of inbreeding, likely a single extended family. Population isolation during glacial conditions restricts mate exchange. Effective population size is critically low.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density drops as habitable area contracts. Estimates for late Neanderthal Iberia suggest total populations in the low thousands. Replacement rates are barely positive; local extinctions are common, recolonization slow. The demographic margin against extinction is thin.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependent load consists of juveniles and the injured. The small size of the productive cohort means any additional dependency — a disabled adult, multiple children — strains provisioning. The elderly rarely survive to impose significant load.

    D03. Economic Domain

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Hunting of medium and large herbivores remains the subsistence base: red deer, horse, ibex, aurochs depending on local fauna. Coastal groups exploit shellfish, as evidenced at Bajondillo and later at Gorham's Cave. Plant foods contribute seasonally but are archaeologically invisible.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Fire management is essential for survival during cold phases. Hearths are structured and repeatedly used. No evidence of hafted projectile weapons in this window — hunting remains close-range with thrusting spears, maintaining high injury risk.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Labor organization remains imitative and informal. Genetic evidence from El Sidrón suggests task division by sex: adult males show heavier wear patterns consistent with hunting, females with hide processing. This division is inferred, not observed; enforcement is social, not coercive.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production remains hand-to-mouth. A successful hunt generates temporary surplus consumed before spoilage. No technology extends preservation beyond natural cold storage during winter.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: Body fat is the primary reserve. Cached meat may freeze at altitude during winter. No evidence of systematic food storage or caching. Inter-group buffering is unlikely given population isolation.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Raw material sourcing continues to indicate mobility ranges of 30–100 km. Some longer-distance transport of high-quality flint occurs, but mechanisms are unclear — group mobility or occasional inter-group contact. No sustained exchange networks are attested.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of stored value or deferred obligation exists beyond remembered reciprocity within the band. Credit has no meaning when nothing stores.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Redistribution remains immediate sharing of kills. No extraction apparatus, tribute, or taxation exists.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Portable wealth is negligible. Personal tool kits may vary in quality, but accumulation is impossible. No stratification by wealth occurs.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Organic waste, bone, and lithic debitage accumulate at occupation sites. Spatial organization within caves separates activity zones, but waste routing is informal — discard rather than management.

    D04. Social Domain

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: El Sidrón's genetic evidence reveals a patrilocal residence pattern: related males stayed together while females came from outside groups. This implies inter-group contact for mate exchange despite population isolation. Inheritance remains meaningless where knowledge transmits by imitation.

    Association & Mutual Aid: The band remains the sole unit of sustained association. Mutual aid is obligatory within it. Inter-group relations are episodic — mate exchange implies contact, but no evidence of alliance, conflict, or regular interaction survives.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Non-work time is spent in proximity within shelter. Juvenile play, grooming, and physical contact maintain bonds. No structured leisure activities leave trace.

    Stratification, Status & Group Structure: Status remains informal and personal. Skill, success, and generosity confer respect; none can be inherited or formalized. Group structure is the band of 10–30 individuals, likely a single extended family.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administration exists. Coordination is face-to-face and real-time. No decision persists beyond the presence of those who made it. No institution carries memory or authority across generations.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Coercion is interpersonal. Violence is personal and immediate — no organized warfare, no raiding apparatus. Weapons remain hunting tools.

    Discharge, Disorder & Decay: Social friction discharges through fission or personal conflict. The El Sidrón remains show evidence of cannibalism — whether survival, mortuary, or conflict-related is debated. No disorder accumulates because no order exists to disorder; no apparatus decays because none exists.

    D05. Emotional Domain

    Leadership & Personal Authority: Leadership is situational. The individual who knows the game trails, who has survived longest, who knaps best, leads in that domain. Authority cannot be delegated, represented, or transmitted.

    Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance: Allegiance is to persons present and proven. Legitimacy is demonstrated daily through competence and generosity. No abstract legitimacy attaches to positions or institutions.

    Political Will & Collective Resolve: Collective action is limited to what can be coordinated by co-presence — a hunt, a move to new ground. No sustained campaign is possible. Resolve dissipates when the group disperses.

    External Relations & Posture: Relations with other bands are episodic. Mate exchange implies some positive contact. Resource competition may produce avoidance or conflict, but no evidence survives. No posture or policy persists across encounters.

    Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper: Affect is interpersonal and immediate. Trust is kin-based and band-limited. Mood responds to immediate circumstances — successful hunts, harsh weather, injury. No collective sentiment spans groups or persists across absences.

    Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will: Individual withdrawal or departure is the only expression of disaffection. No collective apathy accumulates because no collective enterprise endures long enough to exhaust.

    D06. Informational Domain

    Communication Accessibility: Communication remains gestural, vocal, and demonstrative. No evidence of symbolic language. Mutual intelligibility depends on shared practice within the band; outsiders are intelligible only after extended observation.

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information travels at walking speed. No message can precede the messenger. Range is limited to face-to-face contact. Distant groups are informationally isolated.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: All information resides in living memory. The death of a knowledgeable individual extinguishes their knowledge. No external record preserves anything. Each generation rebuilds its understanding from observation.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Without propositional language, framing is limited to what demonstration emphasizes. No censorship operates because no propositions circulate. Conduct, not speech, is regulated.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention is held by salience and proximity. No amplification mechanism exists. Information reaches only those who witness events directly.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Imitation introduces drift with each transmission. Techniques simplify or shift without correction. No verification mechanism identifies or reverses degradation. Information integrity decays across generations.

    D07. Moral Domain

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Justice is retributive and interpersonal. Fairness in sharing is enforced by social pressure and the threat of exclusion. No abstract principle underwrites conduct; right is what the group tolerates.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No codification exists. Custom is transmitted by imitation and enforced by sanction. Rules drift with practice and personnel; no deliberate reform is possible without articulation.

    Adjudication, Liability & Sanction: Disputes are resolved by dominance, mediation, or departure. Sanction is personal — shaming, violence, exclusion. No formal adjudication process exists.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Rights are not conceptualized. Protection derives from band membership and personal ties. Compliance is to observed norms, not articulated rules.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongs against the powerless may go unredressed. Impunity accrues to the strong and central. No mechanism accumulates grievance or enforces delayed justice.

    D08. Intellectual Domain

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No propositional inquiry exists. Understanding is practical — pattern recognition in prey behavior, lithic fracture, fire management. Theory is implicit in action, never articulated.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Mousterian technology continues: Levallois, discoid, and Quina reduction strategies. Regional variants persist. Techniques are stable within the window; no major innovation is attested. Point forms and retouched scrapers dominate assemblages.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Knowledge transmits by observation and guided practice. Not every individual masters every technique. The knowledge stock is limited to what the current generation can demonstrate and transmit.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Techniques are lost when no practitioner survives. Innovation is rare and accidental. Lost techniques are not recovered; each group works with what its members can teach each other.

    D09. Cultural Domain

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No mythos can be articulated without language. Identity is band membership, demonstrated through familiarity, shared practice, and kinship. Memory is personal and extinguished by death.

    Legitimating Narrative & Ideology: No narrative legitimates the order. Authority rests on presence and performance, not story.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture remains functional. Stone tools, wooden implements, fire-hardened points. Some evidence of pigment collection (manganese dioxide at several sites) suggests possible body decoration or hide treatment, but no figurative or symbolic art is attested in Iberia during this window. Ornament use is absent or archaeologically invisible.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Without symbolic forms, no exhaustion or hollowing occurs. Techniques may become rote, but no attached meaning can decay.

    D10. Spiritual Domain

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No cosmology can be reconstructed from evidence. Whether Neanderthals conceptualized death, spirits, or forces beyond the visible remains unknown. Deliberate body treatment at El Sidrón — cannibalism with possible ritual elements — hints at awareness of death but cannot demonstrate belief.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: No unambiguous ritual is attested. Repeated behaviors at hearths and discard areas are practical. The boundary between routine and rite cannot be drawn without symbolic evidence. If sacred practices exist, they are invisible.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: No institutions or doctrine exist. Whatever spiritual intuitions individuals hold remain private and untransmitted.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Without transmissible belief, no diffusion or syncretism occurs. Each individual's orientation is idiosyncratic; nothing cumulates or spreads.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Disenchantment presupposes enchantment that can erode. If Neanderthals hold sacred orientations, they cannot accumulate or decay collectively. Each generation's spiritual life, if any, begins from its own inference and experience, unbequeathed.
