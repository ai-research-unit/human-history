    130,000 BCE – 80,000 BCE — Neanderthal Florescence in the Last Interglacial

    Summary: Peak Neanderthal population density across Iberia during Eemian interglacial warmth, with site proliferation from Gibraltar to Catalonia; Mousterian prepared-core technology standardized; dietary breadth expanding to include marine shellfish and small game; ochre procurement at coastal sites attests to non-utilitarian material use; population well within carrying capacity; system in demographic and technological equilibrium.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Iberian topography unchanged — Cantabrian coast, Ebro and Guadalquivir valleys, interior Meseta, Mediterranean littoral — offering diverse microhabitats and transit corridors between coastal and upland zones with moderate friction.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Eemian interglacial conditions bring temperatures 1–2 degrees above present, reduced seasonality, expanded forests, and elevated sea levels flooding coastal shelves; climate stable within window, trending toward cooler conditions only near its close.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Acute hazards minimal; seismic activity episodic along Betic-Rif zone; volcanic quiescence; no evidence of catastrophic events during window; ample margin against environmental shocks.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Diverse lithic raw materials — high-quality flint in Cantabria and Catalonia, quartzite on Meseta — abundant; marine and terrestrial fauna concentrated; standing stock far exceeds extraction rate.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Knapping debris and organic discard scattered across open landscapes and cave mouths; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited relative to negligible anthropogenic load.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Pathogen exposure low in dispersed populations; skeletal pathology where attested shows healed trauma and degenerative conditions but no epidemic signature; mortality primarily from trauma and senescence.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Neanderthal bodies robust and well-nourished; isotopic evidence indicates high trophic-level diet dominated by large herbivores, supplemented by marine resources at coastal sites; stature and bone mass at population norms.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Small-group mating networks; inferred consanguinity elevated but not critically constrained; reproductive physiology typical of archaic Homo; pair-bonding patterns unrecoverable but likely flexible.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density at its Iberian peak; site density highest recorded for Pleistocene hominins in region; birth-death balance positive or stable; age structure shows juveniles and adults but high juvenile mortality.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependency load absorbed within band structures; evidence of healed injuries implies care of incapacitated individuals; elderly rare but present; no demographic overhang threatening renewal.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Subsistence secure; large-game hunting provides bulk calories; shellfish exploitation at Gibraltar and Murcia coast expands dietary base; plant foods likely but archaeologically invisible; well above carrying-capacity edge.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Energy derived from human muscle, animal tissue, and controlled fire; fire use consistent and routine for cooking, warmth, and possibly hafting adhesives; conversion efficiency adequate for subsistence needs.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Division of labor unattested but likely minimal and age- or sex-graded; coercion absent beyond intra-group social pressure; cooperation organized through imitation and direct demonstration.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Tool production exceeds immediate breakage replacement; surplus capacity limited to durable lithics and possibly hides; no evidence of accumulation beyond immediate-use horizon.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No durable storage attested; buffering achieved through mobility and dietary flexibility; fat reserves on bodies serve as short-term caloric buffer; no granary or cache equivalent.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Raw-material transport distances modest (typically under 30 km); no evidence of inter-group exchange networks; logistics confined to seasonal rounds within home ranges.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of deferred exchange; reciprocity immediate and embedded in group sharing norms; concept of credit or stored value absent.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Kill sharing distributes returns within band; no extraction by supra-group authority; redistribution immediate and consumption-oriented.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Portable toolkit constitutes personal wealth; no durable wealth concentration; asset defense unnecessary in context of group sharing.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Butchery waste, knapping debris, and hearth residues discarded at site; routing is dispersal into open environment; load negligible relative to sink capacity.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: High intra-band trust; mutual aid evidenced by healed injuries requiring extended care; reciprocity immediate and obligatory within small groups; radius of trust limited to familiar individuals.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Stratification minimal; status likely age- and skill-graded; group identity localized to band; no evidence of inter-group markers or boundaries beyond home-range familiarity.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship structures unattested but likely bilateral or matrilineal given small-group dynamics; inheritance limited to learned skills and portable tools; no property regime.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: Band is sole unit of association; coordination achieved through imitation and direct interaction; no supra-band organization attested.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Leisure time present given adequate provisioning; play among juveniles inferred from comparative ethnography; sociability centered on hearths and shared consumption.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Grievances resolved through avoidance, fission, or direct confrontation; no institutionalized mediation; social friction low in context of small, mobile groups.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: No formalized authority; leadership situational and skill-based; decision-making distributed among adults; power neither concentrated nor contested.

    Succession & Power Transfer: Succession irrelevant absent institutionalized positions; leadership roles contingent on context and competence; no transfer mechanism required.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: No legitimating narrative recoverable; authority rests on demonstrated competence and familiarity rather than ideology.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administrative apparatus; coordination achieved through direct observation and imitation; institutional integrity concept inapplicable.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: All adults participate in group decisions through presence and action; no formal mobilization or factionalism attested.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Inter-band relations sparsely attested; encounters with other bands likely rare given low population density; no evidence of territorial conflict or alliance.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: No specialized coercive apparatus; hunting weapons serve defensive purposes; force capacity distributed among adult males.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Internal disorder minimal in small, kin-based groups; predator threat from hyenas and large cats present but manageable with fire and vigilance.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information transmission gestural, demonstrative, and observational; velocity limited to co-presence; reach confined to band members; no linguistic channel attested for window.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: Knowledge stored exclusively in living memory and embodied skill; persistence limited to lifespan; ochre procurement may indicate rudimentary marking but no durable record system.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Framing and censorship concepts inapplicable absent linguistic discourse; communication norms embedded in observational learning; no recoverable taboos.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention focused on immediate demonstrators; amplification limited to sequential observation within group; no broadcast capacity.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Signal fidelity depends on demonstrator skill and observer attention; copying errors accumulate slowly given stable repertoire; noise load low.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Baseline sense of fairness inferred from sharing behavior and care of injured; formal moral code absent; justice intuitive and contextual.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No law or codification; behavior regulated by custom and imitation; no evolution attested within window.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: No third-party adjudication; disputes resolved by direct parties through avoidance, submission, or confrontation; liability concept absent.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: No formalized rights; protections derive from group membership; compliance burden nonexistent absent rules.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Impunity and redress concepts inapplicable; wrongs absorbed through group dynamics or fission; no accumulating grievance backlog.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No formal inquiry or theory; practical problem-solving embedded in technological tradition; paradigm concept inapplicable.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Levallois and Mousterian reduction sequences represent sophisticated applied knowledge transmitted through imitation; method stable and standardized across region.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Knowledge transmitted through apprenticeship-by-observation; skill distribution age-graded with expertise concentrated in experienced adults; stock stable within window.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Technological repertoire stable; no obsolescence attested; knowledge decay through demographic loss remains latent risk but not realized in window.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No recoverable mythos or origin narrative; identity likely embedded in place familiarity and group membership; collective memory limited to living generations.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Ochre use at Cueva de los Aviones and other coastal sites suggests non-utilitarian material engagement; no figurative art; shell collection at Gibraltar possibly ornamental though interpretation contested.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Mood unrecoverable; behavioral disposition conservative and risk-averse as inferred from stable technological tradition; no evidence of crisis or disruption.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: No form exhaustion attested; traditions renewed through continuous practice; meaning decay concept inapplicable absent symbolic elaboration.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No recoverable cosmology; existential orientation embedded in immediate experience; metaphysical beliefs unattested.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: No unambiguous ritual attested; ochre use may indicate proto-ritual behavior but function uncertain; sacred practice not demonstrable.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: No religious institutions; doctrinal authority concept inapplicable; spiritual leadership unattested.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: No spiritual system to diffuse or displace; concept inapplicable to window.
