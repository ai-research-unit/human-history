    200,000 BCE – 100,000 BCE — Iberian Neanderthal Establishment

    Summary: Classic Neanderthals establish themselves across the Iberian Peninsula during a window spanning interglacial warmth and early glacial cooling, developing regional Mousterian variants and occupying diverse ecological niches from the Cantabrian coast to the Mediterranean littoral. The binding constraint is the imitative transmission ceiling: all technological knowledge passes hand-to-hand, limiting the complexity of coordinated action and ensuring that each group's repertoire drifts with the deaths and births of its members.

    D01. Physical Domain

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Iberian Peninsula provides a topographically varied arena bounded by the Pyrenees, the Cantabrian Cordillera, the central Meseta, and coastal margins on three sides. River valleys — the Ebro, Tagus, Guadiana, Guadalquivir — channelize movement and concentrate resources, while limestone massifs punctuated by caves and rockshelters offer defensible occupation sites. The Gibraltar corridor connects to North Africa across a strait narrow enough to sight land but too wide for crossing by available means.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: The window opens in MIS 6 glacial conditions, passes through the Eemian interglacial (MIS 5e, ~130–115 ka) with temperatures warmer than present, then cools into early MIS 5 substages. Forest cover expands during the interglacial — oak, pine, and Mediterranean sclerophyll — then contracts and gives way to steppe-woodland as cooling resumes. Seasonality intensifies, with colder winters at altitude and along the northern coast.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Drought cycles and cold snaps during stadial intervals impose periodic stress on prey populations and plant resources. Seismic activity along the Betic margin poses localized risk. Flooding in valley floors seasonally displaces occupation to higher terraces.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Abundant flint, quartzite, and chert outcrops supply lithic raw material; quality varies by region, with fine-grained sources concentrated in the Cantabrian and eastern coastal zones. Fuel wood is plentiful during interglacial forest phases but scarcer on glacial steppes. Game biomass fluctuates with vegetation regime.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: At band-level occupation densities, the landscape absorbs all organic and lithic discard without saturation. Fire-altered soils accumulate at hearth sites but disperse over generational timescales. No feedback from waste accumulation constrains settlement.

    D02. Biological Domain

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic parasites and zoonotic exposure from prey processing impose a background mortality load. No epidemic disease signatures appear in the skeletal record; wound infection and sepsis from trauma contribute to premature death. Life expectancy at birth likely sits below 35 years, with high juvenile mortality.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Neanderthal bodies are stocky, cold-adapted, and heavily muscled, requiring elevated caloric intake — estimated at 3,500–5,000 kcal daily. Isotopic evidence from later Iberian Neanderthals indicates heavy reliance on large herbivores. Dental wear and skeletal stress markers suggest episodic nutritional shortfall.

    Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity: Healed fractures on Neanderthal remains across Europe demonstrate that injured individuals survive significant trauma with group support. No specialized healing knowledge exists; wound tending and convalescent provisioning are imitated behaviors constrained by what watching and copying can transmit.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Small effective population sizes inferred from later genetic evidence suggest frequent inbreeding and low genetic diversity. Pair-bonding patterns are unknown; extended juvenile dependency implies sustained parental investment.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density remains low — perhaps one individual per 10–50 square kilometers in favorable zones. Groups number 15–30 individuals. Replacement rates are slow; a single failed cohort can extinguish a local population, and recolonization depends on neighboring bands.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: The elderly rarely survive past 40; the dependent load consists primarily of juveniles and the recently injured. The productive adult cohort absorbs this load through sharing, but any spike in dependency strains the group's capacity.

    D03. Economic Domain

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Subsistence centers on hunting large and medium herbivores — red deer, horse, aurochs, and wild boar during interglacials; ibex and reindeer during colder phases. Plant foods supplement the diet seasonally but leave scant archaeological trace. Coastal groups exploit shellfish and occasional marine mammals.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: All energy conversion relies on human muscle and controlled fire. Hearth structures concentrate heat for warmth, cooking, and possibly hafting adhesive preparation. No evidence of hafted spears in this window; thrusting spears require close-range hunting with high injury risk.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Labor is organized by task visibility and demonstrated competence. No coercion apparatus exists beyond social sanction. Division by age and sex is plausible but undocumented; imitation channels task assignment toward those who have seen the task performed.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production barely exceeds immediate consumption. A successful kill generates a temporary surplus that must be consumed within days before spoilage. No storage technology extends this window.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: Fat reserves in bodies constitute the only buffer against lean periods. Cached carcass parts freeze in winter at altitude but rot rapidly in Mediterranean zones. Risk pooling occurs through sharing within the band; no inter-group pooling is attested.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Raw material sourcing indicates movement ranges of 30–100 km, accomplished through group mobility rather than exchange networks. No evidence of inter-group trade; exotic materials are absent from assemblages.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of deferred exchange exists. Reciprocity operates within the band on immediate or short-term cycles; obligations are remembered in living minds and cannot outlast the parties.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Redistribution is informal and immediate: large carcasses are shared at the kill site or at camp. No extraction apparatus or tribute system exists.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Portable wealth is negligible — personal tool kits and ornaments if any. No stratification by accumulation is possible when nothing stores.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Bone refuse, lithic debitage, and ash accumulate at occupation sites, forming middens that attract scavengers. Discard is spatially organized — knapping areas separated from sleeping zones — but no formal waste management exists.

    D04. Social Domain

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship structures are reconstructed only by analogy; genetic evidence from later Neanderthals suggests patrilocal residence with female dispersal. Inheritance is meaningless where nothing durable is owned; knowledge passes by imitation, not bequest.

    Association & Mutual Aid: The band is the sole unit of association. Mutual aid is universal within it — obligatory sharing of food and cooperative defense. Inter-group relations are unknown; territorial boundaries may exist but cannot be codified or negotiated in the absence of language.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Non-work time is spent at camp in proximity to others. Juvenile play likely rehearses adult tasks. Grooming and physical contact maintain social bonds. No structured leisure activity leaves archaeological trace.

    Stratification, Status & Group Structure: Status differentiation is minimal and situational — the skilled knapper, the successful hunter — but cannot be formalized or inherited. No stratification apparatus exists.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administration exists. Coordination is face-to-face, limited to what can be gestured and demonstrated. Decision-making is consensus-based or dominance-based within small groups; no institutional memory carries decisions across generations.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Coercion is personal — physical dominance, ostracism, violence. Weapons are hunting tools repurposed. No organized means of force exists; inter-group conflict, if it occurs, is raid-level and undocumented.

    Discharge, Disorder & Decay: Social friction is absorbed by fission — dissatisfied individuals leave to join other bands or found new ones. Disorder at the group level means dissolution, not reform. No apparatus decays because none exists.

    D05. Emotional Domain

    Leadership & Personal Authority: Leadership is situational and personal — the individual who knows where prey congregates, who has knapped the best tool. Authority cannot be abstracted from the person or transmitted beyond their presence.

    Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance: Allegiance is to persons, not positions. Legitimacy is demonstrated competence and generosity. Disaffection leads to departure, not reform.

    Political Will & Collective Resolve: Collective action is limited to what can be coordinated by gesture and example in real time. Sustained campaigns are impossible; resolve lasts only as long as participants remain co-present and motivated.

    External Relations & Posture: Relations with other bands are episodic and undocumented. Encounters may be hostile, neutral, or affiliative depending on kin ties and resource competition. No posture can be maintained across distance or time.

    Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper: Affect is immediate and interpersonal. Trust extends to band members and no further. Behavioral temper is shaped by recent success or failure in provisioning; mood shifts are unmediated by narrative or ideology.

    Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will: Spent will results in individual withdrawal or group fission. No collective apathy accumulates because no collective enterprise persists long enough to exhaust commitment.

    D06. Informational Domain

    Communication Accessibility: Communication relies on gesture, facial expression, vocalization, and demonstrated action. No symbolic language is attested; mutual intelligibility is limited to what can be shown. Strangers are unintelligible until they have been observed long enough to learn local conventions.

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information travels at the speed of human movement — no faster than a person can walk to deliver it. Transmission range is limited to face-to-face contact; no relay or broadcast mechanism exists.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: All information resides in living memory. When the knower dies, the knowledge dies. No external record — no mark, no mnemonic device, no notation — preserves information beyond a lifespan. Each generation rebuilds its repertoire from observation.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Without language, framing is limited to what demonstration emphasizes. No censorship is possible because no propositional content circulates. Norms govern action, not speech.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention is held by proximity and salience — the dramatic kill, the new technique. No amplification mechanism exists; information reaches only those present to witness it.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Imitation introduces drift with each transmission. No verification mechanism corrects accumulated error. Signal integrity degrades across generations; techniques simplify or shift without correction.

    D07. Moral Domain

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Justice is retributive and immediate — wrongs are avenged or forgiven in person. Fairness in sharing is enforced by social sanction. No abstract principle underlies conduct; rightness is what the group tolerates.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No codification exists. Conduct is regulated by custom transmitted through imitation and social pressure. Rules cannot be articulated, debated, or reformed; they drift with practice.

    Adjudication, Liability & Sanction: Disputes are resolved by dominance, mediation by respected individuals, or fission. Sanction is personal — shaming, beating, expulsion. No formal adjudication process exists.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Rights are not conceptualized. Protection derives from group membership and personal ties. Compliance is to group norms, enforced by the cost of transgression — loss of sharing, ostracism, violence.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongs against the weak or marginal may go unredressed if no advocate presses the claim. Impunity accrues to the strong. No institutional mechanism accumulates or clears grievance.

    D08. Intellectual Domain

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No inquiry exists in the propositional sense. Understanding is practical — the recognition of patterns in prey behavior, raw material quality, fire management — never articulated as theory.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Mousterian technology represents the applied knowledge ceiling: Levallois and discoid core reduction, prepared platforms, retouched flake tools. Regional variants — Quina, Ferrassie facies — indicate local traditions transmitted by imitation. Techniques are robust but not innovative within the window.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Knowledge is transmitted by observation and guided practice. Skill distribution is uneven; not every individual masters every technique. The knowledge stock is limited to what the current generation can demonstrate.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Techniques are lost when no practitioner survives to demonstrate them. Innovation is accidental and rare; most change is drift or loss. No mechanism recovers abandoned knowledge.

    D09. Cultural Domain

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No mythos can be articulated or transmitted without language. Identity is group membership, demonstrated by familiarity and shared practice. Memory is personal and dies with the rememberer.

    Legitimating Narrative & Ideology: No narrative legitimates the order because no narrative exists. Authority rests on demonstrated competence and presence, not story.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture is functional: stone tools, possibly wooden spears, fire-hardened points. No unambiguous symbolic expression — no figurative art, no ornament — is attested for Iberian Neanderthals in this window. Pigment use, if present, may serve functional or aesthetic purposes undistinguished from utility.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Without symbolic forms, exhaustion is meaningless. Techniques may become rote, but no meaning attaches to them that could hollow out.

    D10. Spiritual Domain

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No cosmology can be reconstructed. Whether Neanderthals conceptualized death, an afterlife, or forces beyond the visible is unknown. Deliberate burial at some European sites suggests awareness of death; Iberian evidence in this window is sparse.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: No ritual is attested. Repeated behaviors — hearth placement, discard patterns — are practical, not demonstrably sacred. The boundary between routine and rite cannot be drawn without symbolic evidence.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: No institutions or doctrine exist. Whatever spiritual intuitions individuals hold remain private and untransmitted.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Without transmissible belief, no diffusion or displacement occurs. Each individual's orientation is idiosyncratic and dies with them.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Disenchantment presupposes enchantment that can be lost. If Neanderthals hold sacred orientations, they cannot accumulate or erode collectively; each generation starts fresh, shaped only by what it can observe and infer.
