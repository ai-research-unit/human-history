    850,000 BCE – 750,000 BCE — The Gran Dolina TD6 Population

    Summary: A skeletal assemblage of at least six Homo antecessor individuals preserved in level TD6 of Gran Dolina, Atapuerca, with clear evidence of anthropophagous processing. The population sample spans infants to adults, offering a rare window into hominin biology and behaviour. Mode 1 lithics accompany the remains. The binding constraint is the fragility of small-group demography with high juvenile mortality. Margin against extinction is thin; population turnover is the dominant dynamic.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Sierra de Atapuerca forms a low karst ridge on the northern Meseta, penetrated by cave systems that channelled hominin and animal movement; surrounding uplands impose moderate friction on dispersal toward Atlantic and Mediterranean coasts.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: An interglacial or warm-interstadial interval prevails, with temperate continental conditions; seasonal contrast is moderate and the regime is relatively stable across the window, though longer-term glacial-interglacial oscillation continues in the background.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seismic activity is negligible; flood risk within the cave system is localised; wildfire and drought present only intermittent pressure on surrounding landscapes; exposure is low and absorptive capacity is ample.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Local limestone provides knappable raw material of adequate quality; faunal biomass on the Meseta supports foraging; floral resources are seasonally available; the endowment is sufficient for small bands with no sign of depletion.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Organic discard and lithic debris accumulate slowly in cave sediments; the landscape absorbs all outputs with negligible saturation; sink capacity is effectively unlimited at present population densities.
