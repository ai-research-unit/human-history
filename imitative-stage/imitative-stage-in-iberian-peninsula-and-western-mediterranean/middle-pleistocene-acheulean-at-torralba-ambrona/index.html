    500,000 BCE – 400,000 BCE — Middle Pleistocene Acheulean at Torralba-Ambrona

    Summary: Around the twin lake-margin sites of Torralba and Ambrona in the upper Jalón drainage, hominin groups repeatedly process elephant, horse, and bovid carcasses using refined Acheulean handaxes and cleavers; the snapshot captures a mature bifacial technology applied to large-fauna exploitation, with the binding constraint remaining the imitative channel—every reduction step, butchery stroke, and fire-tending behavior must be watched and copied, so the complex chaîne opératoire can spread no faster than embodied learners carry it.

    D01. Physical Domain

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Torralba-Ambrona basin lies in the Alto Jalón valley of Soria province, a high interior plateau (~1,100 m elevation) between the Iberian and Central ranges; lake-margin and marshy ground concentrate fauna and raw material, reducing search friction for foragers who range across open grassland-woodland mosaic.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: The window falls within a broadly interglacial phase; the basin experiences cool-temperate continental conditions with seasonal extremes—cold winters, warm summers—and enough moisture to sustain shallow lakes and gallery woodland along streams; climate variability is moderate, without catastrophic oscillation.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seismic and volcanic hazard negligible; flash flooding along drainages possible but localized; drought may seasonally shrink water sources, concentrating fauna but stressing foragers if prolonged.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Quartzite cobbles and chert nodules available in nearby river terraces and limestone outcrops supply lithic raw material; large herbivore herds—straight-tusked elephants, horses, aurochs, deer—provide high-yield carcasses when secured; firewood abundant in gallery woodland.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Bone beds and debitage accumulate at processing locales; at low population density the landscape absorbs refuse without saturation; no feedback pressure recorded.

    D02. Biological Domain

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic parasites and wound infection impose attrition; epidemic unlikely at sparse densities; mortality peaks at infancy, childbirth, and injury events.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Large-fauna exploitation delivers periodic caloric surpluses—elephant yields thousands of kilograms of meat and fat; skeletal robustness consistent with heavy physical labor and adequate, if episodic, nutrition.

    Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity: Group attendance aids recovery from minor injury; severe trauma or infection fatal; no pharmacological intervention beyond incidental plant use.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Multi-male, multi-female bands with inferred pair-bonds; female reproductive span limited; slow juvenile maturation extends dependency.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population sparse across the plateau; renewal rate marginally positive, vulnerable to local extinction; Torralba-Ambrona sites represent seasonal aggregation, not permanent occupation.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Juvenile dependency absorbs adult provisioning; few aged individuals survive; attrition cleared by mortality before heavy accumulation.

    D03. Economic Domain

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Subsistence combines plant gathering with large-fauna processing; the provisioning floor is close to carrying capacity but buffered by mobility and the windfall surplus a successful elephant kill provides.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Fire use attested by charcoal and burned bone at the sites, enabling cooking and extending edible hours; human muscle remains the principal motive force; energy capture per capita low but supplemented by thermal processing.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Division of labor emerges around knapping, butchery, and fire-tending; cooperation reciprocal and immediate; no coerced labor.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Refined Acheulean handaxes and cleavers—large, symmetrical, carefully thinned—represent peak Middle Pleistocene production; a single elephant yields surplus meat beyond immediate consumption, converted to body fat or shared out before spoilage.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No durable storage; surplus realized as immediate consumption, sharing, and body reserves; groups exposed to shortfall between kills.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Raw-material transport over 10–30 km implies logistical planning; no formalized exchange but inter-group contact probable at seasonal aggregations.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Absent; all value realized immediately.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Sharing distributes large-kill yields within the band; no extraction hierarchy; redistribution face-to-face and obligatory.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No durable wealth; possession extends only to carried tools, defended by proximity.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Bone beds at processing sites represent accumulated discard; no routing strategy beyond abandonment; waste disperses naturally.

    D04. Social Domain

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kin recognition structures the band; inheritance biological and imitative—skills pass from adults to juveniles through observation and practice.

    Association & Mutual Aid: Bands of 20–50 individuals cooperate in carcass processing and defense; mutual aid obligatory for survival; aggregations at rich sites may bring multiple bands into temporary contact.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Play among juveniles transmits motor skills; adult sociability expressed in grooming, proximity, and shared rest around fires.

    Stratification, Status & Group Structure: Status gradients track age, physical prowess, and knapping skill; no institutionalized hierarchy; dominance fluid and task-specific.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: None; coordination ad hoc and face-to-face.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: No organized coercion; handaxes and wooden spears double as weapons; interpersonal violence possible but unstructured.

    Discharge, Disorder & Decay: Friction resolved through avoidance, fission, or departure; no institutional process; grievance cleared by exit.

    D05. Emotional Domain

    Leadership & Personal Authority: Leadership situational—skilled knappers, successful hunters, and assertive individuals attract followership for specific tasks; authority dissolves when the task ends.

    Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance: Allegiance personal; legitimacy rests on demonstrated competence and presence, not office.

    Political Will & Collective Resolve: Collective will coalesces for hunts, butchery, defense, or movement; coordination dissolves when the task is complete.

    External Relations & Posture: Other bands encountered as potential mates or rivals; posture cautious; no sustained alliance or enmity.

    Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper: Affect expressed immediately within the band; trust confined to familiar faces; strangers approached with caution; mood rises with successful kills, falls with scarcity.

    Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will: Discontent resolved through departure; apathy does not accumulate where exit remains open.

    D06. Informational Domain

    Communication Accessibility: Shared code is gestural, postural, and vocal but pre-linguistic; imitation and demonstration transmit the complex chaîne opératoire; mutual intelligibility limited to co-residents and recently encountered neighbors.

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: All transmission face-to-face; reach limited to line of sight and earshot; velocity instantaneous within the band, nil beyond.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: No external record; knowledge persists only in living memory and embodied habit; death erases individual skill stocks.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: No formal framing; attention structures what is copied—successful demonstrations attract imitation.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention gravitates to competent knappers and butchers; amplification occurs only through repetition by multiple learners.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Each transmission drifts slightly; no verification mechanism; occasional correction occurs when a variant proves more effective in practice.

    D07. Moral Domain

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Reciprocity and kin loyalty anchor implicit norms; free-riding punished by social exclusion; fairness intuitive, not articulated.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No codified law; conduct regulated by custom internalized through observation and consequence.

    Adjudication, Liability & Sanction: Disputes resolved by dominance contest, avoidance, or fission; sanction ranges from coldness to expulsion; no third-party adjudication.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: No formal rights; protection derives from band membership; compliance is behavioral conformity.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongs unredressed accumulate as grudge or trigger fission; no institutional redress.

    D08. Intellectual Domain

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No formal inquiry; understanding procedural—embodied know-how without propositional theory.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: The refined Acheulean chaîne opératoire—platform preparation, bifacial thinning, symmetry maintenance, edge sharpening—represents the leading technical method; transmission requires extended apprenticeship through observation and guided practice; error correction is mimetic.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Knowledge stock sufficient for the lifeways practiced; skill concentrates in experienced adults; distribution widens as juveniles mature; no formal schooling.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Earlier Mode 1 techniques recede but persist for expedient tasks; knowledge decays at death and must be rebuilt by each learner; drift introduces variation across groups.

    D09. Cultural Domain

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No recoverable mythos; identity is the band—those one travels with, shares meat with, sleeps beside.

    Legitimating Narrative & Ideology: None articulated; legitimacy is presence and shared practice.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: The refined handaxe is the signature artifact—large, symmetrical, often exceeding strict functional necessity, suggesting aesthetic attention or display; no decoration, pigment, or personal ornament yet attested.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Forms remain fresh within the slow drift of imitative transmission; no exhaustion within this window.

    D10. Spiritual Domain

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No recoverable cosmology; orientation to the world is practical and immediate; existential outlook unknowable.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: No clear ritual evidence at Torralba-Ambrona; if present, it leaves no archaeological trace.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: None.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Not applicable; no system to diffuse.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: No prior enchantment to lose; dimension inert.
