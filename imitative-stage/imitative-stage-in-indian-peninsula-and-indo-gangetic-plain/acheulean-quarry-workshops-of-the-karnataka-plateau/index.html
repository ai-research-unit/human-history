    800000 BCE – 400000 BCE — Acheulean Quarry-Workshops of the Karnataka Plateau

    Summary: The Hunsgi-Baichbal landscape in northern Karnataka with over 200 documented Acheulean sites including the Isampur quarry-workshop (ESR dated 730 +/- 100 ka); a window into the complete chaîne opératoire from raw material extraction to finished handaxe; seasonal mobility patterns organized around perennial seep springs; the binding constraint is access to quality siliceous limestone at quarry outcrops; margin against environmental shock remains high with multiple water sources and alternative quarry locations; directional change shows stable technological tradition with incremental refinement over 400,000 years.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The northern Karnataka plateau presents a semi-arid peneplain at 400–600 m elevation; the Hunsgi and Baichbal valleys cut through Deccan basalt and limestone formations; spatial friction is low across open grassland and scrub; siliceous limestone outcrops provide concentrated raw material nodes that anchor site distribution.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Semi-arid conditions with seasonal monsoon rainfall; interannual variability in monsoon strength affects surface water availability; no dominant secular drift across the window; multiple glacial-interglacial cycles (MIS 19–12) impose background oscillation but perennial springs buffer local conditions.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seismic risk minimal on the stable Deccan craton; no volcanic hazard; drought is the primary risk during weak monsoon years; perennial seep springs at valley bottoms provide refugia; margin against acute shock is adequate given multiple fallback locations.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Siliceous limestone of excellent flaking quality outcrops at Isampur and comparable localities; stock is effectively inexhaustible at prevailing extraction rates; fauna includes medium and large mammals; perennial springs ensure year-round water access at key points.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Knapping debris accumulates at workshop sites — over 15,000 artifacts at Isampur alone — but dispersal across the open landscape is unconstrained; absorptive capacity remains unlimited; no sink saturation.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: No direct evidence; endemic parasites and waterborne pathogens inferred; background mortality typical of Pleistocene hominins with no indication of epidemic pressure.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: No skeletal remains from this landscape; adequate nutrition inferred from sustained multi-site occupation over 400,000 years; body robustness presumed consistent with Homo erectus or related hominins.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Sparsely attested; inferred pair-bonding and extended juvenile dependency; no direct evidence for mating systems or reproductive success rates.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density very low; positive demographic renewal inferred from continuous occupation across multiple sites; site density suggests repeated seasonal visits rather than permanent settlement; no crowding.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Minimal; small mobile groups absorb dependents without strain; no evidence of accumulated burden.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Foraging and probable hunting of fauna attracted to permanent water sources; plant foods from open woodland and grassland; margin above starvation adequate given low density and reliable spring-fed refugia.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle is the sole confirmed power source; fire use inferred but not directly attested at these sites; energy conversion limited by hominin physiology; per-capita work ceiling sufficient for subsistence and tool production.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Cooperative quarrying and knapping within kin-based bands; no coercion; task differentiation minimal — tool production is a shared skill; site revisitation implies coordinated seasonal scheduling.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production of handaxes and cleavers exceeds immediate consumption needs — caches and dense artifact scatters suggest anticipatory manufacture; this represents the earliest clear surplus production in the regional record; output does not strain raw material stock.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: Finished tools cached at workshops and transported to activity sites provide a durable reserve; no food storage; buffering achieved through tool stockpiling and landscape knowledge.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: No evidence of inter-group exchange; raw materials procured directly at quarry outcrops; finished tools transported within the seasonal range (10–30 km); logistics involve carrying blanks and finished pieces across the landscape.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of deferred exchange; cached tools function as stored value in labor but not as exchange tokens; reciprocity operates within immediate band context.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No coordinating authority; communal sharing of kills and gathered resources within the band; surplus tools presumably available to all band members.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No durable wealth concentration; tool caches are communal or abandoned rather than defended; no protective strategies attested.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Massive knapping debris accumulation at workshop sites — flakes, cores, failed blanks — remains where discarded; no routing decision required; waste externalized into unlimited absorptive capacity.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Trust radius limited to the mobile band; mutual aid strong within kin networks; site density suggests multiple bands may have used the landscape sequentially or overlapping, but inter-band relations are not directly attested.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: No evidence of stratification; skill in knapping may confer informal status; group identity tied to shared seasonal range and familiar individuals.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Inferred multi-generational bands with extended kin ties; no durable property beyond tools; inheritance inapplicable at this scale.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: The band is the sole organizational unit; seasonal aggregation at spring sites may bring multiple bands into proximity, but no evidence for formal association above band level.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Non-work time presumably spent in rest and social bonding; no material evidence for play; dense artifact scatters suggest extended stays at favored locations with time beyond subsistence tasks.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Disputes resolved within the band through direct interaction or fission; no formal mediation; standing instability minimal.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Record, Storage & Persistence: No external records — all knowledge held in living memory and transmitted through embodied demonstration; the quarry-workshop itself functions as a mnemonic anchor, with artifact scatters encoding accumulated practice but not readable as explicit record; persistence depends on generational overlap.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: No recoverable discourse norms; communication gestural and vocal but pre-linguistic; no evidence for taboos.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Reception limited to direct observation of knapping demonstrations; attention shaped by practical utility; no amplification beyond individual repetition and teaching.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Signal-to-noise ratio high in face-to-face demonstration; verification immediate through stone testing; no information overload; decay minimal where transmission succeeds.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No formal law; conduct governed by unwritten behavioral expectations enforced through social pressure and exclusion.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: No third-party adjudication; disputes settled directly or through group fission; liability personal and immediate.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: No formal rights; autonomy constrained by group norms; compliance burden negligible.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongdoing addressed immediately or absorbed through social distance; no accumulation of unredressed grievance; impunity gap minimal.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No formal inquiry; practical knowledge of raw material quality, fracture mechanics, and landscape accumulated through observation; knappers distinguish superior from inferior stone by testing; no explanatory paradigm recoverable.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Complete Acheulean chaîne opératoire attested at Isampur: raw material selection at outcrop, initial reduction, blank production, shaping through hard hammer percussion, and finishing; technique transmitted through demonstration and guided imitation; method stable with gradual refinement toward smaller and more symmetric forms over the window.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Knowledge transmitted through observation and practice at quarry-workshops; skill distribution presumably universal among adults; novice learning inferred from presence of failed pieces and irregular attempts; stock includes raw material assessment, reduction sequences, and landscape navigation.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Technique highly conservative; innovation minimal; knowledge loss occurs through transmission failure; no paradigm shifts to shed; decay rate low given redundancy and repeated site use.
