    Planning: Imitative in The Southern African Plateau — 2,000,000 BCE – 150,000 BCE

    Age Constraint (FIXED)

    Imitative: The substrate of public life before language: everything a group knows held in living heads and passed only by showing and copying, hand guiding hand — dominant by default until speech becomes the primary medium.

    This constraint governs every article: knowledge transmission is strictly imitative (gestural, demonstrative), not linguistic. The Informational domain must reflect this — no symbolic encoding, no speech-based transmission. All cultural learning proceeds through observation and emulation.

    Shared Evidence Base

    Sterkfontein (Member 5) (~2.0–1.5 Ma)

    Location: Cradle of Humankind, Gauteng, South Africa Dating: Member 5 dates to ~2.0–1.5 Ma (paleomagnetic, biostratigraphy, cosmogenic nuclides) Evidence: Oldowan artifacts (cores, flakes, choppers); Homo habilis and early Homo erectus fossils (StW 53, disputed); transition from Australopithecus-bearing deposits Significance: Documents shift from Australopithecus-dominated to Homo-dominated faunal assemblages with stone tool association Sources: Kuman & Clarke 2000; Granger et al. 2015 Contested Points: Dating of specific fossils remains debated; hominin taxonomy under revision

    Swartkrans (~1.8–1.0 Ma)

    Location: Cradle of Humankind, Gauteng, South Africa Dating: Members 1-3: ~1.8–1.0 Ma (ESR, paleomagnetism, biostratigraphy) Evidence: Homo erectus (SK 847, SK 15) and Paranthropus robustus remains; Acheulean artifacts in upper levels; bone tools (digging sticks); burnt bones suggesting fire use Significance: Co-occurrence of Homo and Paranthropus; earliest bone tool evidence; possible controlled fire Sources: Brain 1993; Backwell & d'Errico 2001; Brain & Sillen 1988 Contested Points: Fire evidence disputed (may be natural); bone tool interpretation debated

    Wonderwerk Cave (~2.0 Ma – recent)

    Location: Northern Cape, South Africa Dating: Stratum 12-10: ~2.0–1.0 Ma; fire evidence at ~1.0 Ma Evidence: Long Acheulean sequence; burnt bone and plant material at ~1.0 Ma; continuous occupation; Oldowan at base Significance: Possibly earliest fire use in a cave context globally; exceptional stratigraphic continuity Sources: Berna et al. 2012; Chazan et al. 2008 Contested Points: Dating precision; whether fire use is controlled or natural at earliest levels

    Kathu Pan 1 (~500–300 ka)

    Location: Northern Cape, South Africa Dating: ~500–300 ka (OSL, ESR) Evidence: Large Acheulean assemblage with refined handaxes; hafted spear points (Kathu Pan 1 points); spring-fed pan environment Significance: Evidence of hafting technology; sophisticated hunting equipment; water-focused land use Sources: Wilkins et al. 2012; Wilkins & Chazan 2012 Contested Points: Hafting interpretation based on use-wear; dating range broad

    Canteen Kopje (~1.0–0.3 Ma)

    Location: Northern Cape, South Africa Dating: Multiple phases spanning ~1.0–0.3 Ma Evidence: Extensive Acheulean surface and stratified deposits; Victoria West cores; handaxe production sequence documented Significance: Type site for Victoria West prepared-core technique; long Acheulean presence Sources: McNabb & Beaumont 2011; Sharon 2009 Contested Points: Stratigraphic mixing at some levels

    Fauresmith Industry (~400–250 ka)

    Location: Type site at Fauresmith, Free State; distributed across interior plateau Dating: ~400–250 ka (OSL, associated fauna) Evidence: Bifacial handaxes combined with prepared-core (proto-Levallois) technique; transitional between Acheulean and MSA Significance: Regional transitional industry bridging ESA and MSA; indigenous technological development Sources: Porat et al. 2010; Herries 2011 Contested Points: Whether distinct industry or late Acheulean variant; dating imprecise at many sites

    Florisbad (~260–250 ka)

    Location: Free State, South Africa Dating: ~259 ka (ESR on tooth enamel) Evidence: Partial cranium with archaic Homo sapiens features; associated MSA artifacts; spring site with excellent preservation Significance: Key fossil documenting archaic-to-modern human transition in Africa; early MSA association Sources: Grün et al. 1996; Kuman et al. 1999 Contested Points: Taxonomic assignment debated (Homo heidelbergensis vs. archaic H. sapiens)

    Early MSA Sites (~300–150 ka)

    Key Sites: Kathu Pan, Wonderwerk Cave upper levels, Florisbad, various open-air locales Evidence: Prepared cores (Levallois), points, blades; reduction in handaxe frequency; hafting evidence Pattern: Gradual transition rather than abrupt replacement; regional variants developing Sources: Porat et al. 2010; Wilkins 2020 Contested Points: Timing of transition varies by site; definition of MSA onset debated

    Paleoenvironment

    Southern African Plateau: Elevated interior (1000-1500m); savanna-woodland mosaic; seasonal rainfall Climate Oscillations: Glacial-interglacial cycles affecting rainfall patterns; wetter interglacials supported denser vegetation; drier glacials expanded grasslands Water Sources: Springs, pans, and seasonal rivers as focal points for hominin activity Sources: Partridge et al. 1999; Marean 2015

    Faunal Context

    Lower Pleistocene: African large mammal fauna; Megantereon, Dinofelis as predators; abundant bovids and equids Middle Pleistocene: Gradual modernization; extant species appearing; megafauna declining Hominin Predators: Leopard, hyena, and large cats as threats and competitors Sources: Brain 1981; Faith 2014
