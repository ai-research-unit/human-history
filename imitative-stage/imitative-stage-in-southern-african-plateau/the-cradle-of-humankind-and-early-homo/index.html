    2,000,000 BCE – 1,700,000 BCE — The Cradle of Humankind and Early Homo

    Summary: A snapshot of early Homo populations at Sterkfontein and Swartkrans during the early Pleistocene, where dolomitic caves preserve the earliest evidence of the genus Homo on the Southern African Plateau alongside robust Paranthropus. The binding constraint is the absolute dependence on imitative transmission for all knowledge of tool-making, foraging, and predator avoidance — nothing can be taught except by showing, nothing learned except by watching and copying, hand guiding hand.

    D01. Physical Domain

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Cradle of Humankind lies in a dolomitic karst landscape northwest of present-day Johannesburg, where soluble bedite rock has dissolved over millions of years to form cave systems that trap sediment, bone, and artifact. Sterkfontein and Swartkrans sit within a few kilometers of each other in the Blaaubank River valley, connected by seasonal watercourses that carve through low ridges. Movement across the plateau is relatively unimpeded — open savanna and light woodland offer few natural barriers — but the caves themselves create focal points, drawing hominins for shelter, water seepage, and accumulated prey carcasses.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: The early Pleistocene plateau experiences a subtropical regime with marked wet and dry seasons, though cooler and somewhat drier than the preceding Pliocene. Orbital forcing produces oscillations between more humid and more arid phases across millennia. Temperatures remain warm year-round, with frost rare at these elevations. Seasonal rainfall concentrates growth and animal movement, creating predictable pulses of abundance followed by leaner months.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: The karst terrain itself poses the primary hazard: vertical cave shafts act as natural death traps, and the fossil record at Sterkfontein includes hominins who fell or were dragged into sinkholes. Predation pressure is intense — leopards, saber-toothed cats, and hyenas share the landscape and actively hunt or scavenge hominin bodies. Wildfires sweep the grasslands seasonally, though lightning ignition remains the only source, uncontrolled and uncontrived.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: The dolomitic geology provides no flakeable stone directly; quartz pebbles from river gravels and quartzite cobbles from outcrops several kilometers distant serve as the raw material base for Oldowan tools. Water seeps through cave walls, creating reliable but limited sources. The surrounding savanna-woodland mosaic supports diverse herbivore populations — antelope, equids, suids — and edible plants, tubers, and fruits, though recovery requires wide daily ranging.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: The landscape absorbs hominin impact without measurable strain. Population densities are extremely low — perhaps a few dozen individuals across hundreds of square kilometers — and the material throughput of Oldowan technology leaves only scattered stone flakes and abandoned cores, biologically inert and absorbed into sediment over millennia. No saturation, no feedback.

    D02. Biological Domain

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic parasites, enteric infections from surface water, and wounds from predator encounters or falls set a high background mortality. Life expectancy at birth is likely under twenty years. Infant and juvenile mortality dominates; few individuals survive to what would later be considered old age. The population lives close to the replacement threshold.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Early Homo bodies show increased stature and reduced gut-to-brain proportion compared to australopithecines, suggesting dietary shifts toward higher-quality foods — meat, marrow, and fat — made accessible by Oldowan tools. Nutritional stress remains common, inscribed in dental enamel hypoplasias and seasonal body-mass fluctuations, but the species is viable and expanding.

    Care, Healing & Recovery Capacity: Care consists of what can be shown and copied: wound-licking, rest, the presence of kin. There is no medicine, no pharmacopoeia, no taught treatment — only the behaviors a watcher can imitate from a doer. Recovery depends on individual constitution and the group's willingness to slow for an injured member; severe injury means death.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Sexual maturation occurs by the mid-teens; pair bonds, if they exist, are fluid and enforced only by proximity and social tolerance. Females bear dependent young at intervals of several years, investing heavily in each offspring. Weaning extends longer than in other apes, reflecting the learning load that imitative transmission imposes — the young must watch and copy for years before competence.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density is vanishingly low by later standards — perhaps one person per ten to twenty square kilometers of usable range. Group sizes likely range from a dozen to a few dozen individuals, constrained by the foraging radius and the carrying capacity of seasonal resource patches. Replacement rates hover near unity; the population is stable to slowly expanding.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: The dependency load is light in absolute numbers but heavy in relative cost: every non-productive juvenile and every injured adult draws on the group's margin. The short lifespan means few aged dependents, but orphaned young are common. Groups that lose too many adults to predation or accident fall below the threshold of viability.

    D03. Economic Domain

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Subsistence is immediate-return foraging: daily collection of plant foods, opportunistic scavenging of carcasses, and occasional small-game capture. The Oldowan toolkit — cores, choppers, and sharp-edged flakes — enables access to marrow and meat from scavenged bones, a caloric windfall otherwise locked away. The provisioning floor is precarious; a few days of failed foraging threaten survival.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: All energy comes from uncooked food and human muscle. Fire is not yet controlled; raw meat, tubers, and fruit provide the caloric input. The energetic ceiling is low, and most activity goes to foraging itself. There is no stored surplus, no deferred consumption, no accumulated wealth.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Division of labor is minimal and unformalized — individuals forage, process, and consume largely as they can, with some sharing of large carcasses when acquisition is visible to the group. There is no coercion beyond social pressure; no one can compel labor from another except through physical dominance or the withdrawal of association.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production is limited to Oldowan tool-making: cobble selection, percussion, flake removal. Each tool represents minutes of work; there is no investment horizon longer than the immediate task. Surplus does not exist — nothing is produced beyond the moment's need, and nothing is stored.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No storage. Food spoils within hours or days; there are no granaries, no dried provisions, no cached reserves. The only buffer is body fat, accumulated in times of plenty and drawn down in scarcity. Risk pooling consists of sharing a kill or a find with those present.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: No exchange in any formal sense. Stone raw material is carried from source to use-site, sometimes several kilometers, but this is individual procurement, not trade. There is no network, no specialization, no interdependence beyond the face-to-face group.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No value storage, no credit, no deferred exchange. Reciprocity is immediate and unreckoned — help given now may be returned later, but there is no accounting, no obligation tracked, no medium of promise.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No surplus to extract. Distribution of large carcasses follows who is present and who can take a share; dominance and proximity determine access, not rule or institution.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No wealth accumulation. Personal possessions are minimal — perhaps a carried cobble, a stick — and easily replaced. There is nothing to inherit, nothing to defend, nothing to transmit.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Waste is organic and disperses without trace beyond discarded flakes and cores, which enter the sedimentary record. No pollution, no accumulation, no routing problem.

    D04. Social Domain

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship is recognized through proximity and repeated association — mothers and offspring, siblings who grew together — but there is no named system, no codified rule, no inheritance of anything beyond learned behavior. The family is the nursing dyad extended outward to those who remain.

    Association & Mutual Aid: The band is the unit of association: a small, fluid group of individuals who forage together, sleep in proximity, and provide mutual aid through presence and copying. Aid consists of tolerating a companion's proximity to a food source, watching for predators, and, perhaps, slowing for the injured. There is no formal structure, no institution, no named role.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Juveniles play — chasing, wrestling, object manipulation — and through play learn the motor patterns they will later apply to foraging and tool-making. Adults rest when sated, groom, and engage in whatever social behaviors the moment affords. There is no organized festivity, no ritual calendar, no bounded leisure.

    Stratification, Status & Group Structure: Status exists only as dominance: larger, stronger, or more assertive individuals take precedence at carcasses and sleeping spots. There is no class, no caste, no permanent rank beyond what the moment enforces. Leadership, if any, is situational — who moves first, who others follow.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administration. No institution. No office, no role, no procedure. The group acts by individual decision aggregated through imitation and proximity, not through any coordinating structure.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Coercion is personal and immediate: physical threat, actual violence, expulsion from the group. There is no organized force, no weapon specialized for intraspecific conflict, no apparatus of control. Conflict resolution is dominance or departure.

    Discharge, Disorder & Decay: Social friction discharges through avoidance, submission, or dispersal — an aggrieved individual moves away, forms or joins another band, or dies. There is no grievance that outlasts the moment, no feud that can be transmitted, no institutional sclerosis because there is no institution.

    D05. Emotional Domain

    Public Sentiment & Behavioral Temper: The emotional baseline is vigilance: constant scanning for predators, for food, for the movements of companions. Trust extends to the familiar — those who have been present, whose behavior is known — and suspicion greets the stranger. Mood shifts with satiation, danger, and social harmony; there is no public sentiment in the collective sense, only aggregated individual states.

    Leadership & Personal Authority: Leadership is situational and bodily: the individual who moves first toward a resource, who faces down a threat, who others choose to follow in the moment. There is no office, no title, no authority that persists beyond the action.

    Legitimacy as Lived Allegiance: Legitimacy does not exist as a concept. Allegiance is to companions, not to a role or a rule. The group coheres because its members stay together, not because they believe in the group's right to exist.

    Political Will & Collective Resolve: Collective action is emergent: when one moves, others may follow; when danger appears, the group may flee together or scatter. There is no deliberation, no plan, no rallying. Will is individual, and collective outcomes are aggregates.

    External Relations & Posture: Other bands are encountered occasionally, met with wariness or curiosity, and either avoided or briefly joined. There is no diplomacy, no alliance, no territorial claim that can be communicated or defended in language. Boundaries are enforced by presence and avoidance, not by treaty.

    Disaffection, Apathy & Spent Will: Distress, frustration, and grief are felt and expressed — vocalizations, agitation, withdrawal — but cannot be narrated or named. Spent will appears as lethargy, disengagement, death. There is no despair that can be transmitted, only the visible slowing of the body.

    D06. Informational Domain

    Communication Accessibility: Communication is gestural, postural, and vocal but not linguistic. Calls signal alarm, aggression, affiliation, and perhaps the presence of food, but they do not name, do not reference the absent, do not compose. Meaning is iconic and immediate: the gesture that points, the cry that warns, the posture that threatens. Understanding is limited to what can be shown.

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: The channel is face-to-face, line-of-sight, within earshot. Nothing can be sent ahead, nothing transmitted beyond the group's immediate range. Information moves at the speed of bodies.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: There is no record, no storage, no persistence beyond living memory. What is known dies with the knower unless it has been shown to and copied by another. Knowledge is fragile, drifts with each transmission, and cannot be checked against an original.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: There is no framing in the narrative sense, no censorship, no discourse. Communication is stimulus and response, not argument and persuasion.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention is drawn by movement, novelty, and social gaze. What the group watches, the individual notices. Amplification is physical — the repeated gesture, the persistent demonstration — not rhetorical.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Signal is immediate and short-range; noise is environmental — rustling, wind, distance. There is no corruption of record because there is no record, only the drift inherent in copying from a copy.

    D07. Moral Domain

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: There is no codified morality, but there is behavioral regularity: sharing with those present, punishing cheaters through aggression or exclusion, reciprocating aid. These patterns are emotional and social, not reasoned or articulated. Justice is the immediate reaction to transgression.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No law, no codification, no regulation. Behavioral norms are enforced by reaction, not by rule.

    Adjudication, Liability & Sanction: Disputes are settled by dominance, avoidance, or violence. There is no third-party adjudication, no assigned liability, no proportioned sanction — only the immediate consequence of the moment.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: There are no rights, no protections, no compliance burden. The individual has what they can hold and loses what they cannot defend.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrong is unredressed when the transgressor is stronger or when the victim is dead or departed. Impunity is the default for the dominant; there is no appeal, no memory of injustice beyond the aggrieved.

    D08. Intellectual Domain

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: There is no inquiry, no theory, no paradigm. Understanding is procedural and imitative: how to do, not why it works. The world is engaged, not explained.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Technical knowledge consists of the Oldowan chaîne opératoire: selecting a cobble, striking it at the correct angle, producing a usable flake. This is learned by watching and doing, corrected by failure, refined by repetition. It is the sum of applied intellect in this window.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Education is imitation. The young watch the skilled, attempt what they see, fail, and try again. Knowledge stock is limited to what living individuals carry — there is no archive, no curriculum, no institution. Skill is distributed unevenly by aptitude and opportunity; the clumsy never master what the dexterous learn quickly.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Knowledge decays when carriers die without demonstrating, when learners fail to copy accurately, when the context that gave a skill meaning disappears. There is no way to recover what is lost, no record to consult, no teacher who can explain.

    D09. Cultural Domain

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: There is no mythos, no origin narrative, no foundational identity that can be articulated. Memory is individual and embodied — the familiar place, the known companion — not collective or storied. Identity is being-with, not being-from.

    Legitimating Narrative & Ideology: There is no legitimating narrative, no ideology, no argument for the order. The order is simply how things are done, copied from those who did before.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture is the Oldowan toolkit: cores, flakes, choppers. There is no decoration, no symbolic mark, no object made for meaning alone. The artifact is purely functional, its form dictated by percussion physics, not aesthetic choice. There is no art.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: There is no form to exhaust, no meaning to decay. The Oldowan persists because it works and because there is nothing else. Innovation is glacially slow, measured in hundreds of thousands of years.

    D10. Spiritual Domain

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: There is no cosmology, no existential outlook that can be communicated. The world is immediate, present, unreflected. There is no known afterlife, no named spirit, no explanatory frame beyond the senses.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: There is no ritual, no devotion, no sacred practice. Repeated behaviors exist — sleeping in the same place, returning to the same water source — but they are habitual, not ritualized; functional, not sacred.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: There are no religious institutions, no doctrinal authority, no clergy, no orthodoxy. The spiritual dimension is empty of content in this window.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: There is nothing to diffuse, nothing to syncretize, nothing to displace. The category does not yet apply.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: There is no disenchantment because there was no enchantment. The residue is not absence but non-occurrence — the spiritual dimension remains unfilled, a capacity not yet exercised.
