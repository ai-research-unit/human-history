    1,700,000 BCE – 1,400,000 BCE — Emergence of Acheulean Technology on the Plateau

    Summary: A long-term trend documenting the gradual transition from Oldowan to Acheulean technology across the Southern African Plateau as Homo erectus populations expand and refine bifacial reduction techniques; the window spans approximately three hundred thousand years of incremental elaboration in which handaxes and cleavers progressively supplement and displace simpler core-and-flake assemblages; the binding constraint remains the imitative transmission of increasingly complex motor sequences, which sets the pace of technological diffusion; the system holds ample margin against environmental variability through dietary flexibility and spatial mobility; the direction of change is toward greater technological sophistication, population consolidation, and broader landscape occupation.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The interior plateau extends across present-day Gauteng, Free State, and Northern Cape at elevations of 1,000 to 1,500 meters; dolomitic karst in the northeast provides cave sites while the western interior offers open savanna with seasonal pans and river courses; movement is facilitated by gentle topography and abundant water sources during interglacials.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Early Pleistocene conditions oscillate between wetter interglacials supporting woodland expansion and drier glacial phases favoring open grassland; rainfall ranges from 400 to 800 mm annually depending on phase; secular trend is toward increasing amplitude of glacial-interglacial cycles, widening the environmental band populations must track.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Acute hazards remain minimal; the cratonic plateau is seismically stable and volcanically inactive; drought cycles occur on multi-decadal scales but populations buffer through mobility; fire is recurrent but non-catastrophic; margin against environmental shock is wide.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Lithic raw materials remain abundant: quartzite, dolomite, chert, and banded ironstone provide suitable blanks for Acheulean biface production; water availability fluctuates seasonally but springs and pans ensure perennial access at focal points; large mammal biomass supports scavenging and hunting; the resource stock is not approaching depletion.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Knapping debris accumulates at production sites but the open landscape absorbs refuse without measurable impact; no sink saturation occurs; environmental degradation from hominin activity is negligible relative to landscape scale.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic parasites and bacterial infections persist at background levels; predation remains a significant mortality factor, particularly from large cats, hyenas, and crocodiles at water sources; epidemic disease is not attested; mortality rate is high but stable.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Homo erectus exhibits increased body size and reduced sexual dimorphism relative to earlier hominins, indicating improved nutritional access; meat consumption increases with hunting capability; physiological vitality is trending upward across the window.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Reduced sexual dimorphism suggests shifting mating dynamics, possibly toward increased male investment; pair-bonding remains weakly developed; physiological fertility is stable; no major selection pressure shifts are evident within the window.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density remains low but is gradually increasing as Homo erectus expands across the plateau; birth rates exceed death rates marginally, allowing slow demographic growth; age structure remains young-skewed; internal migration disperses population across newly occupied terrain.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Juvenile dependency load persists given extended hominin childhood; elderly dependents remain rare due to high adult mortality; the productive cohort absorbs dependency without strain; no significant demographic drag is evident.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Subsistence combines plant foraging with increased reliance on animal protein from scavenging and cooperative hunting; the population operates well below carrying capacity; household pooling expands slightly as cooperative hunting requires larger group coordination.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle remains the sole energy source; fire use is not reliably attested until the end of the window (contested evidence at Wonderwerk Cave); caloric return per foraging hour is improving as hunting efficiency increases; per-capita work ceiling rises modestly.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Division of labor remains informal, structured by age and sex; cooperative hunting introduces rudimentary task coordination; no coercive organization exists; participation is voluntary and driven by shared benefit.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Acheulean biface production requires longer reduction sequences and greater skill investment than Oldowan technique; handaxes and cleavers serve as multipurpose tools for butchery and woodworking; no surplus accumulation beyond immediate tool needs occurs.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No storage technology exists; landscape knowledge serves as the primary buffer against shortage; body fat reserves and dietary flexibility provide resilience; no institutional reserves are present.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Raw material transport distances increase with Acheulean technology, suggesting expanded ranging behavior; no formalized exchange exists; logistics remain constrained by individual carrying capacity during daily and multi-day foraging circuits.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No value storage medium exists; reciprocity remains immediate and situational; deferred exchange obligations may operate within bands through remembered sharing history but leave no direct evidence.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No authority extracts or redistributes surplus; sharing of large animal carcasses occurs within the group, likely structured by dominance and kinship; allocation decisions are immediate and visible.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No wealth concentration exists; tools are individually made and used; positional advantage accrues to skilled knappers through social reputation but does not constitute inheritable property.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Lithic debitage accumulates at knapping sites; bone and organic refuse scatter at kill and processing locations; all waste is dispersed without deliberate routing; no recycling or secondary use is evident beyond opportunistic flake reuse.
