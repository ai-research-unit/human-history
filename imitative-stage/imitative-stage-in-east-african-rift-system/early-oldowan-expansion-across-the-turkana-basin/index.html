    2,340,000 BCE – 2,000,000 BCE — Early Oldowan Expansion across the Turkana Basin

    Summary: A long-term trend tracking the geographic spread of Oldowan core-and-flake technology from its Ethiopian highland locus southward into the Kenyan Rift over three hundred millennia; the Turkana Basin — with Lokalalei 2C dated to 2.34 Ma — documents this dispersal through preserved artifact scatters in fluvio-lacustrine sediments; the binding constraint is raw material availability at knapping sites along shifting lake and river margins; the system carries wide margin against shock given low population density and extensive habitat; the principal direction of change is geographic extension of lithic technique without marked typological innovation.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Turkana Basin lies within the northern Kenyan Rift, bounded by Ethiopian highlands to the north and volcanic uplands to east and west; transit follows riparian corridors and lake margins where water and toolstone co-occur; the basin floor imposes minimal friction, but escarpment flanks and seasonal watercourses segment movement.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Warm, semi-arid to arid climate with marked wet-dry seasonality; interannual variability high as orbital precession modulates African monsoon penetration; secular oscillation between lake-high and lake-low phases without sustained unidirectional drift across the window.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Active volcanism in adjacent highlands produces periodic tephra falls that preserve stratigraphic markers; no catastrophic eruption documented within the window; flood pulses along the Omo inflow create local disturbance but hominin exposure remains low.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Abundant lava cobbles, basalt, and phonolite outcrops provide knappable stone along drainage networks; biotic resources concentrated at lake-margin ecotones; endowment effectively unlimited relative to extraction rate.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Negligible lithic debris and organic residue against an open savanna landscape; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited; no detectable degradation.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Pathogen regime poorly attested; probable endemic parasitic and bacterial loads consistent with equatorial African environments; mortality baseline inferred high given short adult lifespans evident in later hominin skeletal samples.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Diet includes plant foods, scavenged meat, and marrow; nutritional adequacy sufficient for survival but marginal during dry-season troughs; physiological robustness inferred from skeletal durability of contemporary hominins.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Moderate sexual dimorphism suggests polygynous or flexible mating; reproductive biology remains australopithecine-grade with relatively small-brained infants and shorter interbirth intervals than later Homo.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density extremely low — scattered small groups across the basin; birth-to-death ratio near replacement; turnover rapid with short generation times; demographic trend is slow expansion as Oldowan-using groups colonize new localities.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Short adult lifespans limit elderly dependency; orphaned juveniles absorbed within small groups; dependency load light relative to productive capacity.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Immediate-return foraging with daily acquisition of plant foods, insects, and scavenged fauna; provisioning floor close to metabolic minimum during seasonal lows but generally met.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle is sole power source; fire use not attested; energy capture limited to direct biomass intake and kinetic work of knapping; per-capita work ceiling low.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: No division beyond age-sex differences; labor mobilized by proximity and observation; coercion minimal — cooperation maintained through co-presence and imitative learning.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Stone tool production generates small, portable flake assemblages; no durable surplus beyond carried cores; output ceiling matched to immediate need.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No food storage; marrow-bearing bones provide short-term caloric reserve; buffering capacity negligible; groups move in response to resource depletion.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: No evidence of inter-group exchange; raw materials acquired locally or within day-range transport; logistics limited to individual carrying.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of deferred exchange; reciprocity operates within immediate co-residential group without formalized obligation.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No coordinating authority; food sharing within group is informal and situational, governed by proximity and begging.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No durable property accumulation; carried toolkit and bodily strength are sole assets; no defense mechanism beyond retention.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Flake debris and bone fragments discarded at processing sites; dispersed across landscape with effectively unlimited absorptive capacity; no routing decision required.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Trust radius confined to co-residential group of perhaps ten to thirty individuals; mutual aid expressed through food-sharing and joint vigilance; radius stable.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Minimal stratification beyond age and sex; status derived from physical competence; no recoverable markers of group identity distinction.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship structure unrecoverable; probable mother-offspring bonds and sibling associations; no material inheritance.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: No formal associations; coordination achieved through spatial proximity and imitative cueing; associational fabric effectively null above family-level.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Juvenile play and social grooming inferred from primate baselines; structured festivity absent; sociability expressed through physical co-presence.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Disputes resolved through avoidance, physical contest, or group fission; no mediating institution; unmetabolized tension discharged by spatial separation.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: No centralized authority; situational leadership by dominant individuals during movement or defense; power diffuse and ephemeral.

    Succession & Power Transfer: No formalized succession; dominance shifts with physical condition and coalitional support; transfer continuous and unritualized.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: No recoverable legitimating narrative; authority rests on immediate physical and social standing, not symbolic justification.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administrative apparatus; coordination achieved through direct observation and co-movement; institutional capacity null.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: Participation in group decisions by presence and action; no formal channels; mobilization occurs through imitative following.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Inter-group relations sparse given low density; encounters likely rare and resolved by avoidance or brief contest; no alliance or treaty structure.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Stone tools serve as weapons alongside unmodified objects; no specialized weaponry; force capacity individual and situational.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Internal disorder limited by small group size and fission option; external threat from carnivore guild (Megantereon, Dinofelis) significant; hominin predation risk moderate.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information travels only at walking pace through direct demonstration; reach limited to line-of-sight and acoustic range of calls; transmission strictly gestural and imitative.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: No external record; all knowledge held in living memory and motor habit; persistence dependent on continuous social learning across generations.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: No symbolic framing or discourse; communication limited to gestural and vocal signals without propositional content; no censorship mechanism.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention governed by proximity and salience of demonstrated action; no amplification beyond immediate observation.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Signal fidelity depends on clarity of demonstration and motor imitation; degradation possible through imprecise copying; verification occurs through functional success of produced tool.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: No recoverable moral code; fairness intuitions inferred from primate baselines — response to inequity, coalition-based reciprocity — but unattested directly.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No formal law; behavioral norms enforced informally through social pressure and physical sanction; regulatory evolution null.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: No third-party adjudication; disputes settled by direct contest or avoidance; liability concept absent.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: No formalized rights; protection derived from group membership; compliance burden effectively null in absence of codified rules.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongs unredressed beyond immediate physical retaliation or social exclusion; impunity the default condition; no accumulating backlog in absence of formal expectation.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No theoretical inquiry; knowledge consists of motor routines and environmental familiarity; no explanatory paradigm.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Core-and-flake reduction technique — unifacial and simple bifacial cores, hard-hammer percussion — constitutes the applied method; replicable through imitative learning.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Knowledge transmitted by observation and guided practice; skill distribution likely uneven, with some individuals more proficient; stock stable and slowly accumulating through geographic spread.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Technique loss possible through demographic bottleneck or transmission failure; no evidence of decay within the window; error cleared by functional failure of poorly made tools.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No recoverable mythos or narrative memory; identity, if any, defined by co-residence and kinship rather than symbolic content.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture limited to utilitarian stone tools; no ornament, pigment, or non-functional modification; aesthetic dimension null.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Mood unrecoverable; behavioral disposition inferred as cautious, reactive, and oriented toward immediate resource acquisition.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: No symbolic forms to exhaust; material tradition stable and functional; meaning decay inapplicable.
