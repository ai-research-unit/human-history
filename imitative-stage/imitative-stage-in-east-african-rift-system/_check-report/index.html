    Layer 2 Flags (Require Arbitration)

    FLAG-01: Article 2 — Fejej Chronology

    File: emergence-of-the-oldowan-at-gona.md Issue: Summary mentions Fejej among sites "by the window's close," but Fejej is dated ~2.0 Ma while the article window ends 2,300,000 BCE. Evidence: Fejej ~2.0 Ma (de Heinzelin et al. 1999). Options: - Remove Fejej from article scope - Extend window end date to 2,000,000 BCE (would overlap with Article 3)

    FLAG-02: Article 4 — Bed I Start Date

    File: olduvai-gorge-bed-i-and-the-oldowan-lifeway.md Issue: Window starts 2,000,000 BCE, but current Bed I dating places the base at ~1.87 Ma. Evidence: Deino 2012; Domínguez-Rodrigo et al. 2014 place Bed I at 1.87–1.79 Ma. Options: - Adjust start date to 1,870,000 BCE - Retain 2,000,000 BCE as conservative upper bound

    FLAG-03: Article 7 — "Upper Olduvai Beds I–II" Imprecise

    File: developed-oldowan-and-technological-coexistence.md Issue: The phrase "upper Olduvai Beds I–II" is imprecise for the 1,700,000–1,400,000 BCE window; Bed I ends ~1.79 Ma. Evidence: Standard Olduvai stratigraphy places Bed II beginning at 1.79 Ma. Options: - Revise to "upper Bed I and Bed II" - Clarify that the window spans the Bed I–II transition

    FLAG-04: Article 10 — E. recki Decline Timing

    File: late-lower-pleistocene-technological-conservatism.md Issue: P01 states E. recki populations "showing early signs of decline toward window's end" (600,000 BCE), but E. recki persisted until ~400–300 ka. Evidence: Faith 2014 documents E. recki persistence into Middle Pleistocene. Options: - Remove "early signs of decline" phrasing - Specify that decline is localized or incipient rather than definitive

    FLAG-05: Article 14 — 315,000 BCE Start Date

    File: early-homo-sapiens-emergence-in-the-rift-system.md Issue: The 315,000 BCE start date appears to reference Jebel Irhoud (Morocco), which is outside the East African Rift geographic constraint. The earliest Rift-specific H. sapiens evidence is Omo-Kibish. Evidence: Hublin et al. 2017 (Jebel Irhoud) is Morocco; Omo-Kibish is the Rift-specific baseline. Options: - Adjust start date to 233,000 BCE (Vidal et al. 2022 Omo-Kibish redating) - Retain 315,000 BCE with explicit note that this references pan-African morphological transition visible through comparison

    FLAG-06: Article 16 — Omo-Kibish Dating Outdated

    File: the-omo-kibish-population-and-late-pleistocene-adaptation.md Issue: Window uses 195,000 BCE (McDougall et al. 2005), but Vidal et al. 2022 redates Omo-Kibish I to ~233 ka. Evidence: Vidal et al. 2022, Nature. Options: - Update start date to 233,000 BCE - Retain 195,000 BCE as traditional date with note on dating controversy
