    2,000,000 BCE – 1,800,000 BCE — Olduvai Gorge Bed I and the Oldowan Lifeway

    Summary: A static snapshot of classic Oldowan occupation in the Tanzanian Rift at the type locality of Olduvai Gorge during Bed I deposition; small hominin groups — Homo habilis alongside Paranthropus boisei — occupy lake-margin sites (DK, FLK Zinj, FLK North) where they process scavenged carcasses with simple core-and-flake tools; the binding constraint is access to animal tissue in competition with a formidable carnivore guild; the system holds moderate margin through mobility and dispersed resources across the paleolake basin; the configuration is stable with no directional technological or demographic shift visible within the window.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Olduvai Gorge lies in the eastern branch of the East African Rift within the Ngorongoro volcanic highlands; the Bed I paleolake and associated wetlands create a mosaic of open water, marsh, gallery woodland, and grassland; transit follows lake margins and seasonal stream channels with low friction across the basin floor.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Warm tropical climate with pronounced wet-dry seasonality; lake levels fluctuate with monsoon variability; no sustained aridification or humidification trend within the window; interannual volatility moderate.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Volcanic activity in adjacent Ngorongoro highlands produces periodic tephra falls (Tuff IB, Tuff IC) that preserve archaeological horizons; no catastrophic eruption impacts hominin occupation; flood pulses along seasonal drainages create localized disturbance but exposure remains manageable.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Abundant lava cobbles, quartzite, and chert available in stream beds and outcrops provide knappable raw material; lake-margin ecotone concentrates herbivore biomass; resource endowment effectively unlimited relative to extraction rate.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Bone and lithic debris scattered across open landscapes; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited; no detectable environmental degradation.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Pathogen regime sparsely attested; probable endemic parasitic and bacterial loads consistent with equatorial wetland environments; mortality baseline inferred high from short adult lifespans in later hominin samples.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Diet combines plant foods, marrow, and scavenged meat; FLK Zinj cutmarked bones document defleshing and marrow extraction; nutritional adequacy sufficient for survival with seasonal stress during dry periods; physiological robustness moderate.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Moderate sexual dimorphism in Homo habilis suggests flexible mating; Paranthropus boisei shows greater dimorphism; reproductive biology transitional with increasing brain size extending infant dependency.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density extremely low — small groups (perhaps ten to twenty-five individuals) dispersed across the basin; birth-to-death ratio near replacement; turnover rapid; demographic configuration stable within the window.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Short adult lifespans limit elderly cohort; orphaned juveniles absorbed within small groups; dependency load light relative to productive capacity; load effectively cleared through attrition.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Immediate-return foraging with daily acquisition of plant foods, invertebrates, and scavenged fauna; provisioning floor close to metabolic minimum during dry-season troughs but generally met; lakeside location buffers seasonal scarcity.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle sole power source; fire use not securely attested at Olduvai Bed I; energy capture limited to direct biomass intake and kinetic work of knapping and butchery; per-capita work ceiling low.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: No division beyond age-sex differences; labor mobilized by proximity and imitative cueing; coercion minimal — cooperation maintained through co-presence.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Stone tool production yields choppers, discoids, polyhedrons, and flakes; output matched to immediate processing need; no durable surplus beyond carried toolkit.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No food storage technology; marrow-bearing bones provide short-term reserve; buffering capacity negligible; groups relocate in response to local depletion.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: No evidence of inter-group exchange; raw materials acquired within day-range foraging radius; logistics limited to individual transport.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of deferred exchange; reciprocity operates within immediate group without formalized obligation.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No coordinating authority; carcass sharing within group is informal, governed by proximity, aggression, and tolerated scrounging.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: No durable property; carried tools and physical capacity are sole assets; defense through retention and vigilance.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Bone fragments and flake debris accumulate at processing loci (forming the "living floor" palimpsests); dispersal across landscape with effectively unlimited sink capacity; no routing decision required.
