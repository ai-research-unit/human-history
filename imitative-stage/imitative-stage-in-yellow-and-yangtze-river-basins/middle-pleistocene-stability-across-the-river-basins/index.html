    400,000 BCE – 250,000 BCE — Middle Pleistocene Stability Across the River Basins

    Summary: Homo erectus populations occupy both the Yellow and Yangtze river basins in dispersed low-density groups, maintaining simple flake-tool technologies with minimal innovation across one hundred fifty millennia. Glacial-interglacial oscillations shift habitat boundaries but do not disrupt the basic adaptive pattern; regional populations develop subtle morphological variations while retaining a shared behavioral repertoire transmitted entirely through imitation. The binding constraint is demographic — populations remain too small and isolated for cumulative technological change; the system holds adequate margin against environmental fluctuation but no pressure drives intensification.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Yellow River basin comprises loess plateau, alluvial plain, and piedmont zones; the Yangtze basin offers subtropical valleys and karst uplands; mountain ranges impose friction between basins while river corridors facilitate internal movement.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Glacial-interglacial cycles drive monsoon intensity; interglacials bring warmer, wetter conditions favoring forest expansion, while glacials shift vegetation toward grassland and steppe; volatility is high but predictable on millennial scales.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Flooding along major rivers, loess erosion, and cold spells during glacials pose recurring hazards; exposure is moderate and mitigated by mobility; margin against individual events is adequate.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Quartz, quartzite, and chert are locally abundant; game and plant foods vary with latitude and climate phase; fuel wood available in forested zones; no evidence of resource depletion.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Organic and lithic waste disperses across open landscapes; population density is too low to stress any sink; absorptive capacity effectively unlimited.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic parasites and zoonotic exposure from prey animals impose background risk; no epidemic signatures; mortality driven primarily by trauma, predation, and environmental stress rather than disease.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Skeletal evidence from Hexian and other sites indicates robust builds and adequate nutrition; dental pathology suggests mixed diet with grit; vitality stable across the window.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Sexual dimorphism consistent with flexible mating systems; pair-bonding patterns unattested; reproductive biology unchanged from earlier Homo erectus.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density remains very low; groups are small and widely dispersed; renewal rates sufficient to maintain presence but not to drive expansion; demographic stasis prevails.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Dependent individuals absorbed within small-group capacity; no evidence of specialized care for severely disabled; dependency overhang minimal.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Hunting and gathering of regionally available fauna and flora sustain groups at safe distance from carrying-capacity edge; seasonal mobility buffers lean periods; provisioning adequate but not abundant.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle provides all motive power; fire use probable given Zhoukoudian precedent, extending caloric returns through cooking; conversion efficiency low but sufficient.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Task differentiation by age and sex; cooperative foraging and processing; no coerced labor; organization arises through observation and participation.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production limited to immediate needs; surplus beyond short-term consumption is minimal; no evidence of stockpiling or intensification.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No storage technology; buffering relies on body reserves, carcass preservation by climate, and mobility; reserves are shallow but adequate given low population pressure.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Raw material sourcing is local; no evidence of inter-group exchange networks; logistics confined to daily and seasonal foraging rounds.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of value storage; deferred reciprocity operates within bands but leaves no trace; credit and debt are immediate and interpersonal.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Meat sharing at procurement sites constitutes redistribution; no extractive authority; allocation proportional to participation.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Portable wealth negligible; no durable assets beyond tools; inequality functionally absent; asset defense not applicable.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Bone and lithic debris left at occupation sites; waste volume trivial; no routing decision required.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Trust operates within the band; mutual aid evident in cooperative hunting and food sharing; trust radius does not extend to strangers, who are rarely encountered.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Status follows age and skill; no material markers of rank; group identity is local, kin-based, and non-symbolic.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship governs residence and mating; inheritance limited to imitated behaviors; no property transfer across generations.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: The band is the sole organizational unit; coordination through proximity and observation; no nested associations.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Non-work time spent in rest and social interaction; no evidence of ornamentation or games; sociability is continuous and face-to-face.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Disputes resolved interpersonally or through group fission; no formal mediation; residual grievance dissipates through mobility.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: Authority is situational and non-coercive; leadership tied to demonstrated competence in hunting or foraging; power diffuse.

    Succession & Power Transfer: No institutionalized succession; leaders emerge and fade without formal transfer; continuity depends on individual capability.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: No narrative legitimation; deference based on observable competence rather than ideology.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administrative functions; coordination is immediate and observational; no records or formal processes.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: Participation universal within the band; mobilization for collective action is voluntary; dissent expressed through departure.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Inter-group contact rare due to low density; no territorial boundaries or diplomatic relations; external environment is open and uncontested.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Hunting weapons serve for personal defense; no specialized military capacity; force is individual.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Predator pressure from large carnivores is the primary threat; internal disorder minimal due to small group size; threat margin adequate.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information moves only through face-to-face demonstration and gesture; velocity limited to walking pace; reach confined to co-present individuals.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: No external storage medium; knowledge persists in living memory and embodied habit; loss of practitioners means loss of technique; persistence fragile.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Framing occurs through repeated demonstration; no censorship mechanism; norms transmitted by imitation of accepted practice.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention directed by proximity and movement; amplification through repeated demonstration; virality impossible without symbolic media.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Signal integrity high for direct observation; noise from imperfect imitation and memory decay; verification through demonstration; information volume very low.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Fairness intuitions likely present as affective responses to cheating; no codified morality; justice is immediate and emotional.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No formal law; behavioral regulation through social pressure and exclusion; codification absent.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: No third-party adjudication; disputes settled directly between parties or avoided; liability personal and immediate.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Autonomy constrained only by physical vulnerability and group norms; no formal rights; compliance burden negligible.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongs addressed immediately or not at all; no accumulating backlog; impunity depends on relative strength and group tolerance.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No formal inquiry; understanding implicit in successful action; discovery frontier is trial-and-error at the behavioral margin.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Flake-tool production follows conservative reduction sequences; fire-tending probable; technique transmitted through observation without articulated theory.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Skill acquired through prolonged observation and participation; knowledge stock small and unevenly distributed; technological conservatism reflects limited transmission bandwidth.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Techniques lost when practitioners die without successful transmission; error correction slow; obsolete methods persist; decay tied to demographic turnover.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No symbolic mythos recoverable; collective memory limited to living recall; identity immediate and situational.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture utilitarian — simple flake tools, possible hearths; no ornaments or non-functional artifacts; aesthetic expression not evidenced.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Mood unrecoverable; stable occupation patterns suggest routine persistence rather than crisis or innovation.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Without symbolic forms, exhaustion and decay do not apply; behavioral routines may ossify but leave no meaning-residue.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No cosmology recoverable; existential orientation, if any, is pre-symbolic and affective rather than articulated.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: No unambiguous ritual behavior; repetitive actions may be purely functional; sacred practice not attested.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: Religious institutions absent; doctrinal authority meaningless without doctrine; spiritual organization null.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Without articulated beliefs, diffusion and syncretism do not apply; spiritual content, if present, is local and ephemeral.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Disenchantment presupposes prior enchantment; in the absence of recoverable cosmology, this residue line is structurally null.
