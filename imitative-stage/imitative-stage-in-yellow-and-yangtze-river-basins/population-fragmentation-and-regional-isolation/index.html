    100,000 BCE – 70,000 BCE — Population Fragmentation and Regional Isolation

    Summary: A long-term trend spanning the descent from MIS 5 interglacial warmth into the glacial conditions of MIS 4 — cooling, aridification, and monsoon weakening progressively fragment archaic Homo sapiens populations across the Yellow and Yangtze basins into isolated refugia; connectivity between groups diminishes; regional morphological divergence accumulates; the archaeological record becomes sparse as population contracts; the binding constraint remains embodied knowledge in few heads, now made more fragile by reduced inter-group contact.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Yellow River loess plateau and Yangtze basin remain structurally unchanged, but expanding grassland and retreating forest increase friction across previously traversable terrain; mountain barriers become more formidable as refugia shrink.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Transition from MIS 5 interglacial warmth into MIS 4 glacial conditions brings progressive cooling, reduced monsoon precipitation, and increased aridity; climate volatility intensifies as the system shifts between states.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Drought frequency increases; dust-storm intensity rises on the loess plateau; flood hazard declines with reduced rainfall; cold stress becomes a binding constraint in northern refugia during glacial maxima.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Shrinking forest reduces plant diversity and medium-game availability; grassland expansion favors large grazers but requires different hunting strategies; raw material endowment unchanged but access more constrained.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Waste load from declining populations trivial; aridification reduces biological decomposition rates but volumes remain negligible; absorptive capacity unlimited relative to load.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Cooler, drier conditions suppress some tropical pathogens but cold stress and nutritional scarcity elevate mortality; small isolated populations limit epidemic potential; attrition tilts toward environmental rather than infectious causes.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Nutritional stress increases as preferred forest foods contract; populations shift toward higher reliance on hunted game and lower-quality plant resources; physiological vitality likely declines, though direct skeletal evidence sparse.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Pair-bonding and reproductive biology unchanged at the physiological level; effective reproductive success constrained by resource scarcity and reduced mate availability in isolated groups.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density declines across the window; bands contract into shrinking refugia in river valleys and cave-rich terrain; replacement rates may fall below unity in marginal zones, driving local extinctions.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Harsh conditions may reduce lifespan and limit dependent accumulation; alternately, loss of productive adults to starvation or cold elevates dependency burden on survivors; net effect uncertain but population contracting.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Provisioning floor drops as forest foods contract; subsistence increasingly precarious in northern zones during glacial peaks; southern refugia retain marginally better conditions but overall margin against starvation narrows.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Fire becomes more critical for warmth and cooking in colder conditions; human muscle remains sole motive power; no technological change in conversion efficiency documented.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Band-level cooperation unchanged; no shift toward coercion; labor pooling within kin groups remains the universal pattern.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production barely meets subsistence; no surplus accumulates; capacity contracts with resource base.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No durable storage; body fat reserves and landscape knowledge provide limited buffering; resilience declines as familiar landscapes transform.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Inter-band contact diminishes as populations fragment; mate exchange and raw-material transfer become rarer; logistics effectively collapse to within-band scale.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Deferred reciprocity persists within kin networks but inter-group obligations lapse as contact ceases; no external medium of exchange.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: No surplus to extract; communal sharing within band continues but pool shrinks with population.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Material equality persists; no wealth concentration; portable tool kits remain sole property; defense unnecessary.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Waste volumes decline with population; residue accumulates at cave sites at imperceptible rates; routing decisions absent.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Within-band trust remains high; inter-band trust erodes as contact diminishes; reciprocity radius contracts toward immediate kin.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Minimal stratification unchanged; group identity intensifies as isolation deepens; boundaries between regional populations harden through lack of contact.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship structures unchanged; inheritance limited to skills and tools passed within shrinking bands; lineage memory foreshortens as elders die without replacement.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: The band remains sole associational unit; no secondary structures; self-organization unchanged but smaller groups have less collective capacity.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Sociability persists but contracted to smaller circles; leisure constrained by increased subsistence effort; play among juveniles continues in refugial camps.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Grievance channels unchanged but smaller groups offer fewer exit options; fission less viable when neighboring bands are absent or distant; some grievance may accumulate unresolved.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: Situational leadership unchanged; authority remains diffuse; no centralizing pressure in contracting populations.

    Succession & Power Transfer: No formal succession; situational roles shift with need; transfer a non-issue.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: Legitimacy through demonstrated competence continues; no narrative justification required.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: No administrative apparatus; coordination through imitation and direct communication; institutional memory limited to living practitioners.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: All adults participate in band decisions; mobilization capacity declines with population; collective action limited to small-group scale.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Inter-group relations attenuate as contact diminishes; territorial boundaries less contested as populations contract into non-overlapping refugia; external relations effectively null in many areas.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: No specialized apparatus; interpersonal violence remains possible but inter-group conflict declines with reduced contact; weaponry unchanged.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Internal disorder potential rises slightly as exit options narrow; external threats shift toward environmental stress and predation; organized violence from other groups diminishes with isolation.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information channels contract with population; transmission remains strictly imitative — gesture, demonstration, guided practice — and slows as inter-group contact ceases.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: No external record; knowledge persists only in living memory; population decline and isolation accelerate knowledge loss as fewer practitioners carry fewer techniques.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: No linguistic discourse; imitative display conveys technique without propositional content; framing and censorship inapplicable.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention limited to shrinking social circle; amplification through repeated demonstration continues but audience contracts; inter-group diffusion of innovation effectively halts.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Copying fidelity depends on demonstrator skill and observer attention; isolation increases drift and divergence between regional populations; functional feedback still corrects gross error but regional variants accumulate.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Proto-moral intuitions around fairness and reciprocity unchanged; no codification; sense of justice rooted in face-to-face interaction.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No formal law; customary norms unchanged; regulatory evolution imperceptible.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: Informal adjudication by senior kin continues; enforcement through social sanction; liability assigned reputationally; no third-party apparatus.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: No articulated rights; protection from kin solidarity; compliance burden negligible.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Smaller groups with fewer exit options may see slightly more unresolved conflict; impunity limited by high interdependence; residue minimal but may rise slightly.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No formal inquiry; tacit understanding of environment remains procedural; no articulated theory.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Lithic techniques, fire management, and foraging strategies persist; methods transmitted by demonstration; regional divergence in technique possible as isolation prevents cross-fertilization.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Skill transmission through observation and practice continues; knowledge stock may contract as practitioners die without full transmission; distribution uneven as specialization intensifies in smaller groups.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Knowledge decay accelerates with population decline and isolation; techniques lost to individual death not relearned if no alternative source exists; intellectual impoverishment a real risk across the window.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No linguistic mythos; collective memory limited to living experience; identity rooted in shrinking territory and contracting kin network; regional distinctiveness deepens through isolation.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture remains expedient flake tools with regional stylistic drift; no decorative elaboration documented; divergence between populations accumulates without diffusion to homogenize.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Behavioral disposition shifts toward caution and conservatism as environment deteriorates; confidence erodes with declining resource base; anxiety may rise but leaves no direct trace.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: No symbolic forms to exhaust; meaning remains tied to functional practice; decay occurs only through skill loss, not symbolic hollowing.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No recoverable cosmology; existential orientation presumably practical and immediate; any proto-spiritual awareness archaeologically invisible.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: No documented ritual; mortuary treatment unknown for this window; sacred practice, if present, indistinguishable from routine.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: No institutions or doctrinal authority; concept inapplicable to pre-linguistic isolates.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: No spiritual system to diffuse; field null for this window.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: No prior enchantment to lose; existential residue undetectable; field null for this window.
