    600,000 BCE – 400,000 BCE — Zhoukoudian Locality 1 and Homo erectus pekinensis

    Summary: A limestone cave system near modern Beijing hosts repeated occupation by Homo erectus pekinensis across two hundred millennia, leaving the densest hominin fossil accumulation in East Asia. The cave's shelter and proximity to water and game sustain seasonal or multi-generational returns; thick ash-bearing deposits suggest fire use, though natural burning remains possible. Technological investment stays minimal — simple flake tools on local quartz — while imitative transmission alone preserves knowledge of fire-tending and carcass processing across cohorts. The binding constraint is the cave's fixed capacity relative to faunal availability; the system holds moderate margin against climatic oscillation so long as prey herds persist.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Zhoukoudian lies at the piedmont transition between the Taihang foothills and the North China Plain; the limestone karst provides natural shelter, while ridgelines channel movement toward nearby springs and open grassland.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Conditions oscillate across glacial-interglacial cycles; interglacials bring warm-temperate monsoon summers and cold dry winters, while glacials push the zone toward steppe; overall climate within the window is variable but not trending sharply.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Roof-fall and sediment slumping pose localized risks inside the cave; regional seismicity is low; drought and harsh winters present the primary external shocks, with moderate margin given faunal diversity.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Local quartz and vein quartz supply adequate raw material for flake tools; limestone and bone are abundant; fuel wood available within foraging radius; no evidence of resource depletion within the window.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Bone, ash, and lithic debris accumulate on the cave floor but dispersal occurs during periods of non-occupation; the open landscape absorbs organic waste without saturation; sink capacity remains effectively unlimited.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Endemic parasites and zoonotic exposure from butchered fauna impose background risk; no epidemic signatures recoverable; mortality baseline set primarily by trauma, predation, and climatic stress rather than disease.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Robust skeletal remains indicate adequate protein intake from large mammals; dental wear patterns suggest grit-laden plant foods; physiological vitality appears stable across the occupation sequence.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Sexual dimorphism visible in skeletal remains implies polygynous or flexible mating structures; pair-bonding duration is unattested; reproductive biology indistinguishable from broader Homo erectus pattern.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density is very low by later standards; multiple individuals across strata suggest repeated small-group occupation rather than continuous residence; renewal rate sufficient to maintain presence over millennia.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Juvenile and adult remains both present; no clear evidence of sustained care for severely disabled individuals; dependency load absorbed within small-group capacity; overhang minimal.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Hunting and scavenging of large mammals — deer, horse, rhinoceros — supplemented by plant foods; seasonal abundance cycles govern provisioning; the carrying-capacity edge is approached during harsh winters but not habitually breached.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human muscle provides all motive power; fire, if controlled, extends energy capture through cooking and warmth, improving caloric yield from meat; conversion efficiency low but adequate for small groups.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Task differentiation is minimal beyond age and sex; no evidence of coerced labor; cooperative hunting and carcass processing organized through observation and participation without formal hierarchy.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Production limited to immediate consumption; surplus beyond a few days' meat is unattested and likely minimal; no storage technology recoverable.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: No granaries or caches evident; buffering relies on body fat, carcass preservation by cold, and mobility to new hunting grounds; reserves are shallow.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Raw material sourcing is local; no evidence of exchange networks extending beyond the immediate foraging range; logistics are foot-based and limited to daily or seasonal rounds.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: No medium of value storage exists; deferred reciprocity may operate within the band but leaves no material trace; credit and debt are purely interpersonal and immediate.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Meat sharing at kill sites constitutes the only redistribution; no extractive authority; communal allocation proportional to participation and proximity.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Portable wealth is negligible; no durable assets beyond carried tools; inequality is functionally absent; defense of holdings not applicable.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Bone, ash, and flaking debris accumulate at occupation surfaces and are left in place; waste volume is trivial relative to cave volume; no routing decision required beyond abandonment.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Co-residence and cooperative hunting imply baseline trust within the band; mutual aid evident in shared processing; radius of trust likely does not extend beyond face-to-face associates.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Status differentiation, if any, follows age and demonstrated skill; no material markers of rank recoverable; group identity is local and kin-based.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Kinship structures are unattested but presumed to govern residence and mating; inheritance is limited to learned behaviors transmitted through imitation; no property transfer.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: The band itself is the sole organizational unit; no nested associations; coordination arises from habitual proximity and observational learning.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Non-subsistence time likely spent in rest, grooming, and informal social interaction; no material evidence of games or ornamentation; sociability is face-to-face and continuous.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Conflict resolution is immediate and interpersonal; fission — splitting the group — is the primary discharge mechanism; no formalized mediation; residual grievance dissipates through mobility.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: No centralized authority; leadership, if present, is situational and tied to hunting success or age; power is diffuse and non-coercive.

    Succession & Power Transfer: Succession is not institutionalized; situational leaders emerge and recede without formal transfer; continuity depends on individual longevity and competence.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: No recoverable narrative legitimating authority; deference based on demonstrated competence rather than ideology.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: Administrative functions do not exist; coordination is immediate and observational; no record-keeping or formal decision processes.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: Participation is universal within the band; mobilization for hunting is voluntary and cooperative; dissent expressed through non-compliance or departure.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Inter-group relations are unattested; contact with other bands likely rare given low population density; no territorial defense structures.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Hunting weapons double as personal defense; no specialized military capacity; force capacity is individual and distributed.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Predator pressure from hyenas and large cats poses the primary threat; internal disorder limited by small group size and immediate conflict resolution; threat margin adequate.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Information travels only as fast as individuals walk or gesture; transmission is face-to-face, gestural, and demonstrative; no acoustic signaling beyond calls; reach limited to co-present group members.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: No external record medium exists; all knowledge persists in living memory and embodied habit; loss of skilled individuals means loss of technique; persistence is fragile across generations.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: In the absence of language, framing is enacted through demonstrated emphasis and repetition; no censorship mechanism; norms transmitted through imitation of accepted practice.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Attention is directed by proximity and movement; amplification occurs through repeated demonstration by multiple individuals; virality is impossible without symbolic media.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Signal integrity is high for directly observed actions; noise arises from imperfect imitation and memory decay; verification is achieved through repeated demonstration; information volume is very low.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Baseline notions of fairness likely exist as emotional responses to cheating or free-riding; no codified morality; sense of justice is immediate and affective.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: No formal law exists; behavioral regulation proceeds through social pressure and exclusion; codification is absent.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: Third-party adjudication does not occur; disputes are settled between parties or by group avoidance; liability is personal and immediate; no formal enforcement machinery.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Individual autonomy is constrained only by physical vulnerability and group norms; no formal rights or protections; compliance burden negligible.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Wrongs are addressed immediately or not at all; impunity depends on relative strength and group tolerance; no accumulating backlog of unredressed grievance.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: No formal inquiry or explanatory paradigm exists; understanding of the world is implicit in successful action; the discovery frontier is the margin of trial-and-error behavior.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Flake tool production follows established reduction sequences transmitted through observation; fire-tending, if controlled, represents embodied technique without articulated theory.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Skill acquisition proceeds through prolonged observation and apprenticeship-style participation; knowledge stock is small and distributed unevenly by individual experience; literacy is meaningless.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Techniques are lost when skilled practitioners die without successful imitation; error correction is slow; obsolete methods persist until demonstrably ineffective; decay rate tied to demographic turnover.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: No symbolic mythos recoverable; collective memory extends only as far as living individuals' recall; identity is immediate and situational rather than narratively constructed.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Material culture is utilitarian — flake tools, possibly fire hearths; no ornaments, pigments, or non-functional artifacts; aesthetic expression is not evidenced.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Mood is unrecoverable; behavioral disposition inferred from stable occupation patterns suggests neither crisis nor exuberance but routine persistence.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Without symbolic forms, exhaustion and decay in the cultural sense do not apply; behavioral routines may ossify but leave no recoverable residue of meaning-loss.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: No cosmological system is recoverable; existential outlook, if any, is pre-symbolic and embedded in affective response to environment rather than articulated belief.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: No unambiguous ritual behavior evidenced; repetitive actions at hearths may be purely functional; sacred practice is not attested.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: Religious institutions do not exist; doctrinal authority is meaningless in the absence of doctrine; spiritual organization is null.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Without articulated belief systems, diffusion and syncretism do not apply; spiritual content, if present, is local and dies with individuals.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Disenchantment presupposes prior enchantment; in the absence of recoverable cosmology, this residue line is structurally null for the window.
