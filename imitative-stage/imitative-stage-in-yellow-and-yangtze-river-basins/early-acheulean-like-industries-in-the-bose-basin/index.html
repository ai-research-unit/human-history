    900,000 BCE – 600,000 BCE — Early Acheulean-like Industries in the Bose Basin

    Summary: A snapshot of a distinctive large cutting tool tradition in the Bose Basin of southern China, associated with Australasian tektites dated to approximately 0.8 Ma. The binding constraint is access to suitable raw material exposed by tektite-associated landscape disturbance. Populations hold adequate margin against subtropical hazards. The technological tradition is stable within the window, showing no directional change.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Bose Basin occupies a subtropical river valley in Guangxi Province; terrain consists of low hills and alluvial terraces with movement facilitated along watercourses and constrained by forested uplands.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Warm subtropical conditions with high humidity and year-round rainfall; monsoon intensification brings summer peaks; interannual variability moderate with occasional drought.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Seasonal flooding along the basin floor poses the principal hazard; tektite-fall event at 0.8 Ma caused widespread fires and vegetation disturbance but is a singular event within the window.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Abundant sandstone, quartzite, and silicified rock exposed by erosion and tektite-associated disturbance; animal biomass concentrated in forest-edge and riparian zones; plant foods available year-round.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Effectively unlimited absorptive capacity in a subtropical landscape with rapid organic decomposition; knapping debris disperses without accumulation.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Physiological parameters unrecoverable; presumed consistent with contemporary Homo erectus populations elsewhere in East Asia.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population density extremely low; artifact distribution suggests sporadic occupation rather than permanent settlement; replacement ratios stable near unity.
