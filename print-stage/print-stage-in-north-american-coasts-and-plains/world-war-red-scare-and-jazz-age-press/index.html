    1917 CE – 1930 CE — World War, Red Scare, and Jazz Age Press

    Summary: A trend spanning wartime mobilization, postwar repression, and consumer prosperity across the eastern United States and Mississippi basin; the binding constraint is the tension between expanded state capacity for propaganda and censorship and the decentralized commercial press; the system holds substantial economic margin after wartime expansion but faces ideological contestation and nascent broadcast competition; the principal direction of change is toward mass consumer culture, advertising-driven media consolidation, and the emergence of radio as print's successor.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: Continental infrastructure mature; transcontinental rail, automobile roads, and air mail routes compress spatial friction further; Great Lakes industrial corridor and Gulf ports handle war materiel; Mississippi system fully integrated into national logistics.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Temperate continental regime stable; drought conditions emerging in southern plains late 1920s presaging Dust Bowl; no major secular drift; agricultural margin adequate through the window.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Great Molasses Flood Boston 1919; Mississippi flood 1927 displaces hundreds of thousands; Florida hurricanes 1926 and 1928; localized exposure high but national-scale margin sufficient.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Petroleum production expanding in Texas and Oklahoma; coal still dominant but oil rising; natural gas beginning exploitation; iron ore abundant; timber managed more systematically; endowment vast.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Industrial pollution intensifying with war production; automobile exhaust emerging; urban sinks degraded; rivers and air quality compromised in industrial zones; absorptive capacity strained locally but dispersed continentally.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Influenza pandemic 1918-1919 kills over 600,000 in United States; mortality spikes then returns to declining trend; public health infrastructure strengthened in response; endemic disease burden lightening.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Wartime rationing minimal; postwar prosperity improves diet for most; processed food expanding; stature continuing to increase; pellagra persists among southern sharecroppers.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Fertility continuing decline among native-born; birth control movement growing despite legal obstacles; Margaret Sanger clinics opening; companionate marriage ideals ascendant; courtship patterns liberalizing.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Immigration restriction 1921 and 1924 sharply reduces inflow; Great Migration accelerating; urban density increasing especially in northern cities; population growth slowing but positive.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: War casualties create veteran disability cohort; influenza leaves orphans and widows; no systematic welfare; charitable organizations and mutual aid absorb partial load; dependency manageable.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Postwar prosperity lifts living standards; consumer goods accessible to broader population; agricultural sector depressed from mid-1920s; subsistence secure for urban employed; sharecroppers and tenant farmers marginal.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Electrification accelerating; oil powering automobiles and industry; per-capita energy consumption rising sharply; coal-fired electricity dominant; conversion efficiency improving.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Union membership peaks 1920 then declines under employer offensive; steel strike 1919 defeated; open shop campaign succeeds; coercion through injunctions and welfare capitalism; wage labor dominant.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Industrial output booming; automobile, electrical, and chemical industries lead; assembly line production mature; surplus substantial; overcapacity emerging by late 1920s.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: Federal Reserve system operational; banking more stable though speculation building; corporate reserves growing; margin adequate but speculative lending undermining systemic stability.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Automobile and truck transport expanding; retail chains spreading; mail order mature; advertising-driven consumer markets; logistics efficient.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Dollar dominant; gold standard maintained; consumer credit expanding dramatically; installment buying normalizing; stock market speculation rising; capital formation strong but increasingly speculative.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Income tax now primary federal revenue; wartime rates reduced in 1920s; Treasury running surpluses; extraction load moderate.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Concentration increasing through 1920s; capital gains untaxed until realization; corporate consolidation continuing; inequality widening; asset protection through trusts and holding companies.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Automobile waste new category; industrial effluent continues; municipal sanitation improving; landfills expanding; routing still largely externalized.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Trust contracted by war hysteria and Red Scare; ethnic suspicion elevated; nativism rising; mutual aid persists within communities but cross-group trust diminished; Klan revival in mid-1920s.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Jim Crow entrenched; Great Migration reconfigures northern race relations; ethnic hierarchies among whites; class consciousness suppressed; boundaries hardening along racial lines, shifting among European ethnics.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Nuclear family normative; divorce rates rising; women gaining legal rights; inheritance partible; generational tensions over cultural liberalization.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: Service clubs proliferating; Rotary, Kiwanis, Lions; women's clubs active; Klan as civic organization in some regions; veterans' organizations powerful; fabric mixed.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Mass entertainment boom; cinema, radio, spectator sports, dance halls; automobile enabling new sociability; commercialized leisure dominant; prohibition reshaping drinking culture.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Labor militancy suppressed; racial violence continues including 1919 riots; Klan provides discharge for white anxieties; reform channels constricted in conservative climate; substantial friction unmetabolized.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: Federal power expanded by war then partially retrenched; executive strong under Wilson, passive under Harding-Coolidge; Congress reasserts; regulatory state maintained; power distributed but concentrated in corporate-government nexus.

    Succession & Power Transfer: Electoral transfer smooth; Harding 1920, Coolidge 1923 succession after death, Hoover 1928; party system stable; succession routinized.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: Business civilization narrative dominant in 1920s; war-era nationalism fading; normalcy ideology prevails; legitimacy resting on prosperity; reform narrative marginalized.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: War administration built capacity; postwar scandals including Teapot Dome; Hoover at Commerce professionalizes; capacity maintained though corruption episodes punctuate.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: Women's suffrage achieved 1920; voter turnout declining; African Americans disenfranchised in South; third parties marginalized; participation narrowing despite formal expansion.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Global creditor; League rejected; Washington Naval Conference 1921-22; interventions in Caribbean and Central America; isolationist sentiment dominant; great power without entanglements.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Army demobilized postwar; Navy maintained at treaty limits; National Guard and state police active against labor; FBI emerging under Hoover; force capacity contracted but professionalized.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Red Scare repression; race riots 1919; labor violence suppressed; organized crime rising under prohibition; order strained but maintained through repressive measures.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Mass-circulation dailies and magazines peak; wire services dominant; radio broadcasting emerges 1920 and spreads rapidly; newsreels in cinemas; velocity accelerating with broadcast; reach expanding to include illiterate populations.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: Government records systematized; corporate archives growing; film and phonograph create new storage media; newspaper morgues extensive; persistence improving through institutional and technological investment.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Wartime censorship and propaganda through Committee on Public Information; Espionage and Sedition Acts suppress dissent; postwar relaxation but radical press targeted; Comstock enforcement continues; discourse window narrowing during war, partially reopening.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Radio creates simultaneous mass audience; tabloid journalism competes for attention; celebrity culture emerging; advertising sophisticated; amplification dynamics shifting toward broadcast.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Propaganda and advertising increase noise; professional journalism standards developing; Associated Press and wire services maintain factual baseline; tabloid sensationalism competes; verification challenged by volume.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Prohibition reflects moralistic strain; anti-radicalism frames justice claims; racial justice suppressed; labor rights contested; moral consensus narrowing around conformity.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: Eighteenth Amendment and Volstead Act; immigration restriction statutes; regulatory framework maintained; labor law tilts toward employers; law evolving toward restriction and conformity.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: Courts active against labor and radicals; Palmer raids 1919-1920; deportations; Sacco and Vanzetti case; prohibition unevenly enforced; throughput adequate but biased against dissent.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: First Amendment tested and partially vindicated in Holmes dissents; African American rights unprotected; labor rights minimal; compliance burden elevated by prohibition.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Lynching continues unpunished; Tulsa massacre 1921; corporate violations often unredressed; radical repression without recourse; gap between justice owed and delivered wide for African Americans and radicals.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: Behaviorism rising in psychology; scientific management mature; physics revolution reaching popular awareness; social sciences increasingly empirical; inquiry active though critical theory marginalized.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Mass production perfected; radio engineering advancing; electrical and chemical industries productive; aviation emerging; technique highly developed and diffusing.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: High school attendance rising dramatically; public education expanding; universities growing; literacy near-universal among native whites; skill distribution broadening; African American education severely underfunded in South.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Traditional knowledge yielding to scientific management and professional expertise; craft skills declining with mass production; decay rate low relative to rapid accumulation.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: American exceptionalism affirmed; war memory selective; Lost Generation critique marginal; immigrant Americanization enforced; African American identity crystallizing in Harlem Renaissance; foundational narratives contested beneath surface consensus.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Jazz and blues ascendant; Art Deco emerging; skyscraper as urban form; modernism in visual arts; Harlem Renaissance in literature; material culture increasingly standardized through mass production.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Postwar disillusionment among intellectuals; mass consumer optimism dominant; anxieties about modernity expressed through nativism and fundamentalism; mood bifurcated between confident consumerism and defensive traditionalism.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Victorian forms exhausted; prohibition as hollow enforcement of depleted moral consensus; modernist forms emerging; renewal and decay proceeding simultaneously across cultural domains.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: Protestant mainline liberalizing; fundamentalism organizing defensively; Scopes trial 1925 crystallizes divide; Catholic and Jewish communities institutionally strong; secularizing drift among urban educated; outlook pluralizing.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: Church attendance stable; revival tradition continues; immigrant devotional practices maintained; fundamentalist piety intensifying; practice differentiated by community.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: Fundamentalist-modernist controversy fracturing Protestant unity; Catholic hierarchy consolidated; Jewish denominations differentiating; authority contested within Protestantism, maintained elsewhere.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Pentecostalism spreading; African American churches growing in northern cities; secularism advancing among intellectuals; spiritual landscape diversifying.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Intellectuals disenchanted; Lost Generation expatriates; consumerism as secular substitute; fundamentalism as defensive re-enchantment; spiritual void widening among some, intensely filled for others; polarization rather than uniform drift.
