    1911 CE – 1919 CE — Early Republic and May Fourth Origins

    Summary: From the Qing collapse through the May Fourth Movement; the binding constraint is the absence of legitimate central authority as Yuan Shikai's dictatorship fails and warlord fragmentation follows, while the treaty port press and intellectual ferment in Peking University generate the New Culture Movement critique; direction is toward political disintegration and cultural radicalization culminating in the May Fourth protests.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The two river basins remain the demographic and economic core; railway construction advances but network remains incomplete; warlord territories impose new transit barriers; Shanghai's coastal position consolidates its role as the communications hub connecting foreign powers and interior.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Monsoon regime continues with normal interannual variation; 1917 Yangtze floods cause significant displacement and agricultural loss; no secular trend, but flood damage compounds political instability in affected provinces.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: Flood, drought, and famine continue as endemic hazards; Yellow River dike maintenance fragmented under warlord administration; 1917-1918 North China drought contributes to famine conditions; civil war damage adds anthropogenic hazard layer.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Coal and iron reserves unchanged; foreign concessions retain control of extraction; Japanese expand mining operations in Manchuria and Shandong under Twenty-One Demands pressure; endowment substantial but sovereignty over it compromised.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Treaty port sanitation continues improving; interior cities stagnant; industrial growth modest so new discharge limited; Shanghai waterways show degradation from textile and light industry effluent; overall sink capacity adequate for current load.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: Plague, cholera, typhoid, and tuberculosis remain endemic; 1918 influenza pandemic reaches China with significant urban mortality; no sustained public health improvement; baseline mortality high and stable.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Subsistence conditions unchanged for rural majority; famine episodic in war-affected and drought regions; urban workers in treaty ports marginally better supplied; physiological status stagnant with periodic acute deterioration.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Traditional marriage patterns persist; footbinding declining more rapidly under republican reform pressure; elite circles begin adopting companionate marriage ideals; reproductive biology unchanged.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population stable or slow growth around 400 million; high mortality and high fertility in rough balance; urban migration to treaty ports accelerating; density in agricultural core unchanged.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: Civil war and famine generate refugees and orphans; opium addiction widespread; lineage networks strained; dependency load accumulating faster than traditional absorption mechanisms can clear.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Peasant subsistence precarious; land concentration continuing; rent extraction heavy; household strategies include handicraft production and labor migration; margin against failure narrow.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Human and animal muscle remain primary for agriculture; coal-fired industry concentrated in treaty ports; railway expansion increases transport efficiency; overall energy capture per capita unchanged for rural population.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Wage labor expanding in treaty port industries; first labor organizing among railway and textile workers; contract labor persists in mines; rural labor embedded in tenancy relations; coercion level stable.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Light industry growing in Shanghai and Tianjin under wartime import substitution as European competition withdraws; textile and flour milling expand; agricultural production stagnant; modest industrial surplus accumulation.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: Granary system defunct; commercial grain storage speculative; foreign exchange reserves negligible; government reserves consumed by military expenditure; buffer capacity minimal.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Treaty port trade continues; World War I disrupts European imports enabling Chinese light industry; internal trade fragmented by warlord taxation and transit barriers; Shanghai financial dominance consolidates.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Silver standard continues; provincial currencies multiply under warlord fragmentation; modern Chinese banks expand; foreign banks dominate international transactions; inflation modest; credit increasingly tight outside treaty ports.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: Warlord armies extract heavily through irregular taxation and forced contributions; Beijing government revenue limited to customs and salt pledged to foreign debt; extraction chaotic and multiple.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: Treaty port merchants and compradors consolidate wealth; foreign capital protected; gentry landholding continues; asset defense requires foreign concession shelter or warlord patronage; concentration increasing.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Light industrial waste discharged into Shanghai waterways; nightsoil cycling continues in agriculture; volume increasing but still within sink capacity; routing decisions unchanged.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: Native-place associations and guilds continue mutual aid functions; trust radius contracts under warlord predation; new student associations form solidarity networks; urban-rural trust gap widening.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Old gentry status eroding; new intellectual class with foreign education gains prestige; warlord military elites rise; compradors and industrialists consolidate urban elite status; boundaries fluid in cities, rigid in countryside.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: Patrilineal system continues; reform discourse attacks arranged marriage and extended family as backward; urban educated begin nuclear family experiments; inheritance practices unchanged in law.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: Chambers of commerce active; student associations proliferate; professional associations form; labor unions embryonic; associational density increasing in urban centers.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Treaty port entertainment culture expands; cinema, dance halls, illustrated magazines reach urban middle class; teahouse and opera continue; student sociability through reading groups and demonstrations.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Student protests; worker strikes beginning; petitions to government ignored; May Fourth demonstrations open new protest repertoire; grievance channels inadequate, instability accumulating.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: Republic proclaimed but central authority collapses; Yuan Shikai's monarchical attempt fails 1916; warlord fragmentation follows; Beijing government nominal; real power fragmented among regional militarists; direction toward increased fragmentation.

    Succession & Power Transfer: No legitimate succession mechanism established; Yuan's death triggers competition; warlord power transfers by military contest; presidential succession contested and meaningless; transfer routinization absent.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: Republican legitimacy claimed but not consolidated; Sun Yat-sen's revolutionary program marginalized; warlords claim order and stability without ideological basis; May Fourth nationalism emerges as competing legitimation frame.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: Central government bureaucracy persists in reduced form; provincial administration under warlord control; corruption endemic; tax collection irregular; institutional capacity declining.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: Parliament exists but is bought and manipulated; student associations mobilize; press campaigns influence urban opinion; May Fourth protests demonstrate new mass mobilization capacity; participation expanding outside formal channels.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Twenty-One Demands 1915 expand Japanese position; China enters World War I 1917 hoping to recover Shandong; Paris Peace Conference 1919 awards German rights to Japan, triggering May Fourth; sovereignty continues to erode.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Armies multiply under warlord control; no unified command; weapons imported or captured; military technology conventional; force fragmented among competing regional commanders.

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Warlord civil wars endemic; banditry rampant in contested zones; urban order maintained in treaty ports by foreign police; revolutionary activity suppressed where warlords have capacity; disorder high and increasing.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Telegraph network intact; postal service functions; newspapers multiply; circulation of major papers reaches hundreds of thousands; student networks transmit rapidly; May Fourth demonstrates coordinated national mobilization capacity.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: Newspaper archives accumulate; government records fragmented; New Youth and other journals preserved; university libraries expand; vernacular publications broaden accessible record.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Treaty port press relatively free; warlord censorship erratic and evadable; New Culture Movement expands discourse boundaries; attack on Confucian tradition opens previously closed topics; discourse norms liberalizing.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Literate urban audience expanding with education; New Youth circulation modest but influence through student networks enormous; May Fourth demonstrations amplify student voice to national attention; attention concentrated on political crisis.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Competing information sources make verification difficult; rumor accompanies political instability; intellectual debates generate competing interpretations; noise load increasing but educated audience developing critical capacity.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Confucian moral order under direct attack from New Culture intellectuals; justice framed increasingly in terms of national self-determination and anti-imperialism; popular sense of injustice focused on foreign privilege and warlord predation.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: Republican legal codes incomplete; mixed jurisdiction continues in treaty ports; warlord territories operate under military law or personal rule; codification stalled by political fragmentation.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: Modern courts limited to major cities; magistrate and customary adjudication persist; warlord summary justice in military matters; enforcement dependent on local power configuration; liability assignment chaotic.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Provisional constitution provides theoretical rights; enforcement absent; foreign nationals retain extraterritorial privilege; compliance burden irregular depending on local warlord demands; protection unavailable for most.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Warlords and their followers enjoy near-complete impunity; foreign privilege entrenched; peasant and worker grievances accumulate without redress; May Fourth represents demand for national-level justice against foreign wrong; impunity gap widening.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: New Culture Movement attacks Confucian tradition; science and democracy (Mr. Science and Mr. Democracy) valorized; social Darwinism, liberalism, anarchism, and socialism compete; paradigm crisis and intellectual ferment intensifying.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Engineering and medical training expand through foreign institutions and returned students; technical method application limited by political instability; practical knowledge concentrated in treaty ports and returned-student networks.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Modern universities expand; Peking University under Cai Yuanpei becomes intellectual center; literacy still limited but expanding; vernacular movement aims to democratize knowledge access; skill distribution remains highly unequal.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: Classical learning rapidly devalued; traditional examination knowledge obsolete; Confucian transmission contracting; some traditional technical knowledge lost; decay accelerating under iconoclastic attack.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: May Fourth generates new founding narrative of national awakening against foreign oppression; iconoclastic rejection of traditional culture; competing visions of Chinese modernity struggle for definition; identity in crisis and reconstruction.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: New literature movement begins; vernacular fiction emerges; traditional opera continues; Western painting and architecture in treaty ports; illustrated magazines flourish; material culture bifurcating between modern and traditional.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Humiliation at Paris Peace Conference generates intense nationalist anger; youth radicalization; optimism about cultural transformation among intellectuals; anxiety about national survival; mood volatile, energized by crisis.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Traditional Confucian forms under direct iconoclastic attack; old rituals and symbols explicitly rejected by educated youth; cultural vacuum intensifies; new forms not yet consolidated; meaning decay accelerating.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: Scientific materialism advanced by New Culture intellectuals; traditional cosmology dismissed as superstition; survival-of-the-fittest framing continues; existential outlook increasingly secular and nationalist among educated; peasant cosmology unchanged.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: Traditional rituals continue in villages and among urban poor; educated youth explicitly reject ancestor worship and temple practice; Christian missions continue expansion; sacred practice bifurcating by class and education.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: Buddhist and Daoist institutions weakened; Confucian orthodoxy intellectually defunct; Christian institutions protected by treaty; no dominant religious authority; institutional fragmentation across traditions.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Christianity continues spreading; secular nationalism functions as quasi-religion for educated youth; Marxism begins diffusing as comprehensive worldview; syncretic folk religion persists in countryside; dominant cosmology fracturing.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: Educated stratum explicitly disenchanted; New Culture iconoclasm celebrates rejection of sacred; spiritual void filled by nationalist passion and revolutionary commitment; disenchantment accelerating and celebrated as liberation from superstition.
