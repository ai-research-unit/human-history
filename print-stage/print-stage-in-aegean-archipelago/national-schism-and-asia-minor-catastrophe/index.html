    1913 CE – 1922 CE — National Schism and Asia Minor Catastrophe

    Summary: Greece enters its most convulsive decade: the National Schism (Ethnikos Dichasmos) splits the kingdom between Venizelist republicans and royalist conservatives over World War I alignment, producing parallel governments and purges; postwar Greek forces overextend into Anatolia under the Treaty of Sevres, only to collapse before Kemalist armies in 1922, ending with the destruction of Smyrna and the flight of over a million Orthodox refugees — the binding constraint is political fragmentation preventing coherent war strategy, and the system exits shattered, its irredentist project terminated.

    P01. Physical Environment

    Geography & Spatial Friction: The Greek state now spans peninsula, Aegean islands, Crete, and newly acquired Thessaloniki-Macedonia; the Anatolian campaign extends Greek forces across the Aegean into roadless Anatolian interior, logistically overextending a small power.

    Climate Regime & Volatility: Mediterranean climate stable; Anatolian plateau interior harsher in winter; seasonal campaigning constraints shape military timetables.

    Hazard Regime & Exposure: No major natural disasters; principal hazards are wartime fires and urban destruction, culminating in the Smyrna conflagration of September 1922.

    Resource & Energy Endowment: Greece lacks coal and iron; dependent on British coal imports; Anatolian campaign strains already thin resource base; petroleum negligible.

    Waste Absorption & Sink Capacity: Urban concentration in Athens and Piraeus increases sanitary strain; Thessaloniki absorbs military traffic; Smyrna's destruction leaves environmental and humanitarian wreckage on Turkish soil.

    P02. Biological Environment

    Pathogen Load & Mortality Baseline: War mortality compounds endemic disease; typhus, malaria, and dysentery afflict troops and refugees; Spanish influenza (1918-1919) adds excess deaths.

    Nutrition & Physiological Vitality: Wartime rationing, blockades, and displacement degrade nutrition; soldiers on extended Anatolian campaign suffer supply shortages; refugee columns in 1922 arrive malnourished.

    Reproductive Biology & Pairing Dynamics: Prolonged mobilization separates families; wartime marriages delayed; refugee trauma disrupts household formation.

    Demographic Renewal & Density: Population doubled by Balkan Wars gains; war casualties and refugee flight alter composition; 1922 exodus removes Greek populations from Asia Minor permanently.

    Attrition & Dependency Overhang: War widows, orphans, and wounded accumulate; 1922 catastrophe generates massive dependent refugee population overwhelming absorptive capacity.

    P03. Economic Environment

    Subsistence & Provisioning Floor: Agricultural output disrupted by mobilization; Macedonia's grain helps but distribution uneven; refugee influx in 1922 overwhelms provisioning.

    Energy Capture & Conversion: Steam and animal power dominate; coal imports vulnerable to wartime disruption; army logistics depend on limited rail and animal transport.

    Labor Organization & Coercion: Mass conscription removes male labor from farms and workshops; women and elderly substitute; demobilization after 1922 releases embittered veterans.

    Production & Surplus Capacity: Industrial base remains small; wartime demand strains capacity; postwar contraction as military orders cease and refugees arrive.

    Storage, Reserves & Buffering: State reserves exhausted by decade of warfare; treasury dependent on Allied subsidies during war, on loans after; 1922 collapse finds reserves depleted.

    Exchange, Trade & Logistics: Allied blockade during schism period disrupts trade; postwar commerce resumes but refugee crisis absorbs capital; Smyrna's loss removes major commercial hub.

    Value Storage, Credit & Deferred Exchange: Drachma under pressure; inflation during war years; postwar instability continues; foreign debt accumulates.

    Surplus Extraction & Redistribution: State extracts through taxation and conscription; war expenditure absorbs all surplus; postwar reconstruction demands exceed revenue.

    Wealth Distribution & Asset Defense: War profiteers coexist with impoverished veterans; 1922 refugees arrive destitute, widening inequality; land reform pressures rise.

    Byproducts & Waste Routing: Military waste, abandoned equipment, and urban rubble accumulate; sanitary infrastructure strained; Smyrna's ruins left to Turkish administration.

    P04. Social Environment

    Social Trust & Mutual Aid: National Schism poisons social trust; families divide along Venizelist-royalist lines; purges and counter-purges create lasting vendettas.

    Stratification, Status & Group Identity: Pre-existing class divisions overlay new political cleavage; military officers gain prominence; 1922 refugees form distinct social stratum.

    Kinship, Family & Inheritance: War deaths and refugee displacement disrupt kinship networks; Asia Minor families arrive severed from ancestral property and kin left behind.

    Civic Association & Self-Organization: Political clubs, veterans' associations, and refugee committees proliferate; civil society fragmented along schism lines.

    Leisure, Play & Sociability: Public festivities reflect political division; coffeehouses become factional meeting points; refugee arrival brings Anatolian musical traditions.

    Grievance Channels & Social Discharge: Political violence, street clashes, and assassination (King George I, 1913; politicians later) indicate grievance exceeding discharge capacity; schism wounds unhealed.

    P05. Political Environment

    Power Structure & Authority: Constitutional monarchy in crisis; King Constantine and Prime Minister Venizelos represent opposed visions; parallel governments 1916-1917; Constantine exiled twice (1917, 1922).

    Succession & Power Transfer: Royal succession contested; Constantine forced out, Alexander dies 1920, Constantine returns, abdicates 1922; Venizelos defeated 1920, returns after catastrophe; transfer mechanism broken.

    Legitimacy & Political Narrative: Venizelists claim liberal reform and Allied victory; royalists claim national unity and neutrality; both narratives collapse in 1922 disaster, discrediting leadership.

    Administrative Capacity & Institutional Integrity: Bureaucracy politicized by purges; military command divided; intelligence and logistics suffer from schism-era disruptions.

    Political Participation & Mobilization: Expanded electorate; intense partisan mobilization through press and rallies; 1920 plebiscite returns Constantine; military intervenes repeatedly.

    Geopolitical Position & External Relations: Greece enters World War I late (1917); gains at Sevres (1920) reversed at Lausanne (1923); Allied support conditional and withdrawn when Constantine returns; isolated during final Anatolian collapse.

    Coercive Apparatus & Means of Force: Army expanded for Balkan Wars; further mobilization for Anatolia; equipment French-supplied; command fractured by schism; 1922 defeat triggers revolutionary committee and "Trial of the Six."

    Internal Order & Threat Environment: Political assassination, military coups, and street violence mark the period; 1922 military revolt executes scapegoat politicians; public order precarious.

    P06. Informational Environment

    Channels & Transmission Velocity: Telegraph and postal service operational; newspapers reach Athens same day; Anatolian front news delayed; censorship during war.

    Record, Storage & Persistence: Government archives maintained despite schism disruptions; newspapers document controversy; Asia Minor records lost in 1922 destruction.

    Framing, Censorship & Discourse Norms: Press intensely partisan; Venizelist and royalist papers attack opponents; wartime censorship imposed and lifted; public discourse poisoned by schism rhetoric.

    Reception, Attention & Amplification: Athenian public closely follows political drama; provincial and diaspora press relay positions; 1922 disaster dominates attention, crowding out nuance.

    Signal Integrity & Noise Load: Partisan distortion high; rumor and propaganda obscure military realities; accurate information on Anatolian campaign scarce until collapse undeniable.

    P07. Judicial Environment

    Moral Foundations & Sense of Justice: Public sense of justice polarized; each faction views the other as traitors; 1922 trials satisfy vengeance rather than legal process.

    Law, Codification & Regulatory Evolution: Legal framework nominally stable; emergency decrees during war; martial law periods; constitutional provisions suspended under crisis.

    Adjudication, Enforcement & Liability: Courts function but politicized; extraordinary military tribunals after 1922 execute six royalist leaders (Trial of the Six) without adequate process.

    Rights, Protections & Compliance Burden: Civil liberties suspended during emergencies; press freedom curtailed by both sides; political prisoners held by each regime.

    Impunity & Unredressed Wrong: Schism-era purges and post-catastrophe executions create unredressed grievances; families of executed leaders harbor resentment for decades; justice perceived as victor's revenge.

    P08. Intellectual Environment

    Inquiry, Theory & Paradigm: Academic institutions continue; University of Athens produces scholars; historiography intensely nationalist; science secondary to national crisis.

    Applied Knowledge & Technical Method: Military technology imported; medical and engineering training continues; no major innovations; applied knowledge focused on war needs.

    Education, Knowledge Stock & Skill Distribution: Education system expanding but disrupted by mobilization; literacy rising; katharevousa remains official language; demotic movement continues.

    Obsolescence & Knowledge Decay: 1922 catastrophe destroys Greek institutional presence in Asia Minor; Smyrna's schools, libraries, and learned societies lost; knowledge stock contracts.

    P09. Cultural Environment

    Mythos, Memory & Foundational Identity: Megali Idea reaches apogee and collapses; the 1922 catastrophe becomes foundational trauma; "Asia Minor Hellenism" memorialized as lost paradise.

    Artistic Expression & Material Culture: Literature and poetry engage war and schism; folk songs lament defeat; material culture disrupted by displacement; refugee objects become relics.

    Collective Mood & Behavioral Disposition: Public mood swings from war enthusiasm (1912-1913) to schism bitterness to catastrophe despair; 1922 trauma shapes collective psychology for generations.

    Form Exhaustion & Meaning Decay: Irredentist rhetoric exhausted by defeat; patriotic symbols tainted by failure; new meanings sought in refugee experience and republican experiment.

    P10. Spiritual Environment

    Cosmology & Existential Outlook: Orthodox Christianity remains framework; catastrophe interpreted through religious lens as divine punishment or test; fatalism deepens.

    Ritual, Devotion & Sacred Practice: Church services continue; refugee populations bring Anatolian liturgical traditions; saints' days observed amid displacement.

    Religious Institutions & Doctrinal Authority: Church of Greece aligned with state; Ecumenical Patriarchate in Constantinople survives but weakened; clergy participate in national mobilization.

    Spiritual Diffusion, Syncretism & Displacement: Population exchange will remove remaining Muslims from Greece and Christians from Turkey; window ends with homogenization in progress; Anatolian Orthodox traditions transplanted.

    Disenchantment & Sacred Discharge: The catastrophe challenges providential national narratives; some disillusionment with institutional religion; spiritual needs channeled into refugee solidarity and mourning rituals.
